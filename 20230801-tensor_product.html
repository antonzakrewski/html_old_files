tensor product

1. Definition

Let \(R\) be a commutative (?) ring, \(M,N\) \(R\)-modules. Then a tensor product \(M _R N\) is a \(R\)-module \(T\) with a homomorphism \(g: M \times N \rightarrow T\) such that for any module \(P\) and homomorphism \(f: M \times N \rightarrow P\) there exists a unique homomorphism \(\tilde{f}: T \rightarrow P\) such that diagramm commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M \times N \arrow[]{rd}{g} \arrow[]{r}{g} & T \arrow[]{d}{\tilde{f}} \\ & P \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
