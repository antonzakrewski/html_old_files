zariski topology as compact space

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) zariski spectrum equipped with the Zariski topology. Then \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) is compact.

2. Proof

compact space and intersection of closed sets Suppose there exists a family of closed sets \(A_i\) such that

\begin{align*} \bigcap A_i = \emptyset \end{align*}

Then there exist ideals \(\mathfrak{a}_i\) such that \(V(\mathfrak{a}_i) = A_i\) (see: Vanishing set and Ideal) Furthermore,

\begin{align*} \bigcap V(\mathfrak{a}_i) =& V(\sum \mathfrak{a}_i) \\ =& \emptyset \end{align*}

see: vanishing sets of a union of ringelements Then we conclude

\begin{align*} \sum \mathfrak{a}_i = R \end{align*}

(see: Vanishing set as empty set and Ring) Hence there exists a linear combination

\begin{align*} 1 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} r_i a_i \end{align*}

Therefore by the statement about a unit and trivial ideal, it follows that

\begin{align*} V(\sum_{i=1}^{n} \mathfrak{a}_i) =& V(R) \\ =& \emptyset \end{align*}

so \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) is compact.
