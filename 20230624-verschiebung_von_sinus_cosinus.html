Verschiebung von Sinus / Kosinus

1. Satz

Sei Sinus bzw. Kosinus gegeben.

\(y\) \(\sin(x + \pi y)\) \(\cos(x + \pi y)\)
\(0.5\) \(\cos(x)\) \(-\sin(x)\)
\(1\) \(-\sin(x)\) \(-\cos(x)\)
\(1.5\) \(-\cos(x)\) \(\sin(x)\)
\(2\) \(\sin(x)\) \(\cos(x)\)

2. Beweis

folgt aus den Additionstheoremen, es reicht \(\sin(x + \pi y)\) zu zeigen:

2.1. \(0.5\)

\begin{align*} \sin(x + 0.5 \pi) =& \sin(x) \cdot \cos(0.5 \pi) + \cos(x) \sin(0.5 \pi) \\ =& \sin(x) \cdot 0 + \cos(x) \cdot 1 \\ =& \cos(x) \end{align*}

2.2. \(1.0\)

\begin{align*} \sin(x + 1 \pi) =& \sin(x) \cdot \cos(1 \cdot \pi) + \cos(x) \sin(1 \cdot \pi) \\ =& \sin(x) \cdot (-1) + \cos(x) \cdot 0 \\ =& -\sin(x) \end{align*}

2.3. \(1.5\)

\begin{align*} \sin(x + 1.5 \pi) =& \sin(x) \cdot \cos(1.5 \pi) + \cos(x)\cdot \sin(1.5 \pi) \\ =& \sin(x) \cdot 0 + \cos(x) \cdot -1 \\ =& -\cos(x) \end{align*}

2.4. \(2.0\)

\begin{align*} \sin(x + 2 \pi) =& \sin(x) \cdot \cos(2 \cdot \pi) + \cos(x) \sin(2 \cdot \pi) \\ =& \sin(x) \cdot 1 + \cos(x) \cdot 0 \\ =& \sin(x) \end{align*}
