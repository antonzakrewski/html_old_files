reduced ring and square

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is a reduced ring
  2. for \(a \in A \setminus \{0\}\) it holds \(a^2 \neq 0\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contraposition Assume there exists an \(b \in A\) such that \(b^n = 0\) for \(n \geq 2\) Let \(n\) be minimal and \(m \coloneqq \ceil(\frac{n}{2})\). Then \(b^{n+1} = b^n = b^m \cdot b^m\) with \(b^m \neq 0\) by assumption of minimality and therefore \((b^m)^2\) is a contradiction
