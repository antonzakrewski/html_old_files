integral domain and cancellable ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring.

TFAE:

  1. \(R\) is an Integral domain
  2. the multiplicative semigroup \((R, \cdot)\) is cancellable for \(a \neq 0\), i.e. for \(a,b,c \in R\) it holds, that
\begin{align*} a \cdot b = a \cdot c \Rightarrow b = c \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose \(a,b,c \in R\) with \(a \neq 0\), then

\begin{align*} a \cdot b =& a \cdot c && \vert - a \cdot c\\ a \cdot (b - c) =& 0 \end{align*}

since \(a \neq 0\), we conclude, that \(b - c = 0\) or equivalently \(b = c\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

suppose

\begin{align*} a \cdot b = 0 \end{align*}

with w.l.o.g. \(a \neq 0\). Then

\begin{align*} a \cdot (b - 0) =& 0 && \vert + a \cdot 0 \\ a \cdot b =& a \cdot 0 && \vert \text{ cancelling} \\ b = 0 \end{align*}
