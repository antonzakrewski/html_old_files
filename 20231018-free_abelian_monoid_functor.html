free commutative monoid functor

1. Definition / Proposition

The free commutative monoid functor \(\mathrm{freeMon}\) is defined

\begin{align*} \mathrm{freeMon}: \mathrm{Set} \rightarrow& \mathrm{Mon} \\ X \mapsto& \mathrm{FreeCMon}(X) \\ (f: X \rightarrow Y) \mapsto& (f': \mathrm{FreeCMon}(X) \rightarrow \mathrm{FreeCMon}(Y)) \end{align*}

where \(f'\) is the monoid-homomorphism induced by

\begin{align*} f'(x) = f(x) \end{align*}
