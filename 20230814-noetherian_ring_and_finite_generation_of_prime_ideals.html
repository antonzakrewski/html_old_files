noetherian ring and finite generation of prime ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring. TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is a noetherian ring
  2. each prime ideal \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) is finitely generated

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(S \coloneqq \{\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A \vert \mathfrak{a} \text{ not finitely generated }\}\). Then \(A\) is noetherian if and only if \(S = \emptyset\). Assume that \(A\) is nonempty, then there exists a maximal element \(\mathfrak{m}\) (see: maximal element for the set of non finitely generated ideals) Since \(\mathfrak{m}\) is not prime, there exist \(a,b \in A \ \mathfrak{m}\) such that \(a \cdot b \in \mathfrak{m}\). By assumption of maximality, \((\mathfrak{m}, a), (\mathfrak{m},b)\) are prime and hence finitely generated. Furthermore

\begin{align*} (\mathfrak{m},a) \cdot (\mathfrak{m},b) =& (\mathfrak{m} + (a)) \cdot (\mathfrak{m} + (b)) \end{align*}

(cf. generated ideal and sum / generated ideal of a union and sum)
