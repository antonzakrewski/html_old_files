chain morphism

1. Definition

Let

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... \arrow[]{r}{} & A_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} & A_{n} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n}} & A_{n-1} & ... \\ ... \arrow[]{r}{} & B_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}}' & B_{n} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n}'} & B_{n-1} & ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be chain complexes A chain morphism is a collection of morphisms \(f_n: A_{n} \rightarrow B_n\) such that following diagram commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} ... \arrow[]{r}{} & A_{n+1} \arrow[]{d}{f_{n+1}} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}} & A_{n} \arrow[]{d}{f_n} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n}} & A_{n-1} \arrow[]{d}{f_{n-1}} & ... \\ ... \arrow[]{r}{} & B_{n+1} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n+1}}' & B_{n} \arrow[]{r}{d_{n}'} & B_{n-1} & ... \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
