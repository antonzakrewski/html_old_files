Noetherian domain with infinite maximal ideals and prime ideals as maximal ideals as Jacobson Ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian Integral domain such that each nonzero prime ideal is maximal and that $ | \mathrm{Spec}\mathrm{max}(A) | = ∞$ Then \(A\) is a Jacobson ring

2. Proof

Let \(\mathfrak{p}\) be a prime ideal, w.l.o.g. not maximal. Then \(\mathfrak{p} = \{0\}\), so it suffices to show

\begin{align*} \mathcal{J}_{A} = 0 \end{align*}

Suppose \(x \in A \setminus A^{\times}\) with $x ≠ 0a and the epimorphism

\begin{align*} \pi_{(x)}: A \rightarrow A/(x) \end{align*}

Then there exists an bijection between ideals in $ \overline{\mathfrak{b}} \subseteq A/(x)$ and in \(\mathfrak{b} \subseteq A\) with \((x) \in \mathfrak{b}\) see: ideal of a quotient ring and ideal containing the quotient

Suppose \(\mathfrak{q} \subseteq \mathfrak{q}'\) are prime ideals in \(A/(x)\), then the preimages are also prime ideals (see: Preimage of a prime ideal as prime ideal). By assumption \(\pi_{(x)}^{-1}[\mathfrak{q}], \pi_{(x)}^{-1}[\mathfrak{q}']\) are maximal ideals with \(\pi_{(x)}^{-1}[\mathfrak{q}] \subseteq \pi_{(x)}^{-1}[\mathfrak{q}']\), hence \(\mathfrak{q} = \mathfrak{q}'\) Therefore, all prime ideals in \(A/(x)\) are minimal prime ideals. Since a quotient ring of a noetherian ring is a noetherian ring and there exist only finitely many minimal prime ideals in a noetherian ring, there exist only finitely many prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}\) with \(x \in \mathfrak{p}\).

Furthermore because there exist infinitely many prime ideals, there exists a prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}_x\) with \(x \not\in \mathfrak{p}\)
