least common denominator

1. Definition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(a, b \in R \setminus \{0\}\). A least common multiple of \(a,b\) - \(\mathrm{lcm}(a,b)\) if unique - is an element \(r \in R\), such that:

  • \(a \mid r\)
  • \(b \mid r\)
  • \(\forall r' \in R: a \mid r' \land b \mid r' \Rightarrow r \mid r'\)

Often it is useful to define \(\mathrm{lcm}(a,0) := 0\)
