radical ideal and affine subvariety for an algebraically closed field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) an ideal. Then

\begin{align*} \mathcal{I}(V(\mathfrak{a})) = \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

see: vanishing ideal, affine subvariety

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. let \(\mathfrak{a}\) be a strict ideal, otherwise \(\mathcal{I}(V(K[X_1,...,X_n])) = \mathcal{I}(\emptyset) = K[X_1,...,X_n]\) By the Lemma about finitely generated algebra over a field as jacobson rings we conclude, that \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) is a Jacobson ring. Hence by the characterization of a radical of an ideal as intersection of prime ideals we get

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(K[X_1,...,X_n]) \vert \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{p}} \end{align*}

and since \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) is jacobson, it follows, that

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(K[X_1,...,X_n]) \vert \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}} \end{align*}

Let \(\mathfrak{m}\) be a maximal ideal with \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\). Then because of the correspondence of points and maximal ideal over an algebraically closed field, there exists an \(a \in \mathbb{A}_K^n\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m} = \mathfrak{m}_a \end{align*}
