Nk is dense in zariski topology

1. Proposition

\(\mathbb{N}^k\) is dense in the zariski topology on the affine space \(\mathbb{C}^k\)

2. Proof

Abschluss als kleinste abgeschlossene Menge

Let \(A \subseteq \mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{C}}^k\) be closed. Then \(A\) is by definition the zero locus of some polynomials \(f_1,...,f_n\). Applying vanishing set, generated ideal and intersection results in \(A = Z(f_1,...,f_n) = \bigcap_{i=1}^n Z(f_i)\) Furthermore, \(f_i[\mathbb{N}^k] = 0\), hence \(\mathbb{N}^k \subseteq Z(f_i)\)

since \(\mathrm{deg}(f_i) < \infty\), we conclude, that \(f_i = 0\) (cf. Zeros of a polynomial in the affine space and maximal degree of an variable)
