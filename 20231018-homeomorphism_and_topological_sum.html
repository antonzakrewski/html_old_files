homeomorphism and topological sum

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, and \(O_i \subseteq X\) subsets such that \(X = \coprod O_i\) in category set.

TFAE:

  1. each \(O_i\) is open
  2. the canonical inclusion
\begin{align*} \iota: \coprod O_i \rightarrow X \end{align*}

is a homeomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

The map is bijective.

2.1.1. continuous

<universal property ?>

2.1.2. open

Let \(O \subseteq \coprod O_i\) be open. Then by construction,

\begin{align*} O = \bigcup_{i \in I} (O_i \cap O) \end{align*}

Thus \(O\) is also open in \(O \subseteq X\) as \(O_i \cap O\) is open and thus \(O\) is open as union of open sets

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

follows from Homeomorphismus als stetig abgeschlossene/offene Bijektion. By Construction, \(O_i \subseteq \coprod O_i\) is open, hence \(\iota[O_i] \subesetq X\) is also open
