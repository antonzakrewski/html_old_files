contraction of a prime ideal

1. Proposition

let \(A,B\) commutative rings, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) a homomorphism and \(\mathfrak{b} \subseteq B\) a prime ideal. Then \(\varphi^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]\) is also prime

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

preimage of an ideal as ideal

2.2. prime condition

Because \(\mathfrak{b}\) is prime, we deduce, that \(B/\mathfrak{b}\) is an Integral domain (see: Primideal in kommutativen Ringen und Faktorring als Integrit√§tsbereich) Since the canonical homomorphism

\begin{align*} \widehat{f}: A/\varphi^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}] \rightarrowtail B/\mathfrak{b} \end{align*}

is a monomorphism (see: injective homomorphism and preimage of an ideal), \(A/\varphi^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]\) is also an integral domain and therefore \(\varphi^{-1}[\mathfrak{a}]\) prime

2.3. strict ideal

Note, that by definition of a ring-homomorphism, we get \(\varphi(1_A) = 1_B\). Since \(\mathfrak{b} \subseteq B\) is prime and hence strict, we conclude that \(1_B \not\in \mathfrak{b}\) and thus \(1_A \not\in \varphi^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]\)
