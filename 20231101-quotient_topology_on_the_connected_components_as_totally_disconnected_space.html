connected components with the quotient topology as totally disconnected space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(\pi(X)\) the set of connected components with the quotient topology for the projection \(p: X \rightarrow \pi(X)\) then \(\pi(X)\) is totally disconnected

2. Proof

Suppose \(A \subseteq \pi(X)\) is a connected component. Then \(A\) is closed (cf. Zusammenhangskomponente als abgeschlossene Menge) and by continuouity, \(\pi^{-1}[A]\) is also closed.

Suppose \(\vert A \vert \geq 2\), then by assumption, \(\pi^{-1}[A]\) is not connected, hence there exists closed sets \(A_1,A_2\) such that

  1. \(\pi^{-1}[A] \subseteq A_i \cup A_2\)
  2. \(A_1 \cap A_2 \cap \pi^{-1}[A] = \emptyset\)

w.l.o.g. by taking the intersection of closed sets we may assume, that \(A_1 \subseteq \pi^{-1}[A], A_2 \subseteq \pi^{-1}[A]\)

Furthermore, since \(\pi^{-1}[A]\) is saturated and by construction

\begin{align*} \pi[A_1] \cap \pi[A_2] = \emptyset \end{align*}

it follows, that \(\pi[A_1], \pi[A_2] \subseteq A\) are closed and disjoint. Furthermore, by surjectivity of \(\pi\), \(\pi[A_1], \pi[A_2]\) is a closed covering of \(A\) Therefore, \(A\) is not connected,

contradiction
