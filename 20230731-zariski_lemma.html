Zariski Lemma

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(L/K\) a field extension such that \(L\) is a finitely generated $K$-Algebra. Then \(L/K\) is a finite field extension

2. Proof

By assumption, there exist \(x_1,...,x_n \in L\) such that \(K[x_1,...,x_n] = L\) Let \(r \in \mathbb{N}\) be the largest number, such that - after reordering - \(x_1,...,x_r\) are algebraically independent. Then for the image \(K[x_1,...,x_r]\) it holds

\begin{align*} K[x_1,...,x_r] \simeq K[X_1,...,X_r] \end{align*}

Let \(E \coloneqq K(x_1,...,X_r)\) be the function field, then the inclusion factors

\begin{align*} K \rightarrowtail E \rightarrow L \end{align*}

By construction, \(L/E\) is finitely generated by \(x_{r+1},..,x_n\) and each of those generators is algebraic over \(E\). Hence \(L/E\) is a finitely generated algebraic field extension and thus a finite field extension

Let \(y_1 = 1\) and \(y_1,y_2,...,y_d\) be a basis of \(L\) as an \(E\)-vector space (see: Existenz einer Erweiterung zu einer Basis) Suppose \(\pi: L \rightarrow E\) is an vector space homomorphism of \(E\)- vector spaces defined (Existenz und Eindeutigkeit eines Homomorphismus durch die Abbildungen der Basis) by Kronecker-Delta

\begin{align*} \pi(y_j) = \delta_{1j} \end{align*}

Then \(\pi_{(y_1)} = \mathrm{id}_{E}\).

For \(x_i\) with \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\) we can write

\begin{align*} x_i = \sum_{j=1}^{d} \alpha_{i,j} \cdot y_j && \alpha_{i,j} \in E \end{align*}

furthermore, since \(y_i \cdot y_j \in L\) we get

\begin{align*} y_i \cdot y_j = \sum_{k=1}^{d} \beta_{i,j,k} y_k && \beta_{ijk} \in E \end{align*}

Let \(R = K[\alpha_{i,j}, \beta_{i,j,k}] \subseteq E\) be the K-subalgebra of \(E\)

By construction, \(R\) is a finitely generated K-Algebra. Now let \(e \in E\) be an arbitrary element. Since \(E \subseteq L\) and \(L\) is finitely generated, we can write \(e\) as polynomial

Substituting with

\begin{align*} x_i = \sum_{j=1}^{d} \alpha_{i,j} \cdot y_j && \alpha_{i,j} \in E \end{align*}

and then iteratively with

\begin{align*} y_i \cdot y_j = \sum_{k=1}^{d} \beta_{i,j,k} y_k && \beta_{ijk} \in E \end{align*}

we find a linear combination of \(y_1,..,y_d\) with coefficients in \(R\)

Applying \(\pi\) to such a presentation and using that \(\pi\) is $E$-Linear, we find, that \(E\) is a finitely generated A-Rlgebra Since \(R\) is a finitely generated K-Algebra, we deduce, that \(E\) is a finitely generated \(K\)-Algebra.
