product of pairwise different prime ideals as proper subset

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{p}_1,...,\mathfrak{p}_n\) prime ideals such that \(\mathfrak{p}_j \not\subseteq \mathfrak{p}_i\) for \(n \neq i\). Then

\begin{align*} \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \mathfrak{p}_i \subsetneq \prod_{i=1}^{n} \mathfrak{p}_i \end{align*}

2. Proof

Proof by contradiction: By Assumption, there exists an \(a_i \in \mathfrak{p}_i \setminus \mathfrak{p}_n\) Let \(a \coloneqq \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} a_i\). Then \(a \in \prod_{i=1}^{n-1} \mathfrak{p}_i\). Furthermore \(\prod_{i=1}^n \mathfrak{p}_i \subseteq \mathfrak{p}_n\). But \(a \not\in \mathfrak{p}_n\), because \(\mathfrak{p}\) is prime and \(a_i \not\in \mathfrak{p}_n\)
