Intersections of boundary and interior of regular closed sets

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(A,B \subseteq X\) regular closed set, such that \(\partial A \cap B^{\circ} \neq \emptyset\) (boundary and interior) Then

\begin{align*} A^{\circ} \cap B^{\circ} \neq \emptyset \end{align*}

2. Proof

Since by assumption \(\overline{A^{\circ}} = A\) it follows that \(A\) is closed (see: Closure as a closed Set). Because a boundary of a closed set is a subset, we know that \(\partial A \subseteq A\). Therefore we conclude, that \(\partial A \subseteq \overline{A^{\circ}}\)
