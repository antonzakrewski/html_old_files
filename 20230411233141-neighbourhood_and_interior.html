Neighbourhood and Interior

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(x \in X\). A subset \(U \subseteq X\) is a neighbourhood if and only if \(x \in U^{\circ}\) (see: interior of a topology)

2. Proof

2.1. a)

Suppose \(x \in A^{\circ}\). Since the interior is a subset \(A^{\circ} \subseteq A\) and the interior is open, it follows that \(A^{\circ}\) is a neighbourhood of \(x\) and therefore also \(A \subseteq A^{\circ}\)

2.2. b)

Suppose \(A\) is a neighbourhood of \(x\) Then there exists an open set \(O \subseteq X\), such that \(x \in O\). Since the interior is the largest open set, it follows, that \(O \subseteq A^{\circ}\) and therefore \(x \in A^{\circ}\)
