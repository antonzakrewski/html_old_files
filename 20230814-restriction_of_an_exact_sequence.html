restriction of an exact sequence

1. Proposition

let

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{}& A_1 \arrow[]{r}{\varphi_1}& A_2 \arrow[]{r}{\varphi_2} & A_3 \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be a short exact sequence of groups and \(A_i' \subseteq A_i\) as well as

\begin{align*} \varphi_i': A_i' \rightarrow A_{i+1}' \\ a \mapsto \varphi_i(a) \end{align*}

for \(i \in \{1,2,3\}\) the restriction - assuming it is welldefined TFAE:

  1. the induced sequence by \(\varphi_i': A_i \rightarrow A_{i+1}\) is exact
  2. \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi_{i+1}') \subseteq \mathrm{im}(\varphi_i')\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

by assumption,

\begin{align*} \varphi_{i+1} \circ \varphi_i = 0 \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} \varphi_{i+1}' \circ \varphi_{i}' = 0 \end{align*}

or \(\mathrm{im}(\varphi_{i}) \subseteq \mathrm{ker}(\varphi_{i+1}')\)
