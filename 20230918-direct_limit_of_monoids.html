direct limit of monoids

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathrm{Mon}\) be the category of monoids and \(F: \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}\) be a filtered diagram. Then the direct limit of \(F\) is

\begin{align*} M \coloneqq (\coprod_{i \in J} M_i)/~ \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} (m,i) \sim (n,j) :\Leftrightarrow \exists k \in J f_{ik}(m) = f_j{jk}(n) \end{align*}

and multiplication is defined

\begin{align*} [(m,i)][(n,j)] = [f_{ik}(m)f_{jk}(n)] \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. monoid

2.1.1. welldefined

Suppose \(m_i \in M_i, m_j \in M_j\) and \(m,n\) are upper bounds for \(i,j\). Then there exists an upper bound \(l\) of \(m,n\), such that by the functorial property we conclude

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & M_m \arrow[]{rd}{\varphi_{m,k}} & \\ M_i, M_j \arrow[]{ru}{} \arrow[]{rd}{}& & M_l \\ & M_n \arrow[]{ru}{\varphi_{n,k}} & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.1.2. neutral element

The neutral element is \([e_i]\) as for any \(m_j \in M_j\) it follows

\begin{align*} [e_i] \cdot [m_j] =& [\varphi_{i,k}(e_i) \cdot \varphi_{j,k}(m_j)] \\ =& [e_i \cdot \varphi_{j,k}(m_j)] \\ =& [\varphi_{j,k}(m_j)] \\ =& [m_j] \end{align*}

2.1.3. associative

for \(i,j,l\) with w.l.o.g. common upper bound \(k\) (see: welldefinedness), it follows that

\begin{align*} ([m_i] \cdot [m_j]) \cdot [m_l] =& [ \varphi_{i,k}(m_i) \cdot \varphi_{j,k}(m_j)] \cdot [m_l] \\ =& [(\varphi_{i,k}(m_i) \cdot \varphi_{j,k}(m_j)) \cdot \varphi_{l,k}(m_l)] \\ =& [\varphi_{i,k}(m_i) \cdot \left( \varphi_{j,k}(m_j) \cdot \varphi_{l,k}(m_l) \right)] \\ =& [ \varphi_{i,k}(m_i)] \cdot [\left( \varphi_{j,k}(m_j) \cdot \varphi_{l,k}(m_l) \right)] \\ =& [ \varphi_{i,k}(m_i)] \cdot [\left( \varphi_{j,k}(m_j) \cdot \varphi_{l,k}(m_l) \right)] \\ =& [m_i] \cdot ([\varphi_{j,k}(m_j)] \cdot [\varphi_{l,k}(m_l)]) \\ =& [m_i] \cdot ([m_j] \cdot [m_l]) \\ \end{align*}

2.2. monoid-homomorphism

for \(m_i \in M_i\) it follows that

\begin{align*} \varphi_i: M_i \rightarrow& M \\ m_i \mapsto& [m_i] \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} \varphi_i(m_i) \cdot \varphi_i(n_i) =& [m_i] \cdot [n_i] \\ =& [\varphi_{i,i}(m_i)] \cdot [\varphi_{i,i}(n_i)] \\ =& [\varphi_{i,i}(m_i) \cdot \varphi_{i,i}(n_i)] \\ =& [\mathrm{id} (m_i) \cdot \mathrm{id}(n_i)] \\ =& [m_i \cdot n_i] \\ =& \varphi_i(m_i \cdot n_i) \end{align*}

2.3. commuting

Suppose \(m_i \in M\). Then we get

\begin{align*} \varphi_{i}(m_i) =& [m_i] \\ =& [\varphi_{i,j}(m_i)] \\ =& \varphi_j(\varphi_{i,j}(m_i) \\ \end{align*}

Hence following diagram commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M_i \arrow[]{r}{\varphi_{i}}\arrow[]{r}{\varphi_{i,j}} & M_j \arrow[]{d}{\varphi_j}\\ & M \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2.4. universal property

Suppose there exists another object \(M'\), such that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M_i \arrow[]{r}{\psi_{i}}\arrow[]{r}{\varphi_{i,j}} & M_j \arrow[]{d}{\psi_j}\\ & M' \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then we define

\begin{align*} \psi: M \rightarrow& M' \\ [m_i] \mapsto& \psi_i(m_i) \end{align*}

2.4.1. welldefined

Suppose there exists \(m_i \in M_i, m_j \in m_j\) such that \([m_i] = [m_j]\) Then there exsts an \(M_k\) and morphisms \(\varphi_{i,k}, \varphi_{j,k}\) such that \(\varphi_{i,k}(m_i) = \varphi_{j,k}(m_j)\). Thus by commutativity of following diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M_i \arrow[]{r}{\varphi_{i,k}} \arrow[swap]{rd}{\varphi_i} \arrow[swap, bend right]{rdd}{\psi_{i}} & M_k \arrow[bend left]{dd}{\psi_k} \arrow[swap]{d}{\varphi_k} \\ & M \arrow[swap]{d}{\psi}\\ & M' \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

we conclude

\begin{align*} \psi([m_i]) =& \psi_i(m_i) \\ =& \psi_k(\varphi_{i,k}(m_i)) \\ =& \psi_k(\varphi_{i,k}(m_j)) \\ =& \psi_j(m_j) \end{align*}

2.4.2. monoid-homomorphism

Suppose \([m_j],[n_k] \in M\), w.l.o.g. \(j = k\) with \(M_j = M_k\) (see: welldefinedness), then it follows

\begin{align*} \psi([m_j]) \cdot \psi([n_k]) =& \psi_j(m_j) \cdot \psi_j(n_k) \\ =& \psi_j(m_j \cdot n_k) \\ =& \psi([m_j \cdot n_k]) \end{align*}

2.4.3. unique

Suppose there is a set-theoretic map \(\psi'\), such that for an \(m_i \in M_i\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} \psi'[m_i] \neq \psi_i(m_i) \end{align*}

Then it follows, that the left half

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} M_i \arrow[bend right, thick]{ddr}{\psi_{i}} \arrow[thick]{dr}{\varphi_i}\arrow[]{rr}{\mathrm{id} }& & M_i \arrow[bend left]{ddl}{\psi_i} \arrow[]{ld}{\varphi_{i}}\\ & M \arrow[thick]{d}{\psi} & \\ & M' & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
