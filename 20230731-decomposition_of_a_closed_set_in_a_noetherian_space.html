existence of a decomposition of a closed set in a noetherian space

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a Noetherian topological space and \(Y \subseteq X\) nonempty and closed. Then there exists \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) an irreducible closed subsets \(Y_1,...,Y_n\) such that

\begin{align*} Y = \bigcup_{i = 1}^n Y_i \end{align*}

2. Proof

Proof by contradiction: Let \(S\) be the subset of closed sets such that there doesn't exist a decomposition into irreducible components. Assuming that \(S\) is nonempty, there exists an minimal element \(Y\) (see: equivalent characterizations of noetherian space).

Furthermore, \(Y\) is not irreducible - otherwise \(Y = Y\) would be a decomposition - so there exist closed sets \(Y_1,Y_2 \subsetneq Y\) such that \(Y_1 \cup Y_2 = Y\). By assumption of the minimality of \(Y\), there exists a decomposition of \(Y_1 = \bigcup_{i=1}^n X_i\) and \(Y_2 = \bigcup_{i=1}^m Z_i\). Therefore, \(Y_1 \cup Y_2 = \bigcup_{i=1}^n X_i \cup \bigcup_{i=1}^m Z_i\) is a decomposition, contradictionwa
