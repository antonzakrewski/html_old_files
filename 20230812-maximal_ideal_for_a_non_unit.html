maximal ideal and non-unit

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(r \in R\) TFAE:

  1. \(r\) is not a unit
  2. there exists a maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m}\) such that \(r \in \mathfrak{m}\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \((r)\) the generated Ideal, then \((r) \neq R\) is a strict ideal. Hence exists a maximal ideal for the strict ideal.

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contraposition: corollary of unit and trivial ideal
