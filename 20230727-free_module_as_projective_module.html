free module as projective module

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(P\) be a free module over \(R\). Then \(P\) is a projective module

2. Proof

Suppose \(M,N\) are \(R\)-modules, \(\varphi: M \twoheadrightarrow N\) an epimorphism, \(f: P \rightarrow N\) a homomorphism and \((e_i)_{i \in I}\) a basis of \(P\). Then \(f\) is uniquely determined by

\begin{align*} f: P \rightarrow& N \\ e_i \mapsto& f(e_i) \end{align*}

Since \(\varphi\) is a surjection, for each \(f(e_i)\), there exists an \(m_i\) such that \(\varphi(m_i) = f(e_i)\) Therefore we define

\begin{align*} g: P \rightarrow& M \\ e_i \mapsto m_i \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} (\varphi \circ g)(e_i) =& \varphi(m_i) \\ = f(e_i) \end{align*}
