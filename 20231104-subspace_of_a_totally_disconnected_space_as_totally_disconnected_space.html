subspace of a totally disconnected space as totally disconnected space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a totally disconnected topological space and \(A \subseteq X\) a subset equipped with the subspace topology. Then \(A\) is also totally disconnected.

2. Proof

Let \(B \subseteq A\) be connected, then by connectedness of the image of a connected set \(\iota[B] \subseteq X\) is connected. Thus, since \(X\) is totally disconnected, it follows, that \(\vert \iota[B] \vert = 1\), hence \(A\) is also totally disconnected
