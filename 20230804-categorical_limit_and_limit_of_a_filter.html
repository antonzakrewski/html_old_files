categorical limit and limit of a topological filter

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(x \in X\) and \(\mathcal{F}\) a filter on \(X\). TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{F} \rightarrow x\) converges
  2. \(\mathcal{F}\) is a categorical limit for the construction below

2. Proof

Let \(\mathcal{U}_x\) be the neighbourhoodfilter, \(\mathcal{F}(X)\) the set of filters on \(X\) as poset category over sets Suppose

\begin{align*} \mathcal{C} \coloneqq \{G \\} \end{align*}

is the full subcategory of \(\mathcal{F}(X)\)

Then \(\mathcal{F} \in \mathcal{C}\) if and only if \(\mathcal{U}_x \subseteq \mathcal{F}\) or equivalently \(\mathcal{F} \rightarrow x\) (see: convergence of a filter and neighbourhoodfilter) Furthermore, by construction, \(\mathcal{F} \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) is initial in the subcategory and by construction every diagram commutes
