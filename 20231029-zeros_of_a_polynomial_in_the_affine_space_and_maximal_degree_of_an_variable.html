Zeros of a polynomial in the affine space and maximal degree of an variable

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) a polynomial, such that the highest degree of a variable is \(m \in \mathbb{N}\). Let \(M \subseteq K\) be a subset with \(\vert M \vert > m\) such that

\begin{align*} f[M^n] = 0 \end{align*}

Then \(f\) is the zero polynomial characteristic \(0\) ?

2. Proof

weak induction

2.1. base case, \(n = 1\)

follows from Grad eines Polynoms und maximale Anzahl an Nullstellen

2.2. induction step

Let \(f \in R[X_1,...,X_n]\) and w.l.o.g. \(X_n\) the variable with highest degree. Evaluating \(X_n\) for \(x \in M\) results in a Polynomial \(f_{x}(x_1,...,x_{n-1}\). Furthermore \(f_{x}(m_1,...,m_{n-1}) = 0\) for \(m \in M^{n-1}\). Therefore, by assumption, \(f_{x}(X_1,...,X_{n-1}) = 0\) is the zero polynomial.

Hence \(f'\) as Polynomial in \((R[X_1,...,X_{n-1}])(X_n)\) has degree \(m\) and at least \(m+1\) roots. Thus we conclude, that \(f' = 0\) (cf. Grad eines Polynoms und maximale Anzahl an Nullstellen)
