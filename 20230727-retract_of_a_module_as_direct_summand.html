retract of a group as direct summand

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(H \subseteq G\) a retract. Then there exists an subgroup \(H' \subseteq G\) such that

\begin{align*} G = H \oplus H' \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from idempotente Abbildung und direkte Summe des Kerns und des Bildes
