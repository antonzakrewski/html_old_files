1. Satz

Sei \(R\) ein kommutativer Ring und seien \(z_i,z_j,z_j' \in R^{n}\), \(r,r' \in R\). Dann folgt

\begin{align*} \mathrm{det} \left( z_1 , z_2 , \dots , r \cdot z_j + r' \cdot z_j' , \dots , z_n \right) =& \mathrm{det} \left( z_1 , z_2 , \dots , r \cdot z_j , \dots , z_n \right) + \mathrm{det} \left( z_1 , z_2 , \dots , r' \cdot z_j' , \dots , z_n \right) =& \end{align*} \begin{align*} \det \begin{pmatrix} z_1 \\ \vdots \\ r \cdot z_j + r' \cdot z_j' \\ \vdots \\ z_n \end{pmatrix} =& r \cdot \det \begin{pmatrix} z_1 \\ \vdots \\ z_j \\ \vdots \\ z_n \end{pmatrix} + r' \cdot \det \begin{pmatrix} z_1 \\ \vdots \\ s'_j \\ \vdots \\ z_n \end{pmatrix} \end{align*}

2. Beweis

Sei \(z_j = \left( \alpha_{j1},...,\alpha_{jn}\right)\) und \(z_j = \left( \alpha'_{j1},...,\alpha'_{jn}\right)\). Dann folgt

\begin{align*} \det \begin{pmatrix} z_1 \\ \vdots \\ r \cdot z_j + r' \cdot z_j' \\ \vdots \\ z_n \end{pmatrix} =& \sum_{\tau \in S_n} \mathrm{sgn}(\tau)\alpha_{1\tau(1)} ... \left(r \alpha_{j\tau(j)} + r' \alpha'_{j\tau(j)}\right)...\alpha_{n\tau(n)} \\ =& \sum_{\tau \in S_n} \mathrm{sgn}(\tau)\alpha_{1\tau(1)} ... \left(r \alpha_{j\tau(j)}\right)...\alpha_{n\tau(n)} + \sum_{\tau \in S_n} \mathrm{sgn}(\tau)\alpha_{1\tau(1)} ... \left(r' \alpha'_{j\tau(j)}\right)...\alpha_{n\tau(n)} \\ =& r \cdot \sum_{\tau \in S_n} \mathrm{sgn}(\tau)\alpha_{1\tau(1)} ... \alpha_{j\tau(j)}...\alpha_{n\tau(n)} + r' \sum_{\tau \in S_n} \mathrm{sgn}(\tau)\alpha_{1\tau(1)} ... \alpha'_{j\tau(j)}...\alpha_{n\tau(n)} \\ =& r \cdot \det \begin{pmatrix} z_1 \\ \vdots \\ z_j \\ \vdots \\ z_n \end{pmatrix} + r' \cdot \det \begin{pmatrix} z_1 \\ \vdots \\ s'_j \\ \vdots \\ z_n \end{pmatrix} \end{align*}
