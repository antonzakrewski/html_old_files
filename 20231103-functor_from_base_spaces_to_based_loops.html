functor from based spaces to based loops

1. Proposition

Given category of based spaces, there exists a canonical functor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Map}_*: \mathrm{Top}_{*} \rightarrow& \mathrm{Top}_{*} \\ (X,x_0) \mapsto& \Omega_{x_0}(X) (f: (X,x_0) \rightarrow (Y,y_0)) \mapsto& (f^{ \leftarrow}: \Omega_{x_0}(X) \rightarrow \Omega_{x_0}(Y))$ \end{align*}

where \(f^{ \leftarrow}\) is the postcomposition

\begin{align*} f^{ \leftarrow}: \mathrm{Omega}_{x_0}(X) \rightarrow& \Omega_{y_0}(Y) \\ g \mapsto& f \circ g \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

2.1.1. based

follows from

\begin{align*} f \circ g(1) =& f(x_0) \\ =& y_0 \end{align*}

2.1.2. continous

follows from the postcomposition for the compact open topology

2.2. functorial

similar to hom-functor
