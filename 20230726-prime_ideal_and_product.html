prime ideal and product

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(I,J \subseteq A\) ideals and \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq A\) a prime ideal. If \(I \cdot J \subseteq \mathfrak{p}\), then \(I \subseteq \mathfrak{p}\) or \(J \subseteq \mathfrak{p}\)

2. Proof

Suppose \(I \not\subseteq \mathfrak{p}\), then there exists an \(i \in I \setminus \mathfrak{p}\) Let \(j \in J\), then by construction \(j \cdot i \in J \cdot I \subseteq \mathfrak{p}\) and therefore \(j \cdot i \in \mathfrak{p}\). Since \(\mathfrak{p}\) is a prime ideal, we conclude that \(j \in \mathfrak{p}\)
