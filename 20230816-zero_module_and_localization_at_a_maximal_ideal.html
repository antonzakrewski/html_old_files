zero module and localization at a maximal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. TFAE:

  1. \(M = 0\)
  2. \(M_{\mathfrak{p}} = 0\) for all \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(R)\)
  3. \(M_{\mathfrak{m}} = 0\) for all \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(R)\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

special case

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contraposition: Assume that \(M \neq 0\), then there exists an \(m \in M \setminus \{0\}\). Let \(\mathrm{Ann}(m)\) be the Annihilator. Then \(\mathrm{Ann}(m)\) is a strict ideal (see: annihilator as strict ideal), hence there exists an maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m} \supseteq \mathrm{Ann}(m)\) (see: existence of a minimal prime ideal for a strict ideal) Therefore \(M_{\mathfrak{m}} = 0\), hence

\begin{align*} \frac{m}{1} = 0 \in M_{\mathfrak{m}} \end{align*}

Thus \(\exists s \in R \setminus \mathfrak{m}\) such that \(s \cdot m = 0\) or equivalently \(s \in \mathrm{Ann}(m) \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\), contradiction
