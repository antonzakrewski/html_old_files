finitely presented module and exact sequence

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module. TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is finitely presented
  2. there exist \(n,m \in \mathbb{N}\) and a short exact sequence of modules
\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} R^n \arrow[]{r}{\psi} & R^m \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & M & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

by assumption, there exists an epimorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi: R^m \twoheadrightarrow& M \end{align*}

where \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\) is finitely generated. Hence there exists another module epimorphism

\begin{align*} \psi: R^n \twoheadrightarrow \mathrm{ker}(\psi) \end{align*}

thus

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} R^n \arrow[]{r}{\psi} & R^m \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & M & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

2.2.1. \(M\) is finitely generated

By Assumption, \(\mathrm{im}(\varphi) = \mathrm{ker}(0) = M\)

2.2.2. the kernel is finitely generated

By Assumption, \(\mathrm{im}(\psi) = \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\)
