projection of a collapse as open map and collapsed set as open set or singleton

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a Hausdorff topological space, \(A \subseteq X\) a subset and \(X/A\) the topological collapse TFAE:

  1. \(\pi: X \rightarrow X/A\) is an open map
  2. \(A\) is open or \(\vert A \vert \leq 1\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.1.1. suppose \(\mathrm{int}(A) \neq \emptyset\)

Then \(\pi[\mathrm{int}(A)] = [A] \in X/A\) is by assumption open. Thus by definition of a quotient topology \(\pi^{-1}[ [A]] = A\) is open.

2.1.2. suppose \(\mathrm{int}(A) = \emptyset\)

Proof by contraposition Suppose \(\vert A \vert \geq 2\). Let \(a,b \in A\) with \(a \neq b\). Then since \(X\) is Hausdorff, there exist disjoint unions \(U_a, U_b\) Furhtermore, \(\pi\) is open. Hence \(\pi[U_a]\) is open and by construction therefore also \(\pi^{-1}[\pi[U_a \cup A]] = U_a \cup A\). Therefore, \((U_a \cup A) \cap U_b = U_a \cap U_b \cup A \cap U_b = A \cap U_b\) is open as intersection of open sets. Furthermore, it is nonempty, since \(b \in U_b \cap A\), thus contradiction to \(\mathrm{int}(A) = \emptyset\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

2.2.1. a)

Suppose \(A\) is open, then by construction \(\{[A]\} \subseteq X/A\) is open. Let \(O \subseteq X\) be open, then

\begin{align*} O = (O \cap A) \sqcup (O \setminus A) \end{align*}

If \(O \cap A = \emptyset\), then \(O \setminus A = O\) and hence again by construction \(\pi[O] = O \subseteq X/A\) with \(\pi^{-1}[O] = O\), thus \(O \subseteq X/A\) is open. If \(O \cap A \neq \emptyset\), then \(\pi[O] = \pi[O \cup A]\). Furthermore, \(\pi^{-1}[\pi[O \cup A]] = O \cup A\) and since \(O \cup A\) is open as union of open sets, we conclude, that \(\pi[O] = \pi[O \cup A]\) is open.

2.2.2. b)

For \(\vert A \vert \leq 1\), \(\pi\) is a Bijection and hence a homeomorphism (cf. final topology for a single bijection as homeomorphism)
