least common denominator and intersection of ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a principal ideal domain and \(r_1,...,r_n \in R\), then the least common denominator is

\begin{align*} \mathrm{lcd}(r_1,...,r_n) =& \bigcap_{i = 1}^n (r_1) \\ =& (d) \end{align*}

where \(\bigcap_{i=1}^n (r_1)\) (see: Intersection of ideals) is generated by \(d\)

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for \(n = 2\), the general folows from the associativity of a greatest common divisor and proof by induction

2.1. denominator

Since \(d \in (r_1)\) we conclude, that \(r_1 \mid d\) (see Assoziiertheit und Ideale), analogue for \(r_2\)

2.2. least common

Let \(d' \in R\) such that \(r_1 \mid d', r_2 \mid d'\). Then it holds true, that

\begin{align*} d' \in (r_1) \cap (r_2) = (d) \end{align*}

and therefore there exists an \(s \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} s \cdot d = d' \end{align*}

or \(d \mid d'\)
