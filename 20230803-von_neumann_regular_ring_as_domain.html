von Neumann regular ring and domain as field

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring TFAE:

  1. \(R\) is a field
  2. \(R\) is a von Neumann regular Integral domain

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of:

  • field as von Neumann regular ring
  • field as integral domain

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(a \in R \setminus \{0\}\), then there exists an \(x \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} a \cdot x \cdot a = a \end{align*}

Therefore

\begin{align*} a \cdot (x \cdot a - 1) =& 0 \end{align*}

And by assumption, since \(a \neq 0\), we get \(x \cdot a - 1 = 0\). Hence \(x\) is the inverse element
