ideal inside the center as two sided ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(\mathfrak{a}\) a left- (right-) ideal such that \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathrm{Z}(R)\) the center of \(A\) Then \(\mathfrak{a}\) is a two-sided ideal

2. Proof

Let \(b_l, b_r \in B\), then for \(a \in \mathfrak{a}\), it holds that

\begin{align*} b_l \cdot a \cdot b_r =& (b_l \cdot b_r) \cdot a \in \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}
