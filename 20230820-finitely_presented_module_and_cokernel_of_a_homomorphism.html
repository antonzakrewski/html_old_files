finitely presented module and cokernel of a homomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(R\)-module.

TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is finitely presented module
  2. there exist free \(R\)-modules \(A^m,A^n\) for \(m,n \in \mathbb{N}\) and a homomorphism \(\varphi: A^m \rightarrow A^n\) such that
\begin{align*} M \simeq \mathrm{coker}(\varphi) \end{align*}

2. Proof

using the characterization as finitely presented module and exact sequence:

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By Assumption there exists an exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A^m \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & A^n \arrow[]{r}{\psi} & M \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

and by Homomorphiesatz f√ºr Moduln, we conclude

\begin{align*} M = \mathrm{im}(\psi) \simeq& A^n/\mathrm{ker}(\psi) \\ \simeq& A^n/\mathrm{im}(\varphi) \\ \simeq \mathrm{coker}(\varphi) \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A^m \arrow[]{r}{\varphi}& A^n \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{coker}(\varphi) \arrow[]{r}{\simeq} & M \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be exact at \(A^n\) (see: exactification of a homomorphism), then by assumption we get, that \(M\) is finitely generated with \(\pi: A^n \rightarrow A^n/\mathrm{im}(\varphi)\) and the kernel is also finitely generated because of \(\mathrm{ker}(\pi) = \mathrm{im}(\varphi) \simeq A^n/\mathrm{ker}(\varphi) = A^n/0\)
