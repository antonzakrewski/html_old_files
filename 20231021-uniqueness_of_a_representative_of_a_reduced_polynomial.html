uniqueness of a representative of a reduced polynomial

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathbb{F}_q\) be a finite field \(\mathbb{F}_q[X_i]\) the polynomial ring. Then for a given polynomial \(f \in \mathbb{F}_q[X_i]\), there exists a unique reduced polynomial as representative

2. Proof

Suppose there exist \(f_1,f_2\) as reduced polynomials. Then \((f_1 - f_2)(x_i) = 0\) for all \(x_i \in (X_i)^I\).

Assume that \(f_1 - f_2 \neq 0\), then there exists a monomial with \(x_i^n\). Applying the evaluation homomorphism for \(x_j = 1, j \neq i\) results in a polynomial

\begin{align*} \varphi_{1}(f_1 - f_2) \in \mathbb{F}_q[X] \end{align*}

with \(\mathrm{deg}(\varphi_{1}(f_1 - f_2)) < q\) and still \(q\) roots, thus contradiction to Grad eines Polynoms und maximale Anzahl an Nullstellen
