elementary wedges under permutations

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring with Characteristic \(\mathrm{char}(R) \neq 2\) and \(M\) an \(R\)-module and \(\bigwedge^n M\) the exterior algebra of a module. Then

\begin{align*} (m_1 \wedge ... \wedge m_n) = \mathrm{sgn}(\sigma) \cdot (m_{\sigma(1)} \wedge ... \wedge m_{\sigma(n)}$. \end{align*}

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for the general case follows from induction

\begin{align*} 0 =& (v + w) \wedge (v + w) \\ =& v \wedge v + v \wedge w + w \wedge v + w \wedge w \\ =& 0 + v \wedge w + w \wedge v + 0 \\ =& v \wedge w + w \wedge v \end{align*}
