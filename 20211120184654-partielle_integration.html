Partielle Integration

1. Satz

Seien \(f\), \(g\) differenzierbare Funktionen. Dann gilt

\begin{align*} \int_a^b f'(x) \cdot g(x) \mathrm{d}x = [f(x)\cdot g(x)]^b_a - \int_a^b f(x)\cdot g'(x)\mathrm{d}x \end{align*}

2. Beweis

\begin{align*} (f(x) \cdot g(x))' =& f'(x) \cdot g(x) + g'(x) \cdot f(x) && \vert \int_{a}^{b} \mathrm{d}x \\ \int_{a}^{b} (f(x) \cdot g(x))' \mathrm{d}x =& \int_{a}^{b} f'(x) \cdot g(x) \mathrm{d}x + \int_{a}^{b} g'(x)f(x) \mathrm{d}x \\ [f(x) \cdot g(x)]_a^b =&\int_{a}^{b} f'(x) \cdot g(x) \mathrm{d}x + \int_{a}^{b} g'(x)f(x) \mathrm{d}x && \vert - \int_{a}^{b} g'(x)f(x) \mathrm{d}x \\ [f(x) \cdot g(x)]_a^b - + \int_{a}^{b} g'(x)f(x) \mathrm{d}x =& \int_{a}^{b} f'(x) \cdot g(x) \mathrm{d}x \\ \int_{a}^{b} f'(x) \cdot g(x) \mathrm{d}x =& [f(x) \cdot g(x)]_a^b - + \int_{a}^{b} g'(x)f(x) \mathrm{d}x \end{align*}
