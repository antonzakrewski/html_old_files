mapping torus

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(\varphi: X \rightarrow X\) a homeomorphism. Then the mapping torus \(M_{\varphi}\) is defined as quotient topology on the product topology

\begin{align*} M_{\varphi} \coloneqq ( X \times \mathbb{R})/\sim \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} (x, t) \simeq (y,s) \Leftrightarrow \exists n \in \mathbb{Z}: (\varphi^n(x), t + n) = (y,s) \end{align*}

2. Proof - welldefined equivalence relation

2.1. reflexive

follows from \(\varphi^0 = \mathrm{id}_{X}\) and thus

\begin{align*} (\varphi^{0}(x), t + 0) = (x, t) \end{align*}

2.2. symmetry

follows from

\begin{align*} (\varphi^n(x), t + n) =& (y, s) \vert (\varphi^{-n}(-), - n) \\ (x, t) =& (\varphi^{-n}(y), s - n) \end{align*}

and thus taking \(n' \coloneqq -n\)

2.3. transitivity

follows from

\begin{align*} (y, t) =& (\varphi^n(x), s + n) \\ (x, s) =& (\varphi^m(z), u + m) \end{align*}

and thus

\begin{align*} y = \varphi^n(x) \\ =& \varphi^n(\varphi^m(z)) \\ =& \varphi^{n + m}(z) \end{align*}

and therefore

\begin{align*} (y,t) = (\varphi^{n+m}(z), u + (n + m)) \end{align*}
