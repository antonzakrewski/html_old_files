injective homomorphism and preimage of an ideal

1. Proposotion

Let \(A,B\) be commutative rings, \(\mathfrak{b} \subseteq B\) an ideal and \(f: A \rightarrow B\) a homomorphism. Then the canonical map

\begin{align*} \widehat{f}: A/f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}] \rightarrow A/\mathfrak{b} \\ a + f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}] \mapsto f(a) + \mathfrak{b} \end{align*}

is an monomorphism

2. Proof

preimage of an ideal as ideal Let \(a + f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}] \in \mathrm{ker}(\widehat{f})\) then

\begin{align*} \widehat{f}(a + f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]) =& f(a) + \mathfrak{b} \end{align*}

with \(f(a) \in \mathfrak{b}\). Then we conclude, that

\begin{align*} a \in f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}] \end{align*}

and therefore

\begin{align*} a \in& f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}] \\ a + f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}] =& 0 \end{align*}
