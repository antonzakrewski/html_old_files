leading coefficients of an ideal as ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(A[X]\) a polynomial ring and \(I \subseteq A[X]\) an ideal. Then

\begin{align*} J_n \coloneqq \{\alpha \in A \vert \exists f \in I, f = \alpha X^i + \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_i X^i \} \subseteq A \end{align*}

is an ideal of \(A\) generalize for multivariable polynomial rings ?

2. Proof

2.1. (abelian) group

Let \(\alpha_1, \alpha_2\) in \(J_n\), then there exist \(f,g \in I\) such that \(f = \alpha_1 X^i + r_f(x), g = \alpha_2 X^i + r_g(x)\) Since \(I\) is a group there exists a \(-g \in I\) and hence \(f - g \in I\) or \((\alpha_1 - \alpha_2) X^i + r_f(x) - r_g(x) \in I\) or \(\alpha_1 - \alpha_2 \in I\) see: Kriterium f√ºr eine Untergruppe

commutativity is inherited from \(A\)

2.2. multiplication with \(A\)

Let \(\alpha \in J_n\) and \(\lambda \in A\), then there exists an \(f = \alpha X^i + \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_i X^i\). Therefore, since \(I\) is an ideal, \(\lambda X^0 \cdot f = \lambda \alpha X^i + r(x) \in I\) and hence \(\lambda \alpha \in J_n\)
