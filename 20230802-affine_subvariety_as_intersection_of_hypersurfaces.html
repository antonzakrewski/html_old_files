affine subvariety as intersection of hypersurfaces

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space and \(V\) an affine subvariety. Then there exist finitely many hypersurfaces \(H_1,...,H_k\) such that

\begin{align*} V = H_1,...,H_k \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(V = V(f_1,...,f_k)\) for polynomials \(f_i \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) (see: finite generation of an affine subvariety) Then let \(H_i \coloneqq V(f_i)\) and then

\begin{align*} V(f_1,...,f_k) =& \bigcap_{i = 1}^k V(f_1) \\ =& \bicgap_{i =1}^k H_i \end{align*}

(see: intersection of affine subvarieties)
