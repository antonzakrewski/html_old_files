equivalence relation of an orbit

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(X\) be a set and \(\triangle: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a group action and \(x\) Then an orbit \(\mathcal{O}_x\) is the equivalence class of the equivalence relation

\begin{align*} x \sim x' :\Leftrightarrow \exists g \in G : g \cdot x = x' \end{align*}

2. Proof(sketch)

2.1. symmetry

follows from \(g = e\)

2.2. reflexive

follows from \(g^{-1}\)

2.3. transitive

follows from $g1 ⋅ g2$d
