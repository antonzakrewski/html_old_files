composition of natural transformations as natural transformation

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{F}_1,\mathcal{F}_2, \mathcal{F}_3: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) be functors and \(\mu: \mathcal{F}_1 \rightarrow \mathcal{F}_2, \eta: \mathcal{F}_2 \rightarrow \mathcal{F}_3\) natural transformations Then the composition defined as

\begin{align*} \mu \circ \eta: \mathcal{F}_1 \rightarrow& \mathcal{F}_3 \\ A \mapsto& (\mathcal{F}_2 \circ \mathcal{F}_1(A): ) \end{align*}

Then

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{C} \arrow[bend left, ""{name = F_1}]{r}{\mathcal{F}_1} \arrow[""{name = F_2}]{r}{\mathcal{F}_2} \arrow[bend right, ""{name = F_3}]{r}{\mathcal{F}_3} \arrow[from=F_1, to=F_2] & \mathcal{D} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
