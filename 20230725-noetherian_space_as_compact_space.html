noetherian space as hereditarily compact space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space. TFAE:

  1. \(X\) is Noetherian
  2. any subset \(Y \subseteq X\) is compact

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(Y\) be a subset and let \(A_i \subseteq Y\) be closed subsets in the subspace topology. Then there exist closed sets \(A_i' \subseteq A_i \sqcup X \setminus Y\), such that \(A_i' \cap Y = A_i\) with

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{i \in I} A_i' \subseteq X \setminus Y \end{align*}

By assumption, there exists an minimal element \(A = \bigcap_{j = 1}^n A_{i(j)}'\) (see: noetherian space and minimal element of finite intersections) and it follows, that

\begin{align*} A \subseteq X \setminus Y \end{align*}

(see: minimal element of all finite intersections and intersection) Hence it follows, that

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{j = 1}^n A_{i(j)} =& \bigcap_{j = 1}^n A_{i(j)} \cap Y \\ =& X \setminus Y \cap Y \\ =& \emptyset \end{align*}

and therefore \(Y\) is compact (see equivalent characterization of compactness)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let $A1 \supseteq A2 …$ be a chain of closed sets. Let

\begin{align*} Y \coloneqq X \setminus \bigcap_{n \in \mathbb{N} } A_i \end{align*}

and \(A_i' \coloneqq Y \cap A_i\). By assumption, \(A_i'\) is closed in the subspace topology of \(Y\) and

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{i = 1}^n A_i' = \emptyset \end{align*}

Since \(Y\) is compact, there exist finitely many \(A_i'\) such that \(\bigcap_{i = 1}^n A_i' = \emptyset\). Therefore \(A_{\mathrm{max}(i)}' = \emptyset\) and \(A_{\mathrm{max}(i)} = \bigcap_{n \in \mathbb{N}} A_i\). Hence \(A_{\mathrm{max}(i)} \subseteq A_{n}\) for \(n \in \mathbb{N}\)
