finitely generated ideal

1. Definition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq R\) an ideal. Then \(\mathfrak{a}\) is said to be finitely generated, if there exist \(a_1,...,a_n \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} (a_1,...,a_n) = \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}
