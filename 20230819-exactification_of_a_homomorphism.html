exactification of a homomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(G,H\) be groups and \(\varphi: G \rightarrow H\) a homomorphism. Then there exists an exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \arrow[]{r}{\iota_G}& G \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & H \arrow[]{r}{\pi} & \mathrm{coker}(\varphi) & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof (sketch)

2.1. \(H\)

\(\pi(h) = 0\) iff \(h \in \mathrm{im}(\varphi)\)

2.2. \(\mathrm{coker}(\varphi)\)

\(\pi\) is surjective
