compact space and minimal closed set

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a compact topological space and \(A \subseteq X\) nonempty and closed. Then there exists a minimal, nonempty closed subset \(M \subseteq A\).

2. Proof

Zorn's lemma: Let \(M_i\) be a chain of closed, nonempty subsets. Let \(M \coloneqq \bigcap_{i = 1}^n M_i\), then we conclude that by construction \(M \subseteq M_i\) and if \(M = \emptyset\), it follows by compactness (cf. compact space and minimal closed set) that there exist \(M_{j(1)} \supseteq ... \supseteq M_{j(n)}\) such that

\begin{align*} \emptyset =& M \\ =& \bigcap_{i = 1}^n M_{j(i)} \\ =& M_{j(i)} \end{align*}

Contradiction to the assumption, hence \(M\) is nonempty and a lower bound.
