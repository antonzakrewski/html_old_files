vanishing set of a prime ideal as irreducible space

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) the Zariski spectrum equipped with the Zariski topology. Then for a prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) the Vanishing set \(V(\mathfrak{p})\) is irreducible.

2. Proof

Since a singleton is irreducible, it follows, that \(\{\mathfrak{p}\}\) is irreducible. Therefore, it is a consequence of

  • closure of a prime ideal (zariski topology)
  • irreducible subspace and closure
