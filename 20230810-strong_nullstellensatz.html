strong nullstellensatz

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field, \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) a polynomial ring and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) an ideal Then for the vanishing ideal and the zerolocus

\begin{align*} I(V(\mathfrak{a})) = \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} \supseteq \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

zero locus

2. Proof

2.1.

Let \(f \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\), then there exists a \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(f^n = g \in \mathfrak{a}\). by assumption, for any \(a \in V(\mathfrak{a}))\) we conclude, that

\begin{align*} g(a) = 0 \end{align*}

and hence

\begin{align*} f^n(a) = g(a) = 0 \end{align*}

since \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) is an Integral domain (cf. field as integral domain, Polynomringe über einem Integritätsbereich als Integritätsbereich) we conclude, that \(f(a) = 0\), hence \(f \in I(V(\mathfrak{a}))\)
