induced continuous map between spectra

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) commutative rings and \(f: A \rightarrow B\) a homomorphism, then the map

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(f): \mathrm{Spec}(B) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Spec}(A) \\ \mathfrak{q} \mapsto f^{-1}[\mathfrak{q}] \end{align*}

is a continuous map between the zariski spectra equipped with their zariski topologies

2. Proof

induced map between spectra by a ring homomorphism

2.1. continuous

Basis der abgeschlossenen Mengen und stetige Abbildung

Suppose \(V(\mathfrak{a}) \subseteq \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) is closed. Then we conclude

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(f)^{-1}[V(\mathfrak{a})] =& \mathrm{Spec}(f)^{-1}[ \{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A) \vert \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{p}\}] \\ =& \{ \mathfrak{b} \in \mathrm{Spec}(B) \vert f[\mathfrak{a}] \subseteq \mathfrak{b}\} \\ =& V(f(\mathfrak{a})) \end{align*}

Where \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]\) if and only if \(f(\mathfrak{a}) \subseteq (f \circ f^{-1})[\mathfrak{b}] \subseteq \mathfrak{b}\)
