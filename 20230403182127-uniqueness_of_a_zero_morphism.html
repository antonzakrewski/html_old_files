Uniqueness of a zero morphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category with a zero object \(0\) Then for objects \(A,B \in \mathcal{C}\) a zero morphism is unique up

2. Proof

Suppose \(z_1,z_2: A \rightarrow B\). It follows, that there exist morphisms \(f_1: A \rightarrow 0\) and \(f_2: A \rightarrow 0\) and \(g_1: 0 \rightarrow B\) and \(g_2: 0 \rightarrow B\) such that:

\begin{align*} g_1 \circ f_1 =& z_1 \\ g_2 \circ f_2 =& z_2 \end{align*}

By assumption, an initial and terminal morphism is unique (up to isomorphism), and thererfore follows \(g_1 = g_2, f_1 = f_2\) and \(z_1 = z_2\)
