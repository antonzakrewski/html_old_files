union of algebraic sets

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space. then for algebraic sets \(Z(S_1),...,Z(S_k)\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i = 1}^{k} Z(S_i) =& Z(\prod_{i =1}^k S_i) =& Z(\bigcap_{i = 1}^{k} S_i) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(\bigcup_{i = 1}^{k} Z(S_i) \subseteq Z(\bigcap_{i =1}^k S_i)\)

Suppose \((p_1,...,p_n) \in \bigcup_{i = 1}^k Z(S_i)\), then there exists an \(j\) such that \((p_1,...,p_n) \in Z(S_j)\). Hence for all \(f \in S_j\), it holds that \(f(p_1,...,p_n) = 0\), thus especially also for all \(f \in \bigcap_{i=1}^n S_i \subseteq S_j\)

2.2. \(Z(\bigcap_{i = 1}^{k} S_i) \subseteq Z(\prod_{i =1}^k S_i)\)

corollary of

  • product of ideals as subset of the intersection
  • algebraic set and order

2.3. \(Z(\prod_{i = 1}^{k} S_i) \subseteq \bigcup_{i = 1}^{k} Z(S_i)\)

Let \((p_1,...,p_n) \in Z(\prod_{i = 1}^{k} S_i)\), then for all \(f = \sum \alpha_j \prod_{i=1}^n f_i\) with $fi ∈ it holds, that \(f(p_1,...,p_n) = 0\). Suppose for all \(S_i\) there exists an \(g_i \in S_i\) such that \(g_i(p_1,...,p_n) \neq 0\), then \((\prod_{i=1}^n g_i)(p_1,...,p_n) \neq 0\). Hence by contraposition, there exists an \(S_j\) such that for all \(f \in S_j\) it holds, that \(f(p_1,...,p_n) = 0\). Therefore \((p_1,...,p_n) \in Z(S_j) \subseteq \bigcup_{i = 1}^{k} Z(S_i)\)
