real projective space as compact space

1. Proposition

the real projective space \(\mathbb{R} \mathbb{P}^n\) is a compact topological space

2. Proof

Let \(S^n \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n+1}\) be the n-sphere. it is closed and bounded, hence compact (see: euklidischer raum als eigentlicher Raum). Note, that it is also compact in the subspace topology \(\mathbb{R}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}\), as every open covering of \(S^n\) in \(\mathbb{R}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}\) is an open covering in \(\mathbb{R}^{n+1}\), since \(\mathbb{R}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}\) is open in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) (see: Ãœbertragung der Offenheit von der Unterraumtopologie

The map

\begin{align*} \pi: \mathbb{R}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\} \rightarrow& \mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n (x_0,...,x_n) \mapsto& [x_0 : ... : x_n] \end{align*}

is by definition of \(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n\) as quotient topology continuous. Furthermorey

\begin{align*} \pi[S^n] = \mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n \end{align*}

thus by continuous image of compact space as compact space, it follows, that \(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n\) is compact

Let \([x_0 : ... x_n] \in \mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n\), then \(\lambda \coloneqq \sqrt{\sum_{i=0}^{n} x_0^2} \neq 0\) since \((x_0,...,x_n) \neq 0\). Therefore

\begin{align*} [x_0 : ... : x_n] = \frac{1}{\lambda} \cdot [x_0 : ... : x_n] \end{align*}

and furthermore by construction

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{\lambda} (x_0,...,x_n) \in S^n \end{align*}

Hence \([x_0 : ... : x_n] \in \pi[S^n]\) and since \([x_0 : ... : x_n]\) was arbitrary.
