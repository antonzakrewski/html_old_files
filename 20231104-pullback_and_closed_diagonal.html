pullback and closed diagonal

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T})\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map Consider the pullback along \(f\), \(X \times_{Y} X\), then the diagonal \(\{(x,x) \in X \times_Y X \}\) is closed if and only if for each \((x,y) \in X \times_Y X\) with \(x \neq y\) there exists disjoint open neighbourhoods \(O_x,O_y\)

2. Proof

2.1. a)

Suppose there exist \((x,y) \in X \times_Y X\) such that there doesn't exist disjoint neighbourhoods. Then for each open neighbourhood of \((x,y)\), it follows, that \(O_x \times O_y \cap \Delta \neq \emptyset\). Hence \((x,y) \in \overline{\Delta}\) and therefore, \(\Delta\) is not closed

2.2. b)

Let \((x,y) \not \in \Delta\), then \(x \neq y\) and by assumption, there exist open neighbourhoods \(O_x,O_y\). Therefore, \(O_x \times O_y\) is open in \(X \times X\) and by <pullback subspace> also \(O_x \times O_y \cap X \times_Y Y\). Thus by openness and neighbourhood for all points, the complement is open and hence the diagonal is closed
