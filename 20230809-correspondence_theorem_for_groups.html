correspondence theorem for groups

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(N \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup, \(\pi_N: G \rightarrow G/N\) the canonical epimorphism for the quotient group. Suppose \(\mathcal{l}_{G/N}\) is the set of subgroups of \(G/N\) and \(\mathcal{l}_{N}\) the set of subgroups \(U\) such that \(N \subseteq U\). Then

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathcal{l}_N \rightarrow& \mathcal{l}_{G/N} \\ U \mapsto& \pi_N[U]\\ \psi: \mathcal{l}_{G/N} \rightarrow& \mathcal{l}_{N} \\ U \mapsto& \pi_N^{-1}[U] \\ \end{align*}

are maps with

\begin{align*} \varphi \circ \psi =& \mathrm{id} \\ \psi \circ \varphi =& \mathrm{id} \end{align*}
