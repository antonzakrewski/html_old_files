reduced ring in general not an integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a reduced ring, then \(R\) is in general not an integral domain

2. Proof

let \(\mathbb{Z} / 6 \mathbb{Z} \simeq \mathbb{Z} /2 \mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z} /3 \mathbb{Z}\), then it is reduced (cf. general chinese remainder theorem, field as reduced ring, product of reduced rings), but \(2,3\) are zero divisors
