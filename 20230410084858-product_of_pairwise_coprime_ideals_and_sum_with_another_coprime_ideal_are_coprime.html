Product of pairwise coprime ideals and sum with another coprime ideal are coprime

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(I_j\) for \(j = 1,..,n\) pairwise coprime ideals. Then \(\prod_{i=2}^{n} I_i\) and \(I_1\) are coprime

2. Proof

proof by induction using the equivalence of ideal with a unit and \(R\)

2.1. basis

\(\prod_{i=2}^{n} I_i = I_2\) and \(I_1\) follows by assumption

2.2. step

Let \(\prod_{i=2}^{n} I_i\) and \(I_1\) be coprime ideals. Then \(a \in \prod_{i = 2}^{n} I_i\) and \(b \in I_1\) exist such that \(a + b = 1\). We also know, that \(a' \in I_{n+1}\) and \(b' \in I_1\) exist, such that \((a' + b') = 1\). Therefore we conclude, that \(1 = 1 \cdot 1 = (a+b) \cdot (a'+b') = a \cdot a' + b \cdot (a' + b') + b' \cdot a\) with \(a \cdot a' \in \prod_{i = 2}^n I_i \cdot I_{n+1} = \prod_{i=2}^{n+1} I_i\) and \(b \cdot (a' + b') + b' \cdot a \in I_1\)
