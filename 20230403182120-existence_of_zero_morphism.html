Existence of zero morphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category with a zero object \(0\) Then for objects \(A,B \in \mathcal{C}\) there exists a zero morphism.

2. Proof

By Assumption there exists an terminal morphism \(t_A: A \rightarrow 0\) and an initial morphism \(i_B: 0 \rightarrow A\) By composition of morphisms we obtain:

\begin{align*} i_B \circ t_A: A \rightarrow B \end{align*}

and by construction a zero morphism
