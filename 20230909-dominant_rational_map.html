dominant rational map

1. Definition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(X,Y\) varieties and \(\varphi: X \rightarrow Y\) a rational map. Then \(\varphi\) is said to be dominant, if there exists an open set \(U\) and a \(\varphi_U\) such that

\begin{align*} \overline{\varphi_U[U]} \end{align*}

is dense in \(Y\), i.e.e

\begin{align*} \overline{\varphi_U[U]} = Y \end{align*}
