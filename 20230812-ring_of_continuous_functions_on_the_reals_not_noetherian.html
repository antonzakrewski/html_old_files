ring of continuous functions on the reals not noetherian

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be the ring of ring of continuous functions \(\{f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \vert f \text{ continuous}\}\), then \(R\) is not noetherian

2. Proof (sketch)

Let

\begin{align*} f_n: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow& \mathbb{R} \\ x \mapsto \begin{cases} (1 - \vert x - n - 0.5 \vert) & \mbox{if } x \in [n-0.5,n+0.5] \\ 0 & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} (f_1) \subsetneq (f_1, f_2) \subsetneq ... \end{align*}
