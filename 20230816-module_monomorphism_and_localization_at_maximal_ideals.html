module monomorphism and localization at maximal ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(M,N\) an \(R\)-modules. Suppose \(\varphi: M \rightarrow N\) is a module homomorphism. TFAE:

  1. \(\varphi\) is a monomorphism
  2. \(\varphi_{\mathfrak{p}}: M_{\mathfrak{p}} \rightarrow N_{\mathfrak{p}}\) is a monomorphism for \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(R)\)
  3. \(\varphi_{\mathfrak{m}}: M_{\mathfrak{m}} \rightarrow N_{\mathfrak{m}}\) is a monomorphism for \(\mathfrak{m} \in \mathrm{Spec}_{\mathrm{max}}(R)\)

see: localization functor

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

localization as exact functor exact functor preserves monomorphisms

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

special case

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

Let

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(phi) \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & M \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & N \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

be an exact sequence at \(M\). Then by assumption

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & \mathrm{ker}(phi)_{\mathfrak{m}} \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & M_{\mathfrak{m}} \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & N_{\mathfrak{m}} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is also exact. Furthermore, by assumption \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi_{\mathfrak{m}}) = 0\) and hence \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi) = 0\) (cf. zero module and localization at a maximal ideal)
