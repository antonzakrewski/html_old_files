greatest common divisor and Fibonacci Sequence

1. Proposition

Given the fibonacci sequence \(F_n\), following statement holds true for the greatest common divisor

\begin{align*} \mathrm{gcd}(F_n,F_m) = F_{\mathrm{gcd}(n,m)} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a) divisor

Let \(g \coloneqq \mathrm{gcd}(m,n)\), then there exist \(k,k' \in \mathbb{Z}\) such that \(n = k \cdot g, m = k' \cdot g\) We can then conclude, that \(F_{n} = F_{k \cdot g} = F_{g + (k-1) \cdot g}\) Applying the recursion formula follows:

\begin{align*} F_{g + (k - 1) \cdot g} =& F_{g} \cdot F_{(k-1) \cdot g + 1} + F_{g} \cdot F_{(k-1) \cdot g - 1} \\ =& F_g \cdot (F_{(k-1) \cdot g + 1} + F_{(k-1) \cdot g - 1} ) \end{align*}
