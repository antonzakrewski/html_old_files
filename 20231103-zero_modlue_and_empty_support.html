zero module and empty support

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(A\)-module.

TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is the zero module
  2. the Support of a module \(\mathrm{Supp}(M)\) is empty

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose that for each \(\mathfrak{p}\) it holds, that \(M_{\mathfrak{p}} = 0\). Then the Annihilator \(\mathrm{Ann}(m)\) of an element \(m \in M\) is not contained in \(\mathfrak{p}\), hence \(\mathrm{Ann}(m)\) is not a strict ideal (cf. existence of a prime ideal for a strict ideal), therefore for any \(m \in M\), \(\mathrm{Ann}(m) = A\) Hence we conclude, that since \(1 \in \mathrm{Ann}(m)\), it holds that

\begin{align*} m =& 1 \cdot m \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

corollary of

  • empty spectrum and zero ring

as \(\mathrm{Supp} \subseteq \mathrm{Spec}(A)\)
