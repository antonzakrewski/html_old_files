monoid as single-object category

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be a monoid. Then \(M\) is canonically an object in the category \(B(M)\) where

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Hom}_{B(M)}(M,M) = M \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} m \circ_{B(M)} m' =& m \cdot m' \\ \mathrm{id}_{M} =& 1_M \end{align*}

2. Proof

the identity morphism is induced by the neutral element \(\mathrm{id}_{M} \sim e\). associativity of the composition of morphisms follows from the associativity of \(M\)
