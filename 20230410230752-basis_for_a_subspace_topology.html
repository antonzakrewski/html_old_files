Basis for a subspace topology

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{B}\) a basis for \(X\). Suppose further \(Y \subseteq X\) with the subspace topology. Then

\begin{align*} \mathcal{B}_Y =& \{B \cap Y \vert B \in \mathcal{B}\} \end{align*}

is a basis

2. Proof

2.1. Bedingung für eine Basis einer Topologie

2.1.1. a)

follows from Allgemeine Distributivgesetz für Vereinigung und Schnitt

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{B \in \mathcal{B}_Y} B =& \bigcup_{B \in \mathcal{B}} (B \cap Y) \\ =& (\bigcup_{B \in \mathcal{B}} B ) \cap Y \\ =& X \cap Y \\ =& Y \end{align*}

2.1.2. b)

Let \(B_Y,B_Y' \in \mathcal{B}_Y\): Then there exist \(B,B' \in \mathcal{B}\) such that \(B \cap Y = B_Y, B' \cap Y = B_Y'\) furthermore there exist \(B_i \in \mathcal{B}\) such that \(\bigcup_{i \in I} B_i = B \cap B'\). Therefore we can construct with \(B_{i,Y} \coloneqq B_i \cap Y \in \mathcal{B}_Y\):

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in I} B_{i,Y} =& \bigcup_{i \in I} (B_i \cap Y) \\ =& (\bigcup_{i \in I} B_i) \cap Y \\ =& (B \cap B') \cap Y \\ =& (B \cap Y) \cap (B' \cap Y) \end{align*}

2.2. equal topology

2.2.1. a)

Let \(O_y \subseteq Y\) an open set in \(Y\), then there exists an open set \(O_x\) such that \(Y \cap O_x = O_y\) It follows, that there exist \(B_i \in \mathcal{B}\) such that \(\bigcup_{i \in I} B_i = O_X\). Furthermore

\begin{align*} O_y =& O_x \cap Y \\ =& ( \bigcup_{i \in I} B_i) \cap Y \\ =& \bigcup_{i \in I} (B_i \cap Y) \end{align*}

2.2.2. b)

Let \(\bigcup_{i \in I} B_{i,Y}\) be a union of sets from \(\mathcal{B}_Y\) with \(B_{i,y} := B_i \cap Y\) Then

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in I} B_{i,Y} =& \bigcup_{i \in I} (B_i \cap Y) \\ =& (\bigcup_{i \in I} B_i ) \cap Y \\ \end{align*}

We know, that \(\bigcup_{i \in I} B_i\) is open in \(X\) and therefore \(\bigcup_{i \in I} B_i \cap Y\) is open in \(Y\)
