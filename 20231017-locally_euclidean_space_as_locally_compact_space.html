locally euclidean space as locally compact space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a locally euclidean topological space. Then \(X\) is weakly locally compact.

2. Proof

let \(x \in X\), then by assumption, there exists an open neighbourhood \(V \subseteq X\) of \(x\) and an open set \(O \subseteq \mathbb{R}^n\) and a homeomorphism \(f: V \rightarrow O\) Since \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is locally compact (cf. euclidean space as locally compact space) , we conclude, that there exists an open subset \(O' \ni f(x)\) with compact closure \(\mathrm{cl}(O') \subseteq O\) By continuous image of compact space as compact space we conclude that \((f^{-1})[\overline{O'}]\) is also compact and by continuity

\begin{align*} f[\mathrm{cl}(f^{-1}[O'])] \subseteq \mathrm{cl}((f\circ f^{-1})[O']) = \mathrm{cl}(O') \end{align*}

Furthermore, since \(f\) as homeomorphism is a closed map \(f\mathrm{cl}([f^{-1}[O'])\) is closed with \(O' \subseteq f(\mathrm{cl}(f^{-1}[O']))\) 1 and hence \(\mathrm{cl}(f^{-1}[O']) = f^{-1}[\mathrm{cl}(O')]\) Thus \(f^{-1}[O']\) is relatively kompakt and a neighbourhood of \(x\)
