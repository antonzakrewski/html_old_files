localization of a sum of modules

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(M\) an \(R\)-module and \(N, L \subseteq M\) submodules. Then for the localization functor it holds, that

\begin{align*} S^{-1}(N + L) = S^{-1}N + S^{-1}L \subseteq S^{-1}M \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(S^{-1}(N + L) \subseteq S^{-1}N + S^{-1}L\)

Suppose \(\frac{n}{s_1} \in S^{-1}N, \frac{l}{s_2} \in S^{-1}L\), then \(s_2 \cdot n + s_1 \cdot l \in N + L\) and hence

\begin{align*} \frac{n}{s_1} + \frac{l}{s_2} =& \frac{s_2 \cdot n + s_1 \cdot l}{s_1 \cdot s_2 } \in S^{-1}(N + L) \end{align*}

2.2. \(S^{-1}(N + L) \supseteq S^{-1}N + S^{-1}L\)

Let w.l.o.g. \(\frac{n}{s} \in S^{-1}N\), then \(n + 0 \in N + L\) and hence \(\frac{n}{s} \in S^{-1}(N + L)\)
