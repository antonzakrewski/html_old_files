nilpotent element and prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq A\) a prime ideal and \(s \in A\) nilpotent. Then

\begin{align*} s \in \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

2. Proof

proof by induction:

2.1. IA

Let \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(s^n = 0\).

2.2. IS

Suppose \(s^n \in \mathfrak{p}\), then \(s^{n-1} \cdot s \in \mathfrak{p}\), so we may assume w.l.o.g. that \(s^{n-1} \in \mathfrak{p}\), since otherwise \(s \cdot s^{n-2} = s^{n-1} \in \mathfrak{p}\). Therefore \(s^{n-1} \in \mathfrak{p}\).
