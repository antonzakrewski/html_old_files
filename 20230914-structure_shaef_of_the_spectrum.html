structure sheaf of the spectrum

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(A\) be a ring, \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) the zariski spectrum equipped with the Zariski topology. Then the structure sheaf of the general spectrum for \(U \subseteq \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) is defined

  1. a section \(s \in \mathcal{O}(U)\) is a function \(s: U \rightarrow \coprod_{\mathfrak{p} \in U} A_{\mathfrak{p}}\) to the categorical coproduct of the localization at the prime ideals
  2. for \(\mathfrak{p} \in U\) there exists a neighbourhood \(V \subseteq U\) and \(a,f \in A\) such that \(s[V] = \frac{a}{f} \in \bigcap_{\mathfrak{q} \in V} A_{\mathfrak{q}}\) - hence \(f \not\in \mathfrak{q} \forall \mathfrak{q} \in V\)

2. Proof

2.1. presheaf

2.1.1. ring

Defining addition and multiplication pointwise:

2.1.1.1. neutral element
  1. additive

    the neutral element is \(s: \mathfrak{p} \mapsto 0\), trivially a regular function

  2. multiplicativ

    the neutral element is \(s: \mathfrak{p} \mapsto 1\), trivially a regular function

2.1.1.2. additive inverse

for \(s: x \mapsto A_x/f_x\) the inverse element is \(s: x \mapsto -A_x/f_x\),

2.1.1.3. welldefined

for \(s_1,s_2\) and \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\), there exist neighbourhoods \(U_1,U_2\) such that \(s_1 = \frac{A_1}{f_1}, s_2 = \frac{A_2}{f_2}\). Thus for the neighbourhood \(U_1 \cap U_2\) (cf. Closure of the neighbourhood system under finite intersection) it follows that

  1. multiplication
    \begin{align*} s_1 \cdot s_2 =& \frac{A_1}{f_1} \cdot \frac{A_1}{f_2} \\ =& \frac{A_1 \cdot A_2}{f_1 \cdot f_2} \end{align*}

    Furthermore, since \(f_1,f_2 \not\in \mathfrak{q} \forall \mathfrak{q} \in U_1 \cap U_2\), we conclude, since \(\mathfrak{q}\) is prime, that \(f_1 \cdot f_2 \not\in \mathfrak{q}\)

2.1.1.4. addition
\begin{align*} s_1 + s_2 =& \frac{A_1}{f_1} + \frac{A_2}{f_2} \\ =& \frac{A_1f_2 + A_2 f_1}{f_1 f_2} \end{align*}

Furthermore, since \(f_1,f_2 \not\in \mathfrak{q} \forall \mathfrak{q} \in U_1 \cap U_2\), we conclude, since \(\mathfrak{q}\) is prime, that \(f_1 \cdot f_2 \not\in \mathfrak{q}\)

2.1.2. restriction

the natural restriction is

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathcal{O}(U) \rightarrow& \mathcal{O}(V) \\ s \mapsto& s_{\vert V} \end{align*}

a ring-homomorphism, as can be seen by evaluating it pointwise furthermore, it commutes <trivially>

2.2. sheaf

2.2.1. locality of a sheaf

follows from the pointwise definition of a map

2.2.2. glueing of a sheaf

follows from glueing lemma for regular functions on an affine variety
