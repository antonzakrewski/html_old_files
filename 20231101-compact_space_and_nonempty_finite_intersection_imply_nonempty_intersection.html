compact space and nonempty finite intersection imply nonempty intersection

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space.

TFAE:

  1. \(X\) is compact
  2. for every family of closed sets \(Z_i\), if every finite intersection is nonempty, \(\bigcap_{i=1}^n Z_i \neq 0\), then \(\bigcap_{i \in I} Z_i\) is nonempty.

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Proof by contraposition Suppose \(Z_i\) is a family of closed sets, such that \(\bigcap_{i \in I} Z_i\) is empty. then by compact space and intersection of closed sets there exists a finite subset, such that \(\bigcap_{i=1}^n Z_i = \emptyset\).

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Proof by contradiction Suppose there exists a family of closed sets, such that \(\bigcup_{i \in I} Z_i\) is empty and there doesn't exist a finite, empty intersection. Then this is a contradiction to 2), hence there exists a finite, empty intersection
