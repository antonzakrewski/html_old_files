generic point and kolmogoroff space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a Kolmogoroff Space. Then there exists at most one generic point

2. Proof

Let \(g \in X\) be a generic point and \(a \in X\) with \(a \neq g\). Then by Assumption of \(X\) being a t0-space there exists a neighbourhood \(O\) such that \(g \in O\) and \(a \not\in O\) or \(a \in O, g \not\in O\). Since \(g\) is generic (cf. generic point and open set), we conclude that \(g \in O, a \not\in O\). Hence \(X \setminus O \subsetneq X\) is a closed set with \(a \in X \setminus O\) and thus \(\overline{ \{a\}} \neq X\).
