top-enriched category

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a small category. Then \(\mathcal{C}\) is said to be topologically enriched, if for objects \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) the hom-set \(\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) is equipped with a compactly generated topological space such that the composition

\begin{align*} \circ_{A,B,C}: \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B) \times \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,C) \rightarrow \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,C) \end{align*}

is continuous for \(A,B,C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\)
