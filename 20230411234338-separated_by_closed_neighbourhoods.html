separated by closed neighbourhoods

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space. Two points \(x,y \in X\) or subsets \(x,y \subseteq X\) are said to be separated by closed neighbourhoods, if there exist closed neighbourhoods \(N_x \in \mathcal{N}(x), N_y \in \mathcal{N}(y)\) such that \(N_x \cap N_y = \emptyset\)
