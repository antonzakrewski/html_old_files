ultarfilter and primeness for the union

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a set, \(\mathcal{F}\) an Ultrafilter on \(X\). For sets \(A,B\) such that \(A \cup B \in \mathcal{F}\), we conclude that \(A \in \mathcal{F}\) or \(B \in \mathcal{F}\)

2. Proof

Suppose w.l.o.g. \(A \not\in \mathcal{F}\). Then \(X \setminus A \in \mathcal{F}\) (see: Ultrafilter und genau ein Komplement) And furthermore,

\begin{align*} B \setminus A = (X \ A) \cap (A \cup B) \end{align*}
