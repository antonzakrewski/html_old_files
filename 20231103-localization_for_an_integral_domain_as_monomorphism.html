localization for an integral domain as injective

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain and \(S \subseteq R\) a multiplicatively closed subset, such that \(0 \not\in S\) Then the ring homomorphism for the localization is injective.

\begin{align*} \iota: R \rightarrow S^{-1}R \end{align*}

2. Proof

minimal kernel and monomorphism: Let \(r \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} \iota(r) = \frac{r}{1} = \frac{0}{1} \end{align*}

then there exists an \(s \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} s \cdot 1 \cdot r =& s \cdot 1 \cdot 0 \\ s \cdot r =& 0 \end{align*}

since \(s \neq 0\), it follows by integrity of \(R\), that \(r = 0\). hence \(\mathrm{ker}(\iota) = \{0\}\)
