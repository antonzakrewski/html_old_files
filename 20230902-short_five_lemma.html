short five lemma

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an abelian category and with following commutative diagram

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{g} & B \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{f} & C \arrow[]{r}{} \arrow[]{d}{h}& 0 \\ 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A' \arrow[]{r}{} & B' \arrow[]{r}{} & C' \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

such that \(g,h\) are isomorphisms. Then \(f\) is also an isomorphism

2. Proof

corollary of the ordinary Five-Lemma
