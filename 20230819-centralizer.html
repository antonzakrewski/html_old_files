centralizer

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(S \subseteq G\) a subset. Then the centralizer is the subgroup containing all elements

\begin{align*} C_{G}(S) \coloneqq \{g \in G \vert \forall s \in S : g \cdot s = s \cdot g \} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(c_1, c_2 \in C_G(S)\), then for any \(s \in S\) we get

\begin{align*} (c_1 \cdot c_2) \cdot s =& c_1 \cdot s \cdot c_2 \\ =& s \cdot (c_1 \cdot c_2) \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} s \cdot c_1 =& c_1 \cdot s && \vert \cdot c_1^{-1}, c^{-1} \cdot \\ c_1^{-1} \cdot s \cdot c_1 \cdot c_1^{-1} =& c_1^{-1} \cdot c_1 \cdot s \cdot c_1^{-1} \\ c_1^{-1} \cdot s =& s \cdot c_1^{-1} \end{align*}
