neutral element and neutral element for an invertible element

1. Proposition

Let \((M,\cdot)\) be a Monoid, \(e \in M\). TFAE:

  1. \(e\) is the neutral element
  2. there exists a invertible element \(s \in M\) such that \(s \cdot e = s\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

follows from \(s = e\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(1\) be the neutral element and \(s,s^{-1} \in M\) such that \(e \cdot s = s\), then we conclude

\begin{align*} 1 =& s \cdot s^{-1} \\ =& e \cdot s \cdot s^{-1} \\ =& e \cdot 1 \\ =& e \end{align*}
