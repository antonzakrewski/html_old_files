transitivity of finitely generated A-Algebras

1. Proposition

let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(B\) a finitely generated A-Algebra and \(C\) a finitely generated B-Algebra. Then \(C\) is a finitely generated A-Algebra

2. Proof

By assumption there exist epimorphisms \(\varphi: A[X_1,...,X_n] \twoheadrightarrow B\) and \(\psi: B[Y_1,...,Y_m] \twoheadrightarrow C\). Since \(B[Y_1,..,Y_m]\) is a finitely generated $A$-algebra (see finitely generated A-Algebra and finitely generated Polynomial Ring of the A-Algebra) there exists an epimorphism \(\varphi': A[X_1,...,X_k] \twoheadrightarrow B[Y_1,...,Y_m]\). Then

\begin{align*} \psi \circ \varphi': A[X_1,...,X_k] \twoheadrightarrow C \end{align*}

is an epimorphism such that \(C\) is finitely generated (see: Komposition von Abbildungen und Surjektivität/Injektivität, composition of ring homomorphisms as ring homomorphism)
