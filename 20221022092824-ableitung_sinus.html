Ableitung Sinus

1. Satz

\begin{align*} \sin(x)' =& \cos(x) \end{align*}

2. Beweis

2.1. Lemma

  • Additionstheorem
  • Grenzwert: \(\frac{\sin(\theta)}{\theta}\)
  • Grenzwert: \(\frac{1 - \cos(x)}{x}\)

2.2. Beweis

\begin{align*} \sin(\theta)' =& \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{\sin(\theta + \varepsilon) - \sin(\theta)}{\varepsilon}\right) \\ =& \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{\sin(\theta)\cos(\varepsilon) + \sin(\varepsilon)\cos(\theta) - \sin(\theta)}{\varepsilon}\right) \\ =& \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{\sin(\theta)\cos(\varepsilon) - \sin(\theta)}{\varepsilon}\right) + \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{\sin(\varepsilon)\cos(\theta)}{\varepsilon}\right) \\ =& -\sin(\theta) \cdot \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{1 - \cos(\varepsilon)}{\varepsilon}\right) + \cos(\theta) \lim_{\varepsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{\sin(\varepsilon)}{\varepsilon}\right) \\ =& -\sin(\theta) \cdot 0 + \cos(\theta) \cdot 1 \\ =& \cos(\theta) \end{align*}
