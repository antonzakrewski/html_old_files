bijective map in general not split mono

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a concrete category and \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) a Bijection. Then \(f\) is in general not split mono

2. Proof

For the category Top and a set \(X\) with \(\vert X \vert > 1\) consider the discrete topology \(\mathcal{T}_d\) and indiscrete topology \(\mathcal{T}_i\). Then for the \(\mathrm{id}_{X}: (X,\mathcal{T}_d) \rightarrow (X, \mathcal{T}_i)\) the set-theoretic Inverse map is \(\mathrm{id}_{X}\) but it is not continuous, hence not a morphism in \(\mathrm{Top}\)
