lower limit topology

1. Definition / Proposition

The lower limit topology on \(\mathbb{R}\) is defined via the basis \(\mathcal{B} := \left \{[a,b) \subsetneq \mathbb{R} \vert a < b \right\}\)

2. Proof

2.1. a)

For \(x \in \mathbb{R}\) we conclude, that \([x,x+1) \in \mathcal{B}\) and therefore \(\mathbb{R} = \bigcup_{B \in \mathcal{B}} B\)

2.2. b)

For \([a,b)\) and \([a',b')\) it holds, that:

\begin{align*} [a,b) \cap [a',b') = \begin{cases} \emptyset & \mbox{if } b \leq a' \\ \left [\mathrm{max}(a,a'),\mathrm{min}(b,b')\right) & \mbox{else } \end{cases} \end{align*}

and thus \([a,b) \cap [a',b') \in \mathcal{B}\)
