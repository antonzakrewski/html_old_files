point and maximal ideal over an algebraically closed field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field, \(S \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) a subset of polynomials and \(\mathcal{M}_S\) the set of maximal ideals containing \(S\) Then the map

\begin{align*} \iota: V(S) \rightarrow& \mathcal{M}_S \\ (a_1,...,a_n) \mapsto& (X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n) \end{align*}

is a welldefined bijection

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined injection

special case of injective map from points of an affine space to maximal ideals

2.2. surjective

Let \(\mathfrak{m} \in \mathcal{M}_S\), then by the weak Nullstellensatz, there exist \(a_1,...,a_n\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m} = (X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n) \end{align*}

Let \(f \in S\), then since \(S \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\), we conclude that \(f(a) = 0\). Hence we get \(a \in V(\mathfrak{m}) \subseteq V(S)\) or \(a \in V(S)\)
