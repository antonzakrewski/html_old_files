commutator subgroup as normal subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(G^{(0)}\) the commutator subgroup. Then \(G^{(1)}\) is a normal subgroup.

2. Proof

Let \(k \in G\) and \(ghg^{-1}h^{-1} = [gh] \in G^{(1)}\). Then we get

\begin{align*} k \cdot ghg^{-1}h^{-1} \cdot k^{-1} = [k[gh]] \in G^{(1)}$ \end{align*}
