centralizer of subsets

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(S_1 \subseteq S_2 \subseteq G\). Then for the centralizer it holds,

\begin{align*} C_G(S_2) \subseteq C_G(S_1) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Definitions: if \(g \in C_G(S_2)\), then \(g\) also commutes with any \(s \in S_1\). Hence \(g \in C_G(S_1)\)
