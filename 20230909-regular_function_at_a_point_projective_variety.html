regular function at a point (projective variety)

1. Definition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(\mathbb{P}_{K}^n\) the projective space and \(Y \subseteq \mathbb{P}_{K}^n\) a quasi-projective variety. A function \(f: Y \rightarrow K\) is said to be regular at \(p \in Y\), if there exists an open set \(O \subseteq Y\) and homogeneous polynomials \(f,h \in K[X_0,X_1,...,X_n]\) such that \(0 \not\in h[O]\) and

\begin{align*} f = \frac{g}{h} && \text{ on O} \end{align*}
