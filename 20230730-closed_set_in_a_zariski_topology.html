maximal ideal as closed set in a zariski topology

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\). TFAE:

  1. \(\mathfrak{p}\) is maximal ideal
  2. \(\{\mathfrak{p}\}\) is closed

2. Proof

The closure of a prime ideal (zariski topology) is

\begin{align*} \overline{\{\mathfrak{p}\}} = V(\mathfrak{p}) \end{align*}

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

If \(\mathfrak{p}\) is maximal, then for any ideal \(\mathfrak{m}\) with \(\mathfrak{p} \subsetneq \mathfrak{m}\) we conclude \(\mathfrak{m} \not\in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\). Hence \(V(\mathfrak{p}) = \{\mathfrak{p}\}\) Because the Closure of a closed set is closed, we conclude that \(\{\mathfrak{p}\}\) is closed

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(\mathfrak{m}\) be an ideal with \(\mathfrak{p} \subsetneq \mathfrak{m}\), then we conclude that \(\mathfrak{m} \not\in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) and therefore \(\mathfrak{m}\) is not prime or \(\mathfrak{m} = A\). If \(\mathfrak{m}\) were a maximal ideal, then it would also be prime (see: maximales Ideal als Primideal)
