Hilbert's basis theorem

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian ring, then \(A[X_1,...,X_n]\) is also noetherian.

2. Proof

w.l.o.g. for \(A[X]\), the general case follows from induction and polynomialring in multiple variables and inductive construction Let \(I \subseteq A[X]\) be an ideal, then \(J_n \coloneqq \{\alpha \in A \vert \exists f \in I \vert f = \alpha X^n + \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_i X^i \}\) is an ideal of \(A\) (see: leading coefficients of an ideal as ideal) Because of the inclusion of ideals of leading coefficients $J0 \subseteq J1 \subseteq …$ and since \(A\) is noetherian (see: equivalent characterization of a noetherian ring) , there exists a \(J_m\) such that $J = Jm+1 = Jm+2 …$. Furthermore each \(J_i\) is finitely generated by \(\alpha_{i,1},...,\alpha_{i,n}\)

Let \(G \coloneqq \{\alpha_{i,j} X^i \vert i \leq m, j \leq n\}\).

Suppose \(f \in I\), then for each Monom \(\lambda_i X^i\) there exists an \(\beta \in A\) and \(\alpha_{j,k}\) such that \(j \leq i\) and \(\alpha_{j,k} X^{j} \cdot (\beta X^{i-j} = \lambda_i X^i\). Therefore \((G) = I\) and since \(\vert G \vert < \infty\), \(I\) is finitely generated ideal and $A[X] noetherian
