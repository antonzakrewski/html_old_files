glueing lemma for regular functions on an affine variety

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(V\) an affine variety and \(U_i\) an open covering of affine subvarieties and \(f_i: U_1 \rightarrow K\) regular functions such that

\begin{align*} \left(f_{j}\right)_{ \vert U_i \cap U_j} = \left(f_{i}\right)_{\vert U_i \cap U_j} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} f(x) \coloneqq f_j(x) & \mbox{if } x \in U_j \\ \end{align*}

2.1. welldefined

follows from construction and requirement for overlapping

2.2. regular

Let \(x \in V\), then there exists an \(i \in I\) such that \(x \in U_i\). Hence, since \(f_i\) is regular, it follows that there exists a neighbourhood \(V \subseteq U_i \subseteq V\) such that \(\left(f \right) = \left(f_{i}\right)_{\vert U_i} = \frac{h}{g}\) for \(0 \not\in g[U_i]\).
