Ideals under localization

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) an ideal and \(S \subseteq A\) multiplicatively closed set. Then

\begin{align*} S^{-1}\mathfrak{a} \coloneqq \{\frac{a}{s} \vert a \in \mathfrak{a}, s \in S \} \subseteq S^{-1}A \end{align*}

is an ideal

2. Proof

2.1. additive group

<Kriterium f√ºr eine Untergruppe> Let \(\frac{a_1}{s_1}, -\frac{a_2}{s_2} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{a}\), then

\begin{align*} \frac{a_1}{s_1} - \frac{a_2}{s_2} =& \frac{a_1 \cdot s_2}{s_1 \cdot s_2} - \frac{a_2 \cdot s_1}{s_2 \cdot s_1} \\ =& \frac{a_1 \cdot s_2 - a_2 \cdot s_1}{s_1 \cdot s_2} \end{align*}

where \(s_1 \cdot s_2 \in S\) and \(a_1 \cdot s_2 - a_2 \cdot s_1 \in \mathfrak{a}\), hence

\begin{align*} \frac{a_1}{s_1} -\frac{a_2}{s_2} \in S^{-1}\mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

2.2. multiplicatively closed

Let \(\frac{r}{s'} \in S^{-1}R\), then

\begin{align*} \frac{r}{s'} \cdot \frac{a}{s} = \frac{r \cdot a}{s' \cdot s} \end{align*}

and since \(\mathfrak{a}, S\) are multiplicatively closed, we get \(r \cdot a \in \mathfrak{a}, s \cdot s' \in S\).
