image of an irreducible space as irreducible space

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T})\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map. Suppose \(X\) is an irreducible space, then the image \(f[X]\) is likewise.

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition Let \(A_1, A_2 \subsetneq f[Y]\) closed such that \(A_1 \cup A_2 = f[Y]\). Then \(f^{-1}[A_1], f^{-1}[A_2] \subsetneq X\) are closed proper subsets and hence \(X\) is reducible
