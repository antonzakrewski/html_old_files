Transformation: Winkelfunktion zur Exponentialfunktion

1. Satz

\begin{align*} \sin(\theta) =& \frac{1}{2i} (e^{i\theta} - e^{-i\theta}) \\ \cos(\theta) =& \frac{1}{2} (e^{i\theta} + e^{-i\theta}) \end{align*}

2. Herleitung

  • Transformation: Exponentialfunktion zur Winkelfunktion
\begin{align*} e^{i\theta} =& \cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta) \\ e^{-i\theta} =& \cos(\theta) - i\sin(\theta) \end{align*}

2.1. Sinus

\begin{align*} e^{i\theta} + e^{-i\theta} =& 2\cos(\theta) && \vert \cdot \frac{1}{2} \\ \frac{1}{2} (e^{i\theta} + e^{-i\theta}) =& \cos(\theta) \end{align*}

2.2. Cosinus

\begin{align*} e^{i\theta} - e^{-i\theta} =& 2i \cdot \sin(\theta) && \vert \cdot \frac{1}{2i} \\ \frac{1}{2i} (e^{i\theta} - e^{-i\theta}) =& \sin(\theta) \end{align*}
