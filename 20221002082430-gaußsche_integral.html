Gaußsche Integral

1. Satz

\begin{align*} \int_{x \in \mathbb{R}}^{} e^{-ax^2} \mathrm{d}x = \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{a}} && a > 0 \end{align*}

2. Beweis

über Integral in Polarkoordinaten und

\begin{align*} \left(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \mathrm{d}x \right)^2 =& \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} \mathrm{d}x \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-y^2} \mathrm{d}y \\ =& \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-(x^2 + y^2)} \mathrm{d}x y \mathrm{d} \\ =& \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-r^2}r \mathrm{d}r \mathrm{d}\theta \\ =& 2 \pi \int_{0}^{\infty} re^{-r^2} \mathrm{d}r \\ =& 2\pi \int_{-\infty}^{0} \frac{1}{2}e^s \mathrm{d}s \\ =& \pi \int_{-\infty}^{0} e^s \mathrm{d}s \\ =& \pi (e^{0} - e^{- \infty}) \\ =& \pi \end{align*}
