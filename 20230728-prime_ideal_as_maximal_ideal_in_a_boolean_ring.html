prime ideal as maximal ideal in a Boolean ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a Boolean ring and \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq R\) a prime ideal. Then \(\mathfrak{p}\) is maximal

2. Proof

Let \(\mathfrak{p} \subsetneq \mathfrak{m}\), then there exists an \(m \in \mathfrak{m} \setminus \mathfrak{p}\). We also get

\begin{align*} m^2 =& m \\ 0 =& m(1 - m) \end{align*}

since \(\mathfrak{p}\) is prime, we conclude, that \(1 - m \in \mathfrak{p}\) and therefore also \(1 - m \in \mathfrak{m}\). Therefore

\begin{align*} 1 =& (1 - m) + m \in \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

and

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m} = R \end{align*}
