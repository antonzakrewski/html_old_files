Image of a functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(F: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor. The Image of the functor consists the classes of all objects and morphisms in \(\mathcal{D}\), namely \(\{FA \vert A \in \mathcal{C}\}\) and \(\{F f \vert f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\}\)
