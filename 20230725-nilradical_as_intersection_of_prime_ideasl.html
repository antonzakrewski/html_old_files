Nilradical as intersection of prime ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathcal{N}_A\) the nilradical and \(\mathcal{P}\) the set of prime ideals. Then

\begin{align*} \mathcal{N}_A \coloneqq \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathcal{P}} \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(\subseteq\)

corollary of nilpotent element and prime ideal

2.2. \(\supseteq\)

Let \(x \in A \setminus \mathcal{N}_A\) and Let \(S\) be the set of ideals \(\mathfrak{a}\) such that \(x^n \not\in \mathfrak{a}\) for all \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) Then the zero ideal \((0) \in S\) because \(x\) is not nilpotent and hence \(S\) is nonempty.

Let $\mathfrak{a}1 \subseteq \mathfrak{a}2 …$ be a Chain of ideals with \(\mathfrak{a}_i \in S\), then

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a} \coloneqq \bigcup_{n \in \mathbb{N} } \mathfrak{a}_n \end{align*}

is an ideal with \(x^n \not\in \mathfrak{a}\) (see: Vereinigung von einer Kette von Idealen als Ideal)

Ergo let \(\mathfrak{p}\) be a maximal element of \(S\)

Suppose \(a_1,a_2 \in A \setminus \mathfrak{p}\). Then by maximality of \(S\), \((\mathfrak{p}, a_1), (\mathfrak{p}, a_2) \not\in S\).

Thus there exist \(n,n' \in \mathbb{N}\) with \(x^n \in (\mathfrak{p}, a_1)\) and \(x^{n'} \in (\mathfrak{p}, a_2)\) Therefore \(x^{n+n'} \in (\mathfrak{p}, a_1a_2)\) or equivalently \(a_1a_2 \not\in \mathfrak{p}\)

This is equivalent to \(\mathfrak{p}\) being prime (see: Ring without a prime ideal as multiplicatively closed set)
