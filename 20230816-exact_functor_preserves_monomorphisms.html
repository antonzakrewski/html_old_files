exact functor preserves monomorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(F: \mathrm{Ab} \rightarrow \mathrm{Ab}\) be an exact functor such that \(F(0) = 0\) and \(\varphi: G \rightarrow H\) a monomorphism for groups \(G,H\) Then \(F(\varphi)\) is also a monomorphism

2. Proof

Consider the sequnce

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & G \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & H \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

It is exact at \(G\) (cf. exact sequence and monomorphism), hence by assumption

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} F(0) \arrow[]{r}{} & F(G) \arrow[]{r}{F(\varphi)} & F(H) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact at \(F(G)\), thus \(F(\varphi)\) is a monomorphims
