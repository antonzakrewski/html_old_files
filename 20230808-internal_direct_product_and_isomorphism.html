internal direct product and isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(N,H \subseteq G\) subgroups. TFAE:

  1. \(N,H\) is a internal direct product
  2. the following map is a isomorphsim:
\begin{align*} \varphi: N \times H \rightarrow G \\ (n,h) \mapsto n \cdot h \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

2.2.1. product of group subset

Suppose \(g \in G\), then since \(\varphi\) is an epimorphism there exist \((n,h) \in N \times H\) such that \(n \cdot h = g\). Hence \(g \in NH\) or \(G = NH\)

2.2.2. intersection

Let \(g \in U \cap N\), then since \(N\) is a group it follows that \(g^{-1} \in N\). Therefore

\begin{align*} \varphi(g,g^{-1}) =& g \cdot g^{-1} \\ =& 1 \end{align*}

hence \((g,g^{-1}) \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\) From the statement about a minimal kernel and monomorphism, we conclude \((g,g^{-1}) = (e,e)\), hence \(g = e\)
