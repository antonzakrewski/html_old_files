image (category theory)

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) a morphism for objects \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) Then the image of \(f\) - if it exists - is defined as Subobject \(I\) with \(m \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(I,Y)\) such that there exists a morphism \(e \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,I)\) that makes following diagram commute

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X \arrow[]{rd}{e} \arrow[]{rr}{f} & & Y \\ & I \arrow[hookrightarrow]{ru}{m} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

And furthermore \(I\) satisfies following universal property: For every object \(I'\) with a monomorphism \(m' \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(I',Y)\) and a morphism \(e' \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(X,I')\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X \arrow[]{rd}{e'} \arrow[]{rr}{f} & & Y \\ & I' \arrow[hookrightarrow]{ru}{m'} & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

there exists a unique morphism \(v \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(I,I')\)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} X \arrow[bend right]{rdd}{e'} \arrow[]{rd}{e}\arrow[]{rr}{f} & & Y \\ & I \arrow[hookrightarrow, ]{ru}{m} \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists! v}& \\ & I' \arrow[hookrightarrow, bend right ]{ruu}{m'}& \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
