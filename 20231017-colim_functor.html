derived colim functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{J}\) be categories, such that \(\mathcal{C}\) admits colimits of the shape \(\mathcal{J}\). Then for the functor category \(\mathcal{C}^\mathcal{J}\) the derived colimit functor is defined as

\begin{align*} \mathrm{colim}: \mathcal{C}^\mathcal{J} \rightarrow& A \\ (\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{J} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}) \mapsto& \mathrm{colim}_{\mathcal{F}} \\ (\eta: \mathcal{F} \Rightarrow \mathcal{G}) \mapsto& \left(\eta': \mathrm{colim}_{\mathcal{F}} \rightarrow \mathrm{colim}_{\mathcal{G}} \right) \end{align*}

where \(\eta'\) is the morphism induced by the universal property

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(A) \arrow[swap]{dd}{\eta(A)} \arrow[]{rr}{\mathcal{F}(f)} \arrow[swap]{rd}{\pi_{\mathcal{F(A)}}} & & \mathcal{F}(B) \arrow[]{dd}{\eta(B)} \arrow[]{ld}{\pi_{\mathcal{F}(B)}}\\ & \mathrm{colim}(\mathcal{F}) \arrow[dashed]{dd}{\exists! \eta'}& \\ \mathcal{G}(A) \arrow[swap]{rd}{\pi_{\mathcal{G}(A)}}& & \mathcal{G}(B) \arrow[]{ld}{\pi_{\mathcal{G}(B)}}\\ & \mathrm{colim}(\mathcal{G}) & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proofsketch

functorial property follows from the uniqueness of the mediating morphism \(\eta'\)
