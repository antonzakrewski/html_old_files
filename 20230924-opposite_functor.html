opposite functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories and \(F: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a functor. the opposite functor is defined

\begin{align*} \mathcal{F}^{\mathrm{op}}: \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}} \rightarrow& \mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{op}} A \mapsto& \mathcal{F}(A) \ f \mapsto& \mathcal{F}(f^{\mathrm{op}})^{\mathrm{op}} \end{align*}

where \(\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}}, \mathcal{D}^{\mathrm{op}}\) are opposite categories
