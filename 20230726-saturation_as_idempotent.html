saturation as idempotent

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring, \(A \subseteq R\) a subset and \(S \subseteq R\) a multiplicatively closed set. Then saturation is idempotent, i.e.

\begin{align*} A^S = (A^S)^S \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(A^s \subseteq (A^S)^S\)

corollary of:

  • saturation of a set with respect to a multiplicatively closed set as superset

2.2. \(A^s \supseteq (A^S)^S\)

Let \(a \in (A^S)^S\), then there exists an \(a \in R\) and \(s_1, s_2 \in S\) such that

\begin{align*} (a \cdot s_1) \cdot s_2 \in& A^S \\ a \cdot s_1 \in& A \end{align*}

Furthermore, by assumption of \(S\) being closed under multiplication, we get \(s_1 \cdot s_2 \in S\) and hence

\begin{align*} a \cdot (s_1 \cdot s_2) \in& A \end{align*}

or \(a \in A^S\)
