radical of an ideal as a radical ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{a}\) an ideal. Then \(\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\) is a radical ideal.

2. Proof

2.1. a)

Suppose \(a \in \sqrt{\sqrt{ \mathfrak{a}}}\), then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(b \coloneqq a^n \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\). Further, by assumption there exists an \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(b^m \in \mathfrak{a}\). Therefore

\begin{align*} b^m =& (a^n)^m \\ =& a^{n \cdot m} \end{align*}

with \(n \cdot m \in \mathbb{N}\) or \(a \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\)
