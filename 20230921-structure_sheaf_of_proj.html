structure sheaf of Proj

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(M\) be a monoid and \(R = \bigoplus_{m \in m} R_m\) a graded ring and \(\mathrm{Proj}\) the set of nonirrelevant ideals equipped with the Zariski topology on Proj. Then the structure sheaf on \(\mathrm{Proj}\) is defined as ringed space:

  1. for an open set \(U \subseteq \mathrm{Proj}\), \(\mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Proj}}(U)\) is the subset of functions
\begin{align*} f: U \rightarrow \coprod_{\mathfrak{p} \in U} R_{\mathfrak{p}} \end{align*}

where \(R_{\mathfrak{p}}\) is the localization at a prime ideal

  1. such that for \(\mathfrak{p} \in U\) it follows, that \(f(\mathfrak{p}) \in R_{\mathfrak{p}}\)
  2. and there exists an open neighbourhood \(V \subseteq U\) with \(\mathfrak{p} \in V\) and homogeneous elements \(s,t \in R_m\) for an \(m \in M\)
  3. for \(\mathfrak{q} \in V\) it follows, that \(f(\mathfrak{q}) = \frac{s}{t} \in R_{\mathfrak{q}}\)

and the canonical restriction

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U,V}: \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Proj}}(U) \rightarrow& \mathcal{O}_{\mathrm{Proj}}(V) \\ f \mapsto& f_{\vert V} \end{align*}
