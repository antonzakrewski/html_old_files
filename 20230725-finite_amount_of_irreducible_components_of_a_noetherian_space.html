finite amount of irreducible components of a noetherian space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a noetherian topological space. Then there exists only finitely many irreducible components.

2. Proof

Since \(X\) is closed, there exists an decomposition of a closed set into finitely many irreducible components. Furthermore this decomposition is unique and therefore there only exist finitely many irreducible components.
