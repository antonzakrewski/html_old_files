union of a chain of ring homomorphisms as ring-homomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(S\) be a ring, \(R_i\) be a chain rings with ring-homomorphisms \(\tau_{i}: R_i \rightarrow S\) such that

\begin{align*} \tau_{i \vert R_i \cap R_j} = \tau_{j \vert R_i \cap R_j} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in I} \tau_i: \bigcup_{i \in I} R_i \rightarrow S \end{align*}

is a welldefined ring-homomorphism

2. Proof

\(\bigcup_{i \in I} R_i\) is a welldefined ring (cf. union of a chain of rings as ring) Furthermore, since \(\tau_i, \tau_j\) coincide, wherevere possible

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in I} \tau_i: x \mapsto \tau_i(x) && \mbox{if } x \in \tau_i \\ \end{align*}

is welldefined.

2.1. multiplicative homomorphism

union of a chain of monoid-homomorphisms as monoid-homomorphism

2.2. additive homomorphism

union of a chain of monoid-homomorphisms as monoid-homomorphism

2.3. unit to unit

follows from \(\tau_i(1) = 1\)
