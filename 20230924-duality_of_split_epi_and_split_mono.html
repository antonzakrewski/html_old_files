Duality of split epi and split mono

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\).

TFAE:

  1. \(f\) is split epi (resp. split mono)
  2. \(f^{\mathrm{op}} \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{op}}}(B,A)\) is a split mono (resp. split epi)

2. Proof

Let \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) be a split epi, then by assumption, there exists a split mono \(g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,A)\) such that

\begin{align*} f \circ g = \mathrm{id}_{B} \end{align*}

Application of the contravariant identity functor results in

\begin{align*} g^{\mathrm{op}} \circ f^{\mathrm{op}} =& \mathrm{Id}^{\mathrm{op}}(f \circ g) \\ =& \mathrm{id}_{B} \end{align*}

Hence by definition, \(f^{\mathrm{op}} \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,A)\) is a split mono
