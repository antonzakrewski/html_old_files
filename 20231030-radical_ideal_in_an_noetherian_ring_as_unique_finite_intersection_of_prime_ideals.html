radical ideal in an noetherian ring as unique, finite intersection of prime ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian ring, \(\mathfrak{r}\) a radical ideal. Then there exist finitely many prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}_1,...,\mathfrak{p}_n\) such that

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{i=1}^n \mathfrak{p}_i = \mathfrak{r} \end{align*}

Furthermore, those prime ideals \(\mathfrak{p}_i\) are unique up to inclusion, i.e for

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{i=1}^n \mathfrak{p}_i =& \bigcap_{j=1}^m \mathfrak{p}_j \end{align*}

it follows for each \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\), that there exists an \(j \in \{1,...,m\}\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p}_i \subseteq \mathfrak{p}_j \end{align*}
