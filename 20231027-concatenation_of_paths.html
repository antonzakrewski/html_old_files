concatenation of paths

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(\gamma_1, \gamma_2: [0,1] \rightarrow X\) be a path such that

\begin{align*} \gamma_1(1) = \gamma_2(0) \end{align*}

Then concatenation of \(\gamma_1,\gamma_2\) is defined as path

\begin{align*} \gamma_2 \cdot \gamma_1: [0,1] \rightarrow& X \\ x \mapsto& \begin{cases} \gamma_1(2x) & \mbox{if } x \in [0,0.5]\\ \gamma_2(2x - 1) & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

2. Proof

continuouity follows from the glueing lemma for a locally finite, closed covering
