real projective space as real manifold

1. Proposition

The projective space \(\mathbb{R}\mathbb{P}^n\) is an \(n\)-dimensional Manifold.

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} U_i \coloneqq \{[x_0 : ... : x_n] \vert x_0 \neq 0\} \end{align*}

for \(i \in \{0,...,n\}\)

Then \(U_1,...,U_n\) is an open covering, as the <TODO>

\begin{align*} U_i = \pi[\prod_{j \neq i} \mathbb{R} \times (\mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\})] \end{align*}

Let

\begin{align*} \varphi_i: U_i \rightarrow& \mathbb{R}^n \\ [x_0 : ... : x_n] \mapsto& \frac{1}{x_i} (x_0,...,x_{i-1},x_{i+1},...,x_n) \end{align*}

2.1. welldefined

Let \([x_0 : ... : x_n] = [y_0 : ... : y_n] \in U_i\). Then by assumption, \(y_i, x_i \neq 0\) and furthermore, there exists an \(\lambda \in \mathbb{R}^\times\) such that

\begin{align*} x_j = \lambda y_j \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \varphi_i([x_0 : ... : x_n]) =& \frac{1}{x_i} (x_j)_{j \neq i} \\ =& \frac{1}{\lambda y_j} \cdot (\lambda y_j)_{j \neq i} \\ =& \frac{1}{y_j} \cdot (y_j)_{j \neq i} \end{align*}

2.2. surjective

Let \((\alpha_1,...,\alpha_n) \in \mathbb{R}^n\). Then \(\varphi_i(\alpha_1,...,\alpha_{i-1},1,\alpha_{i+1},...,\alpha_n) = \frac{1}{1} (\alpha_{1},...,\alpha_n)\)

2.3. injective

Let

\begin{align*} \varphi_i(x_1,...,x_n) =& \frac{1}{x_i} (x_j)_{j \neq i} \\ =& \frac{1}{y_i} (y_j)_{j \neq i} \\ =& \varphi_i(y_1,...,y_n) \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} (x_1,...,x_n) \cdot \frac{y_i}{x_i} = (y_1,...,y_n) \end{align*}
