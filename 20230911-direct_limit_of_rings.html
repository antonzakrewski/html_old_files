filtered colimit of rings

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{J}\) be a filtered category and \(F: \mathcal{J} \Rightarrow \mathrm{Ring}\) be a diagram. Then the direct limit of \(\mathcal{J}\) is isomorphic to

\begin{align*} \coprod R_i / \sim \end{align*}

where for \(r_i \in R_i, r_j \in R_j\) it holds

\begin{align*} r_i \sim r_j :\Leftrightarrow \exists k : \varphi_{i,k}(r_i) = \varphi_{j,k}(r_j) \end{align*}

and addition resp. multiplication is defined as

\begin{align*} [r_i] \cdot [r_j] =& [ \varphi_{i,k}(r_i) \cdot \varphi_{i,k}(r_j)] \\ [r_i] + [r_j] =& [ \varphi_{i,k}(r_i) + \varphi_{i,k}(r_j)] \\ \end{align*}

2.3. distributivity

Let \(r_i,r_j,r_l\), w.l.o.g. with \(k\) as upper bound for \(i,j,k\) (see: welldefinedsness), it follows that

\begin{align*} [r_i] \cdot \left( [r_j] + [r_l] \right) =& [r_i] \cdot \left([\varphi_{j,k}(r_j) + \varphi_{l,k}(r_l)]\right) \\ =& [\varphi_{i,k}(r_i) \cdot \left(\varphi_{j,k}(r_j) + \varphi_{l,k}(r_l)\right)] \\ =& [\varphi_{i,k}(r_i) \cdot \varphi_{j,k}(r_j) + \varphi_{i,k}(r_i) \cdot \varphi_{l,k}(r_l)] \\ =& [\varphi_{i,k}(r_i) \cdot \varphi_{j,k}(r_j)] + [ \varphi_{i,k}(r_i) \cdot \varphi_{l,k}(r_l)] \\ =& [\varphi_{i,k}(r_i)] \cdot [\varphi_{j,k}(r_j)] + [ \varphi_{i,k}(r_i)] \cdot [\varphi_{l,k}(r_l)] \\ =& [r_i] \cdot [r_j] + [r_i] \cdot [r_l] \end{align*}

and analog for the other side

2.4. universal property

Follows from

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} R_j \arrow[]{rdd}{\psi_{j}}\arrow[]{rd}{\phi_{j}} \arrow[]{rr}{\phi_{j,l}} & & R_l \arrow[]{ld}{\pi_{l}} \arrow[]{ld}{\psi_l}\\ & \coprod R_i / \sim \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists! \varphi_{S}}& \\ & S & \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

where

\begin{align*} \varphi_S: \coprod R_i / \sim \rightarrow& S \\ [a_j] \mapsto& \psi_j(a_j) \end{align*}
