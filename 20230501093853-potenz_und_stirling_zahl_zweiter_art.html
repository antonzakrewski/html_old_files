Potenz und Stirling Zahl zweiter Art

1. Satz

Sei \(n \in \mathbb{N}_0\) und \(x \in \mathbb{R}\) gegeben, dann gilt

\begin{align*} x^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k}} \end{align*}

2. Beweis

Beweis durch vollständige Induktion

2.1. IA

Es gilt für \(n = 0\):

\begin{align*} x^{\overline{0}} =& 1 \\ =& 1 \cdot (-1)^{0} x^{\overline{0}} =& \sum_{k=0}^{0} S_{0,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k}} \end{align*}

2.2. IS

Es gilt:

\begin{align*} x^{n+1} =& x^n \cdot x \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k}} \cdot x \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k}} \cdot (x + k - k) \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k}} \cdot (x + k) - k \sum_{k=0}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k}} \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k+1}} - k \sum_{k=1}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k} x^{\overline{k}} + 0 \cdot (-1)^{n-0} x^{\overline{0}} =& \sum_{k=1}^{n+1} S_{n,k-1} (-1)^{n-k-1} x^{\overline{k}} + k \sum_{k=1}^{n} S_{n,k} (-1)^{n-k-1} x^{\overline{k}} + 0\\ =& \sum_{k=1}^{n} (-1)^{n-k-1} \left(S_{n,k-1} + k \cdot S_{n,k} \right) x^{\overline{k}} + S_{n,n} (-1)^{n-n} x^{\overline{n+1}} \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^{(n+1)-k} S_{n+1,k} x^{\overline{k}} + S_{(n+1),(n+1)} (-1)^{(n+1)-(n+1)} x^{\overline{n+1}} \\ =& \sum_{k=0}^{n+1} (-1)^{(n+1)-k} S_{n+1,k} x^{\overline{k}} \end{align*}
