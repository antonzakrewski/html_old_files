digital line topology

1. Definition / Proposition

The digital line topology on \(\mathbb{Z}\) is defined by the basis \(\mathcal{B} := \{ \{n\} \vert n \equiv 1 \mod 2\} \sqcup \{ \{n-1,n,n+1\} \vert n \equiv 0 \mod{2}\}\)

2. Proof

2.1. a)

for \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\) either \(\{n\} \in \mathcal{B}\) or \(\{n-1,n,n+1\} \in \mathcal{B}\). Therefore \(\bicup_{B \in \mathcal{B}} B = \mathbb{Z}\)

2.2. b)

For w.l.o.g. \(n \neq n'\) and \(n,n' \in 2 \mathbb{Z}\) it holds

\begin{align*} \{n-1,n,n+1\} \cap \{n'-1,n',n'+1\} =& \begin{cases} \{n-1\} \in \mathcal{B} & \mbox{if } n-1 = n' + 1 \\ \{n+1\} \in \mathcal{B} & \mbox{if } n'-1 = n + 1 \\ \emptyset & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \\ \{n-1,n,n+1\} \cap \{n'+1\} =& \begin{cases} \{n'+1\} \in \mathcal{B} & \{n' + 1\} \in \{n-1,n,n+1\} \\ \emptyset \in \mathcal{B} & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}
