sheafification

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{V}\) a category and \(\mathcal{F}\) a V-valued presheaf. Then the sheafification is defined as sheaf \(\mathcal{F}^+\) and a morphism of sheafs \(\theta: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{F}^+\) satisfying following universal property: For each Sheaf \(G\) and morphism \(\varphi: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}\) there exists a unique morphism \(\psi: \mathcal{F}^{+} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}\) such that following diagram commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F} \arrow[]{rd}{\varphi}\arrow[]{r}{\theta}& \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists! \psi} \mathcal{F}^+ \\ & \mathcal{G} \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. uniqueness

Follows from

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F} \arrow[]{r}{\theta} \arrow[]{rd}{\theta'} \arrow[]{rdd}{\theta} & \mathcal{F}^+ \arrow[bend left]{dd}{\mathrm{id} } \arrow[dashed, swap]{d}{\exists! \psi}\\ & \mathcal{G} \arrow[swap, dashed]{d}{\exists! \psi'} \\ & \mathcal{F}^+ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
