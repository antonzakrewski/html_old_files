finite product of finitely generated ideals finitely generated

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a}_1,...,\mathfrak{a}_n \subseteq A\) finitely generated ideals. Then the product of ideals

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a} \coloneqq \mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2 \cdot ... \cdot \mathfrak{a}_n \end{align*}

is also finitely generated

2. Proof

Let \(a^i_1,...,a^i_m \in A\) be generators, i.e. \((a^i_1,...,a^i_m) = \mathfrak{a}_^i\). Consider \(a^i_j\) for \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\) and \(j \in \{1,...,m\}\)

Then

\begin{align*} (a_j^i) = \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

2.1. \((a_j^i) \supseteq \mathfrak{a}\)

Let \(a \in \mathfrak{a}\), then there exist \(b_i \in \mathfrak{a}_i\) for \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\) such that

\begin{align*} a = \prod_{i=1}^n b_i \end{align*}

Furthermore, by assumption each \(b_i\) is generated by \(a^i_1,...,a^i_m\) By applying distributivity, we get a sum

\begin{align*} a = \sum_{i,j} \alpha_{i,j} a_j^i \end{align*}

(sloppy)

2.2. \((a_j^i) \subseteq \mathfrak{a}\)

By construction \(a_{j_1}^1 \cdot ... \cdot a_{j_n}^n \in \mathfrak{a}\)
