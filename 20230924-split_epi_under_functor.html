split epi under functors

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(f \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(A,B)\) be a split epi and \(\mathcal{F}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a covariant functor. Then \(\mathcal{F}(f)\) is also split epi

2. Proof

By assumption, there exists an \(g \in \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathcal{C}}(B,A)\) such that

\begin{align*} f \circ g = \mathrm{id}_{B} \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{align*} \mathrm{id}_{B} =& \mathcal{F}(\mathrm{id}_{B}) \\ =& \mathcal{F}(f \circ g) \\ =& \mathcal{F}(f) \circ \mathcal{F}(g) \end{align*}

Therefore, \(\mathcal{F}(f)\) is split epi
