radical of an ideal as intersection of prime ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{a}\) an ideal. Then the radical of an ideal

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} = \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p \in \mathrm{Spec} \vert \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{p}} \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}

2. Proof

radical of an ideal as preimage of the projection to the quotient ring

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}} =& \pi^{-1}[\mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{a}}] \end{align*}

Nilradical as intersection of prime ideals

\begin{align*} \pi^{-1}[\mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{a}}] =& \pi^{-1}[\bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A/\mathfrak{a})} \mathfrak{p}] \end{align*}

Urbild des Durchschnitts

\begin{align*} \pi^{-1}[\bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A/\mathfrak{a})} \mathfrak{p}] =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A/\mathfrak{a})} \pi^{-1}[\mathfrak{p}] \\ \end{align*}

at last we get ideal of a quotient ring and ideal containing the quotient, Preimage of a prime ideal as prime ideal

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A/\mathfrak{a})} \pi^{-1}[\mathfrak{p}] =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A), \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \mathfrak{p}} \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}
