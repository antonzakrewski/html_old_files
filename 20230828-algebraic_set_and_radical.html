algebraic set and radical ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space. Then for a subset \(S \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) it holds

\begin{align*} V(S) =& V((S)) \\ =& V(\sqrt{(S)}) \end{align*}

see: algebraic set

2. Proof

2.1. \(V(S) \subseteq V((S))\)

Let \((p_1,...,p_n) \in V(S)\), then for any \(f \in (S)\) we conclude, that there exist \(f_1,...,f_k \in S\) and \(r_i \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) such that \(f = \sum_{i=1}^{k} r_i f_i\) Thus

\begin{align*} f(p_1,...,p_n) =& \sum_{i=1}^{k} r_i \cdot f_i(p_1,...,p_n) \\ =& \sum_{i=1}^{k} r_i \cdot 0 \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

2.2. \(V((S)) \subseteq V(\sqrt{(S)})\)

Suppose \((p_1,...,p_n) \in V((S))\), then for any \(f \in \sqrt{(S)}\) there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) and \(g \in (S)\) such that \(f^n = g\). Therefore we conclude

\begin{align*} f^n(p_1,...,p_n) =& g(p_1,...,p_n) \\ f^n(p_1,...,p_n) =& 0 \end{align*}

and since \(K[X_1,...,X_n]\) is an Integral domain (cf. polynomial ring over an integral domain as integral domain), we conclude by induction, that \(f(p_1,...,p_n) = 0\)

2.3. \(V(\sqrt{(S)}) \subseteq V(S)\)
