Topology induced by an uniform structure

1. Definition / proposition

Let \((X, \Phi)\) be a uniform space, then the induced topology by the uniform structure is defined as:

\begin{align*} \mathcal{T} := \{O \subseteq X \vert \forall x \in O \exists V_x \in \Phi : V_x[x] \subseteq O\} \end{align*}

see: Set of Relatives

2. Proof

2.1. trivial sets in a topology

\(\emptyset \in \mathcal{T}\) as \(V[\emptyset] = \emptyset\); \(X \in \mathcal{T}\) as \(V[X] = X\)

2.2. Openness of a union of open sets

Let \(O_i\) be a family of open sets. Then we conclude, that for every \(x \in \bigcup_{i \in I} O_i\) there exists a \(i \in I\) such that \(x \in O_i\) and an entourage \(V_x\) such that \(V_x[x] \subseteq O_i \subseteq \bigcup_{i \in I} O_i\)

2.3. Openness of a finite intersection of open sets

Let \(O_1,O_2\) be open sets and \(x \in O_1 \cap O_2\). Then there exist entourages \(V_{1}, V_{2}\), such that \(V_{1}[x] \subseteq O_1\) and \(V_{2}[x] \subseteq O_2\). By assumption, there exists an entourage \(V := V_1 \cap V_2\) and it follows:

\begin{align*} V[x] \subseteq O_1 \cap O_2 \end{align*}

Therefore we conclude, that \(O_1 \cap O_2\) is an open set

Openness of any finite intersection follows from induction
