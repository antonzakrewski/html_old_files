union of a chain of monoid-homomorphisms as monoid-homomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(N\) be a monoid, \(M_i\) be a chain rings with ring-homomorphisms \(\tau_{i}: M_i \rightarrow N\) such that

\begin{align*} \tau_{i \vert M_i \cap M_j} = \tau_{j \vert M_i \cap M_j} \end{align*}

Then

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in I} \tau_i: \bigcup_{i \in I} M_i \rightarrow N \end{align*}

is a welldefined ring-homomorphism

2. Proof

\(\bigcup_{i \in I} M_i\) is a welldefined monoid (cf. union of a chain of monoids as monoid) Furthermore, since \(\tau_i, \tau_j\) coincide, wherevere possible

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i \in I} \tau_i: x \mapsto \tau_i(x) && \mbox{if } x \in \tau_i \\ \end{align*}

is welldefined.

2.1. additivie

Let \(m, n \in \bigcup_{i \in I} M_i\). Then there exists an \(M_{\mathrm{max}}\) with \(m,n \in M_{\mathrm{max}}\). Hence

\begin{align*} \tau(m + n) = \tau(m) + \tau(n) \end{align*}
