abelian quotientgroup and commutator subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(G^{(1)}\) the commutator subgroup and \(N \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup TFAE:

  1. \(G^{(1)} \subseteq N\)
  2. \(G/N\) is abelian

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(g_1, g_2 \in G\), then \([g_1g_2] \in N\)

\begin{align*} g_1N \cdot g_2N =& g_1g_2 N \\ =& g_1 g_2 [g_1g_2]N \\ =& g_1 g_2 [g_2^{-1}g_1^{-1}] N \\ =& g_1 g_2 (g_2^{-1}g_1^{-1}g_2g_1) N \\ =& g_2g_1 N \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(g_1,g_2 \in G\), then

\begin{align*} g_1 g_2 N =& g_2 g_1 N \\ \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} g_1 g_2 N =& g_2 g_1 N && \vert g_2^{-1}g_1^{-1} \cdot \\ N =& g_2^{-1}g_1^{-1} g_2 g_1 N \end{align*}

or equivalently

\begin{align*} g_2^{-1}g_1^{-1} g_2 g_1 \in N \\ [g_2^{-1}g_1^{-1}] \in N \end{align*}

Since \(g_1g_2\) are arbitrary, we conclude

\begin{align*} G^{(1)} \subseteq N \end{align*}
