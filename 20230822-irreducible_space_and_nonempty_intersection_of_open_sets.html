irreducible space and dense open set

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space.

TFAE:

  1. \(X\) is an irreducible space
  2. for nonempty open sets \(O_1,O_2 \subseteq X\), we conclude \(O_1 \cap O_2 \neq \emptyset\)
  3. any nonempty open set \(O_1\) is dense in \(X\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose there exist nonempty open sets \(O_1,O_2 \subseteq X\) with \(O_1 \cap O_2 = \emptyset\). Then we get \(X \setminus O_1, X \setminus O_2 \neq X\) and furthermore by de morgan

\begin{align*} X =& X \setminus \emptyset \\ =& X \setminus (O_1 \cap O_2) \\ =& X \setminus O_1 \cup X \setminus O_2 \end{align*}

where \(X \setminus O_1, X \setminus O_2\) are closed Thus \(X\) is reducible

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3), 3) \(\implies\) 2)

equivalent definitions

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(X\) is reducible, then there exist closed sets \(A_1,A_2 \subsetneq X\) with

\begin{align*} A_1 \cup A_2 = X \end{align*}

Therefore, by de morgan we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \emptyset =& X \setminus X \\ =& X \setminus (A_1 \cup A_2) \\ =& X \setminus A_1 \cap X \setminus A_2 \\ \end{align*}

where \(O_1 \coloneqq X \setminus A_1\) and \(O_2 \coloneqq X \setminus A_2\) are disjoint. Thus \(O_2 \not\subseteq \overline{O_1}\) and hence \(\overline{O_1} \neq X\)
