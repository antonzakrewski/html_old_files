injective map from points of an affine space to maximal ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(S \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) a subset of polynomials and \(\mathcal{M}_S\) the set of maximal ideals containing \(S\) Then the map

\begin{align*} \iota: V(S) \rightarrow& \mathcal{M}_S \\ (a_1,...,a_n) \mapsto& (X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n) \end{align*}

is a welldefined injection

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

Let \(\varphi_a: K[X_1,...,X_n] \rightarrow K\) the evaluation homomorphism. then \(\mathrm{im}(\varphi_a) = K\) and \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi_a) = (X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n)\), hence by <Homomorphiesatz f√ºr Ringe> we get

\begin{align*} K \simeq K[X_1,...,X_n] /(X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n) \end{align*}

Hence since maximal ideals of a commutative rings are characterized by the quotient ring as field, \((X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n)\) is a maximal ideal

2.2. injection

Let \(a = (a_1,...,a_n), b = (b_1,...,b_n) \in \mathbb{A}_K^n\) such that \(\iota(a) = \iota(b)\) Then \(X_i - a_i \in \iota(a), X_i - b_i \in \iota(b)\) and hence

\begin{align*} a_i - b_i \in& \iota(a) \\ \Rightarrow a_i - b_i =& 0 \end{align*}

(see: unit and trivial ideal) Therefore \(a_i = b_i\) and hence \(a = b\)
