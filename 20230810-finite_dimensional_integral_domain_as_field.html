finite dimensional integral domain as field

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(R\) an finite dimensional K-Algebra, such that \(R\) as a Ring is an Integral domain. Then \(K\) is a field

2. Proof

Let \(r \in R \setminus \{0\}\), then the map

\begin{align*} \varphi_r: R \rightarrow& R \\ m \mapsto& m \cdot r \end{align*}

is an monomorphism (see: multiplication as monomorphism and integral domain) Furthermore, it is also an vector space endomorphism and hence also an isomorphism (cf. Äquivalenz von Isomorphismus, Monomorphismus und Epimorphismus für Vektorräume gleicher, endlicher Dimension) Thus by the characterization of multiplication as isomorphism and group, we get, that \(R \setminus \{0\}\) is a multiplicative group, hence \(R\) is a field
