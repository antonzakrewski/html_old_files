multiplication as isomorphism and group

1. Proposition

Let \((M,\cdot)\) be a Monoid.

TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is a group
  2. for \(s \in M\) the map \(\varphi_s: m \mapsto m \cdot s\) is an isomorphsim
  3. for \(s \in M\) the map \(\varphi_s: m \mapsto s \cdot m\) is an isomorphsim

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.1.1. surjective

Let \(g \in G\), then

\begin{align*} \varphi_s(g \cdot s^{-1}) =& g \cdot s^{-1} \cdot s \\ =& g \end{align*}

2.1.2. injective

consequence of the uniqueness of an inverse and minimal kernel as monomorphism

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(s \in M\) and \(\varphi_s\) the map then by assumption of \(\varphi\) as epimorphism there exists an \(s^{-1} \in M\) such that

\begin{align*} \varphi_s(s^{-1}) = e \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} s^{-1} \cdot s = e \end{align*}

2.3. 1) \(\Leftrightarrow\) 3)

proven analogue
