localization along the unit group isomorphic to ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a Ring and \(S \coloneqq R^*\) the unit group. Then it follows for the localization

\begin{align*} S^{-1} R \simeq R \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \varphi: R \rightarrow& S^{-1}R \\ r \mapsto& \frac{r}{1} \end{align*}

the canonical map

2.1. injective

minimal kernel and monomorphism Suppose \(r \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\) Since \(s \in S\) is a unit and hence not a zero divisor, we get

\begin{align*} s \cdot (1 \cdot r - 1 \cdot 0) =& s \cdot r \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

or equivalently \(r = 0\).

2.2. surjective

Let \(\frac{r}{s} \in S^{-1}R\) for an \(s \in S\). Then there exists an \(s^{-1}\) and hence

\begin{align*} \varphi(r \cdot s^{-1}) =& \frac{r \cdot s^{-1}}{1} \\ =& \frac{r}{s} \end{align*}

2.3. homomorphism

follows from the construction of \(S^{-1}R\)
