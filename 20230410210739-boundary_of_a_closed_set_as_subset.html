boundary of a closed set as subset

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(A \subseteq X\) a closed set. Then following holds true for the boundary:

\begin{align*} \partial A \subseteq A \end{align*}

2. Proof

Since \(A\) is closed, it follows \(\overline{A} = A\) (see Closure of a closed set) Therefore follows \(\partial A = \overline{A} \setminus A^{\circ} \subseteq \overline{A}\) from Boundary as Closure without interior
