group action as functor

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(\triangle: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a group action. Then \(\triangle\) is canonically a functor

\begin{align*} \triangle: B(G) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Set} \\ G \mapsto& \mathrm{Hom}_{\mathrm{Set}}(X,X) \\ g \mapsto& (x \mapsto g \cdot x) \end{align*}

where \(B(G)\) is the group as single-object category
