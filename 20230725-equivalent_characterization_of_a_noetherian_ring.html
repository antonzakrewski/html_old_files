equivalent characterization of a noetherian ring

1. list

  1. \(A\) is noetherian, as in each ideal is finitely generated
  2. noetherian ring and stabilizing of an ascending chain
  3. noetherian ring as noetherian module
  4. noetherian ring and existence of a maximal ideal for any set of ideals
  5. noetherian ring and finite generation of prime ideals

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \((\mathcal{a}_n)_{n \in \mathbb{N}}\) be a chain, then

\begin{align*} \mathcal{a} \coloneqq \bigcup_{n \in \mathbb{N}} \mathcal{a}_n \end{align*}

is an ideal and by assumption, there exist finitely many \(a_1,...,a_k \in \mathcal{a}\), such that

\begin{align*} (a_1,...,a_n) = \mathcal{a} \end{align*}

For each \(a_i\), there exists an \(\mathcal{a}_{j}\) such that \(a_i \in \mathcal{a}_j\) Suppose \(m \coloneqq \mathrm{max}(j)\), then we conclude

\begin{align*} \mathcal{a} = (a_1,...,a_n) \subseteq \mathcal{a}_m \subseteq \mathcal{a} \end{align*}

and therefore

\begin{align*} \mathcal{a} = \mathcal{a}_m \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

Suppose there exists a nonempty set \(S\) without a maximal element, then we can construct an infinite chain

\begin{align*} \mathcal{a}_1 \subsetneq \mathcal{a}_2 ... \end{align*}

with \(\mathcal{a}_i \subseteq S\). By assumption, there exists an \(\mathcal{a}_m\) such that \(\mathcal{a}_n \subseteq \mathcal{a}_m\) for \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), contradiction

Alternitavely, it's a consequence of Zorn's lemma

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)
