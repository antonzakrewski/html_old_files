Artinian ring and finite amount of maximal ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be an Artinian ring, then \(\mathrm{max}(R)\) is finite

2. Proof

Suppose there exist infinitely many maximal ideals \(\mathfrak{m}_i\). Let \(\mathfrak{m}_n\) for \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\) be countably, pairwise different many of those. Then

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m}_1 \supseteq \mathfrak{m}_1 \cap \mathfrak{m}_2 \supseteq ... \end{align*}

is a Chain.

Furthermore, \(\bigcap_{i=1}^n \mathfrak{m}_i \neq \{0\}\) (see: finite intersection of maximal ideals as zero ideal and finite amount of maximal ideals) and \(\mathfrak{m}_i \not\subseteq \mathfrak{m}_j\) for \(i \neq j\), we get a chain, that doesn't stabilize. Contradiction
