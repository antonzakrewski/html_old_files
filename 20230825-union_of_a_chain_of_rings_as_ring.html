union of a chain of rings as ring

1. Proposition

Let $R1 \subseteq R2 …$ be a chain of rings Then \(R \coloneqq \bigcup_{n \in \mathbb{N}} R_i\) is also a ring.

2. Proof

2.1. additive abelian group

cf. union of a chain of abelian groups as abelian group

2.2. multiplicative monoid

cf. union of a chain of monoids as monoid

2.3. distributivity

Let \(r_1,r_2,r_3 \in R\), then by assumption there exist \(R_m,R_n,R_l\) with \(r_1 \in R_m, r_2 \in R_n, r_3 \in R_l\). Thus \(r_1,r_2,r_3 \in R_{\mathrm{max}(m,n,l)}\) and hence

\begin{align*} r_1 \cdot (r_2 + r_3) =& r_1 \cdot r_2 + r_1 \cdot r_3 \\ (r_2 + r_3) \cdot r_1 =& r_2 \cdot r_1 + r_3 \cdot r_1 \end{align*}
