integral closure

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(A \subseteq B\) be a commutative rings. Then the integral closure of \(A\) in \(B\) is defined as subring

\begin{align*} \overline{A} \coloneqq \{\alpha \in B \vert \alpha \text{ integral over } A\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(\alpha, \beta \in B\), then by <subring for finitely many integral elements as finitely generated module> \(A[\alpha,\beta]\) is a finitely generated module carrying a ring structure and by <integral element and finitely generated subring> it follows, that \(\alpha + \beta, \alpha \cdot \beta \in A[\alpha,\beta]\) Thus \(\overline{A}\) is closed under operations
