finite intersection of maximal ideals as zero ideal and finite amount of maximal ideals

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathfrak{m}_1,...,\mathfrak{m}_n\) pairwise different maximal ideals such that

\begin{align*} \bigcap_{i=1}^n \mathfrak{m}_n = \{0\} \end{align*}

Then \(\mathfrak{m}_1,...,\mathfrak{m}_n\) are the only maximal ideal

2. Proof

Suppose there exists another, different maximal ideal \(\mathfrak{m}\). Then by the general chinese remainder theorem

\begin{align*} \varphi: R/0 \rightarrow& \bigoplus_{i=1}^n R/\mathfrak{m} r \mapsto& (r + \mathfrak{m}_i) \psi': R/0 \rightarrow& \bigoplus_{i=1}^n R/\mathfrak{m}_i \oplus R/\mathfrak{m} r \mapsto& ((r + \mathfrak{m}_i), r + \mathfrak{m}) \end{align*}

are isomorphisms. But \(\vert R/\mathfrak{m} \vert \geq 2\), so it follows, that

\begin{align*} \varphi[\psi((0 + \mathfrak{m}_i), r + \mathfrak{m})] =& \mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \\ \vert \varphi[\psi((0 + \mathfrak{m}_i), r + \mathfrak{m})] \vert \geq& 2 \end{align*}
