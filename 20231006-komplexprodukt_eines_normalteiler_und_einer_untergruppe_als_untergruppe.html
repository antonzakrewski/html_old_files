product of a normal subgroup and subgroup as subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(N \trianglelefteq G\) a normal subgroup and \(H \subseteq G\) a subgroup. Then the product \(HN \subseteq G\) is a subgroup

2. Proof

\begin{align*} h_1n_1 \cdot (h_2 n_2)^{-1} =& h_1 n_1 n_2^{-1} h_2^{-1} \\ =& h_1 \left( h_2^{-1} h_2 \right) n_1 \left( h_2^{-1}h_2 \right) n_2^{-1}h_2^{-1} \\ =& \left(h_1 h_2^{-1} \right) \cdot \left(h_2 n_1 h_2^{-1} \right) \cdot \left(h_2 n_2^{-1} h_2^{-1} \right) \end{align*}

Since \(N \trianglelefteq H\), we conclude that \(\left(h_2 n_1 h_2^{-1} \right), \left(h_2 n_2^{-1} h_2^{-1} \right) \in N\) and thus

\begin{align*} \left(h_1 h_2^{-1}\right) \in& H \\ \left(h_2 n_1 h_2^{-1} \right) \cdot \left(h_2 n_2^{-1} h_2^{-1} \right) \in& N \end{align*}
