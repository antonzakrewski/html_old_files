complex projective space as compact space

1. Proposition

The complex projective space \(\mathbb{C} \mathbb{P}^n\) is a compact topological space

2. Proof

Let \(\mathbb{C}S^n \subseteq \mathbb{C}^{n+1}\) be the complex n-sphere it is closed and bounded, hence compact (see: komplexer Raum als eigentlicher Raum) Note, that it is also compact in the subspace topology \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}\), as every open covering of \(\mathbb{C}S^n\) in \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}\) is an open covering in \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\), since \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\}\) is open in \(\mathbb{C}^{n+1}\) (see: Ãœbertragung der Offenheit von der Unterraumtopologie

The map

\begin{align*} \pi: \mathbb{C}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\} \rightarrow& \mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n (x_0,...,x_n) \mapsto& [x_0 : ... : x_n] \end{align*}

is by definition of \(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n\) as quotient topology continuous. Furthermore,

\begin{align*} \pi[\mathbb{C} S^n] = \mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n \end{align*}

thus by continuous image of compact space as compact space, it follows, that \(\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n\) is compact

Let \([x_0 : ... x_n] \in \mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n\), then \(\lambda \coloneqq \sqrt{\sum_{i=0}^{n} \vert x_0 \vert^2} \neq 0\) since \((x_0,...,x_n) \neq 0\). Therefore

\begin{align*} [x_0 : ... : x_n] = \frac{1}{\lambda} \cdot [x_0 : ... : x_n] \end{align*}

and furthermore by construction

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{\lambda} (x_0,...,x_n) \in \mathbb{C} S^n \end{align*}

Hence \([x_0 : ... : x_n] \in \pi[\mathbb{C} S^n]\) and since \([x_0 : ... : x_n]\) was arbitrary.
