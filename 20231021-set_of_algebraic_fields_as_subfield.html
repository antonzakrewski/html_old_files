set of algebraic elements as subfield

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(L/K\) a field extension. Then the set of algebraic elements is a subfield

2. Proof

\(K\) is algebraic because of \((x - \alpha)\) for \(\alpha \in K\). Hence \(0,1 \in K\).

2.1. closed under operations

integral elements closed under addition and multiplication

2.2. additive inverse

Let \(\alpha \in L\) and the corresponding minimal polynomial be \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \beta_i X^i\). Thus

\begin{align*} f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \beta_i (-1)^i X^i \end{align*}

has \(-\alpha\) as root, thus \(-\alpha\) is also algebraic

2.3. multiplicative

Let \(\alpha \in L\) and the corresponding minimal polynomial be \(f = \sum_{i=0}^{n} \beta_i X^i\). w.l.o.g., \(\beta_0 \neq 0\) because otherwise \(f\) wouldn't be irreducible, as

\begin{align*} f' = x \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \beta_i X^{i-1} \end{align*}

Thus we conclude that

\begin{align*} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \beta_i \alpha^i =& -\beta_0 \\ \alpha \cdot \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_{i+1} \alpha^i =& - \beta_0 \\ \alpha \cdot (-\frac{1}{\beta_0} \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_{i+1} \alpha^i) =& 1 \end{align*}

and hence \(\alpha\) is a unit, because \((-\frac{1}{\beta_0} \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_{i+1} \alpha^i)\) is induced as multiplication and addition of integral elements
