zero ideal as prime ideal and integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(0\) the zero ideal. TFAE:

  1. \(0\) is a prime ideal
  2. \(R\) is an Integral domain

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose \(a \cdot b = 0\), then by assumption of \(0\) being prime, we conclude w.l.o.g. \(a \in 0 \Leftrightarrow a = 0\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(a \cdot b = 0\), then we conclude w.l.o.g. \(a = 0\) and hence \(0\) is a prime ideal
