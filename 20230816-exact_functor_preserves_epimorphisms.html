exact functor preserves epimorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(F: \mathrm{Ab} \rightarrow \mathrm{Ab}\) be an exact functor such that \(F(0) = 0\) and \(\varphi: G \rightarrow H\) an epimorphism for groups \(G,H\) Then \(F(\varphi)\) is also an epimorphism

2. Proof

Consider the sequnce

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & G \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & H \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

It is exact at \(H\) (cf. exact sequence and monomorphism), hence by assumption

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} F(G) \arrow[]{r}{F(\varphi)} & F(H) \arrow[]{r}{} & F(0) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact at \(F(H)\), thus \(F(\varphi)\) is an epimorphism
