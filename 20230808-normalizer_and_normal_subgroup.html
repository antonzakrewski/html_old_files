normalizer and normal subgroup

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(U\) a subgroup. Then the Normalizer \(N_G(U)\) is the biggest subgroup, such that \(U\) is a normal subgroup of \(N_G(U)\)

2. Proof

Let \(N \subseteq G\) be a group, such that \(U \trianglelefteq N\) and \(n \in N\). Then for any \(u \in U\) it holds, that

\begin{align*} n \cdot u \cdot n^{-1} \in U \end{align*}

and by construction, we conclude, that

\begin{align*} n \in N_G(U) \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} N \subseteq N_G(U) \end{align*}
