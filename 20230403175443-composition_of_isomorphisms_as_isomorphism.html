composition of isomorphisms as isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(f: A \rightarrow B\) and \(g: B \rightarrow C\) isomorphisms for \(A,B,C \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) Then \(g \circ f\) is a Isomorphism with inverse morphism:

\begin{align*} (g \circ f)^{-1} = f^{-1} \circ g^{-1} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Using associaciativity of the composition and uniqueness of a two-sided inverse morphism we conclude:

\begin{align*} (g \circ f) \circ (f^{-1} \circ g^{-1}) =& g \circ (f \circ f^{-1}) g^{-1} \\ =& g \circ \mathrm{id}_{B} \circ g^{-1} \\ =& g \circ g^{-1} \\ =& \mathrm{id}_{C} \\ (f^{-1} \circ g^{-1}) \circ (g \circ f) =& f^{-1} \circ g^{-1} \circ g \circ f \\ =& f^{-1} \circ \mathrm{id}_{B} \circ f \\ =& f^{-1} \circ f \\ =& \mathrm{id}_{A} \end{align*}
