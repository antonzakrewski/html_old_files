noetherian space of a spectrum in general not a noetherian ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) the zariski spectrum with the Zariski topology, such that \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) is a Noetherian topological space. Then \(A\) is not in general noetherian

2. Proof

Let \(K\) be a field, \(K[X_1,X_2,...]\) the polynomial ring in countably many variables. Suppose \(A \coloneqq K[X_1,X_2,...]/(X_1^2,X_2^2,...)\). Then \(I \coloneqq (X_1,X_2,...)\) is not finitely generated, so \(A\) is not noetherian. Nonetheless, \(I\) is the only prime ideal, hence \(\mathrm{Spec}(A) = \{I\}\).
