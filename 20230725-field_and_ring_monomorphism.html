field and ring monomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(R \neq \{0\}\) be a commutative ring. TFAE:

  1. \(R\) is a field
  2. for a ring \(S \neq \{0\}\) and a homomorphism \(\varphi: R \rightarrow S\), \(\varphi\) is a monomorphism

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(r \in R \setminus 0\), then the canonical projection

\begin{align*} \pi_{(r)}: R \rightarrow R/(r) \end{align*}

is an monomorphism. Since \(\pi_{(r)}(r) = r + (r) = 0 = \pi_{(r)}(0)\), we conclude that \(R/(r) = \{0\}\). Therefore, we conclude, that \(r\) is a unit (see: von einer Einheit erzeugtes Ideal und triviales Ideal)
