universal property of a localization

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(S^{-1}R\) a localization along \(S \subseteq R\), \(\varphi: R \rightarrow S^{-1}R\) the canonical ring homomorphism for a localization and

\begin{align*} \psi: R \rightarrow R' \end{align*}

a ringhomomorphism, such that \(\psi(S) \subseteq (R')^{\times}\) Then there exists a unique homomorphism \(f: S^{-1}R \rightarrow R'\) such that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} R \arrow[]{rd}{\psi} \arrow[]{r}{\varphi} & S^{-1}R \arrow[]{d}{\exists! f}\\ & R' \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
