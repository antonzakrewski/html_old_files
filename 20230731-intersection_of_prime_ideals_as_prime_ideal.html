intersection of a chain of prime ideals as prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{p}_n \in \mathbb{N}\) a chain of prime ideals. Then

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} = \bigcap_{i \in I} \mathfrak{p}_i \end{align*}

is also a prime ideal

2. Proof

2.1. ideal

Intersection of ideals

2.2. prime

Let \(s,t \in A\) such that \(s \cdot t \in \mathfrak{p}\). w.l.o.g. \(s \not\in \mathfrak{p}\), so there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(s \not\in \mathfrak{p}_{n'}\) for \(n' \geq n\). since \(\mathfrak{p}_{n'}\) is prime, we conclude, that \(t \in \mathfrak{p}\) and since \(\mathfrak{p}_j \supseteq \mathfrak{p}_{n'}\) for \(j \leq n'\), we conclude, that

\begin{align*} t \in& \mathfrak{p}_n \forall n \in \mathbb{N} \\ t \in& \mathfrak{p} \end{align*}
