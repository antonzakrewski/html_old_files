Existence of a pullback for an isomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category, \(S,X,Y \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) objects and

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} & X \arrow[]{r}{f}\\ S \arrow[]{r}{g}& Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

a span. Then the pullback exists and is

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} S \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}_{S}}\arrow[]{r}{f^{-1} \circ g} & X \arrow[]{r}{f}\\ S \arrow[]{r}{g} & Y \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. commuting

follows from

\begin{align*} g \circ \mathrm{id}_{S} =& g \\ =& \mathrm{id}_{Y} \circ g \\ =& f \circ f^{-1} \circ g \end{align*}

2.2. universal property

Suppose there exists an object and a morphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[bend left]{rrd}{\beta} \arrow[bend right]{rdd}{\alpha}& & \\ & S \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}_{S}} \arrow[]{r}{f^{-1} \circ g} & X \arrow[]{d}{f} \\ & S \arrow[]{r}{g} & Y \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then for

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} P \arrow[dashed]{rd}{\alpha} \arrow[bend left]{rrd}{\beta} \arrow[bend right]{rdd}{\alpha}& & \\ & S \arrow[]{d}{\mathrm{id}_{S}} \arrow[]{r}{f^{-1} \circ g} & X \arrow[]{d}{f} \\ & S \arrow[]{r}{g} & Y \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

it follows, that

\begin{align*} g \circ \alpha = f \circ f^{-1} \circ g \circ \alpha \end{align*}

2.2.1. uniqueness (?)

By Assumption, there exists a right-inverse \(f^{-1}\). Therefore, by commutativity we get

\begin{align*} g \circ \alpha =& f \circ \beta \\ (f^{-1} \circ g) \circ \alpha =& \beta \end{align*}
