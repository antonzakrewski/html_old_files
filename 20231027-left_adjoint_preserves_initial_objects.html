left adjoint preserves initial objects

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}, \mathcal{D}\) be categories, \(\mathcal{L}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathcal{D}\) a left adjoint. Then \(\mathcal{L}\) preservers initial object

2. Proof

corollary of

  • colimit of the empty diagram as initial object
  • left adjoint preserves colimits
