automorphism group acting on the set

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a concrete category, \(O \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) an object and \(\mathrm{Aut}(O)\) the automorphism group. Let \(\mathcal{Set}: \mathcal{C} \rightarrow \mathrm{Set}\) be the underlying-set functor, then \(\mathrm{Aut}(O)\) acts canonically on \(\mathrm{Set}(O)\) by

\begin{align*} \triangle: \mathrm{Aut}(O) \times \mathrm{Set}(O) \rightarrow& \mathrm{Set}(O) \\ \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. identity

follows from the definition of a functor

2.2. associativity

follows from associativity of the composition of morphisms
