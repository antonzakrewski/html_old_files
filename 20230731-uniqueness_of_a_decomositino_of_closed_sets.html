uniqueness of a decomosition of closed sets

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space and \(Y \subseteq X\) nonempty and closed. Then a decomposition into irreducible components is unique.

2. Proof

Suppose there exist decompositions

\begin{align*} Y =& \bigcup_{i = 1}^n Y_i \\ =& \bigcup_{i= 1}^m X_i \end{align*}

w.l.o.g. we may assume, that \(Y_i \not\subseteq Y_j, X_k \not\subseteq X_l\) for \(i \neq j, k \neq l\). Then \(X_1 = \bigcup_{i=1}^n (X_1 \cap Y_i)\) so the irreducibility of \(X_1\) implies after renaming \(Y_i\) \(X_1 \subseteq Y_1\) Likewise for \(Y_1 \subseteq X_k\), hence \(Y_1 = X_k \eqqcolon X_k\).

Furthermore, \(\bigcup_{i=2}^n Y_i \supseteq \overline{Y \setminus Y_1}\) and by induction, we conclude that \(Y_i = X_i\).
