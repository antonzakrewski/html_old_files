uniqueness of a decomosition of closed sets

1. Proposition

Let \(X\) be a topological space and \(Y \subseteq X\) nonempty and closed. Then a decomposition into irreducible components is unique up to order and multiplicity

2. Proof

Suppose there exist decompositions

\begin{align*} Y =& \bigcup_{i = 1}^n Y_i \\ =& \bigcup_{i= 1}^m X_i \end{align*}

w.l.o.g. we may assume, that \(Y_i \not\subseteq Y_j, X_k \not\subseteq X_l\) for \(i \neq j, k \neq l\). Then \(X_1 = \bigcup_{i=1}^n (X_1 \cap Y_i)\) so the irreducibility of \(X_1\) implies after renaming \(Y_i\), that \(X_1 \subseteq Y_1\).

Thus we conclude

\begin{align*} (\bigcup_{i=1}^n Y_i) \setminus X_1 =& (\bigcup_{i=1}^n Y_i \setminus X_1) \subseteq& \bigcup_{i=2}^n Y_i \\ (\bigcup_{i=1}^n X_i) \setminus X_1 =& (\bigcup_{i=1}^n X_i \setminus X_1)\subseteq& \bigcup_{i=2}^n X_i \end{align*}

Furthermore, by closure of a subset, we conclude that

\begin{align*} \overline{(\bigcup_{i=1}^n Y_i \setminus X_i)} \subseteq& \overline{\bigcup_{i=2}^n X_i} \end{align*}

By Abschluss von einer endlichen Vereinigung we get

\begin{align*} \overline{(\bigcup_{i=1}^n Y_i \setminus X_i)} =& \bigcup_{i=1}^n \overline{(Y_i \setminus X_i)} \subseteq \bigcup_{i=2}^{n} Y_i \end{align*}

where \(Y_i \setminus X_i\) is open in the subspace topology of \(Y_i\) and hence by denseness of an open set in an irreducible space, we conclude that

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i=1}^n \overline{(Y_i \setminus X_1} =& \emptyset \cup \bigcup_{i=2}^n \overline{Y_i \setminus X_1} \\ =& \bigcup_{i=2}^n Y_i \end{align*}

and analoguosly

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i=1}^n \overline{X_i \setminus X_1} =& \bigcup_{i=2}^n X_i \end{align*}

Thus it follows, that

\begin{align*} \bigcup_{i=2}^n Y_i = \overline{\bigcup_{i=1}^n Y_i \setminus X_1} \\ =& \overline{\bigcup_{i=1}^n X_i \setminus X_1 } \\ =& \bigcup_{i=2}^n X_i \end{align*}

Hence by induction, we conclude that \(Y_i \subseteq X_i\) for suitable \(i \in \{1,...,n\}\). Symmetry of the argument also provides us with \(X_i \subseteq Y_j\) for suitable \(i,j\). Thus by assumption of maximality of an irreducible component, it follows, that \(X_i = X_j\) or \(X_i = Y_i\)
