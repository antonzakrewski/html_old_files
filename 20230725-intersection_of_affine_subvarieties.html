intersection of algebraic sets

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space and \(V(f_1,...,f_k)\) an algebraic set for polynomials \(f_1,...,f_k\). Then

\begin{align*} V((f_1,...,f_k)) =& \bigcap_{i = 1}^k V(f_i) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. \(\subseteq\)

Let \(a \in \mathbb{A}_K^n\) such that \(a \in V((f_1,...,f_k))\), then for each polynomial \(f \in V((f_1,...,f_k))\) - w.l.o.g. \(f(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{k} k_i f_k(x)\) - we get \(f(a) = 0\). Therefore, by choosing \(k_j = \delta_{i,j}\) we get \(f_k(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{k} \delta_{i,j} f_k(x)\) and hence \(f_k(a) = 0\). Therefore \(a \in \V(f_i)\) for \(i \in I\) or

\begin{align*} a \in \bigcap_{i = 1}^k V(f_i) \end{align*}

2.2. \(\supseteq\)

Let \(a \in \mathbb{A}_K^n\) such that \(a \in \bigcap_{i=1}^k V(f_1)\). Suppose \(f \in V((f_1,...,f_k))\), then w.l.o.g. \(f(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{k} k_i f_k(x)\) for \(k_i \in K\) and hence \(f(a) = 0\) Therefore \(f \in V((f_1,...,f_k))\)
