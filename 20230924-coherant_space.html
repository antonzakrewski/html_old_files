coherant space

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space. Then \(X\) is said to be a coherant space, if there exists a commutative ring \(A\) such that the Zariski topology is homeomorphic to \(X\), i.e.

\begin{align*} \mathrm{Spec}(A) \simeq X \end{align*}
