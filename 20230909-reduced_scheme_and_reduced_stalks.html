reduced scheme and reduced stalks

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{O}_X, O_i)\) be a scheme.

TFAE:

  1. \((X, \mathcal{O}_X)\) is a reduced scheme
  2. each stalk \(\mathcal{O}_p\) is a reduced ring

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose there exists a germ \(\alpha \in \mathcal{O}_p \setminus \{0\}\), which is nilpotent. Then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), an open \(U \subseteq X\) and a section \(\beta \in \mathcal{O}(U)\), such that

\begin{align*} \varphi_{O,p}(\beta) = [\beta] = \alpha \in \coprod O/\sim \end{align*}

Since \(\alpha \neq 0\), it follows, that \(\beta \neq 0\). Furthermore

\begin{align*} [0] =& \alpha^n \\ =& [\beta]^n \\ =& [\beta^n] \\ \end{align*}

Therefore \(\beta^n \sim 0\), i.e. there exists an open set \(U' \subseteq U\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U,U'}(\beta^n) =& 0 \end{align*}
