intersection of standard opens

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a ring and \(\mathrm{Spec}(R)\) the zariski spectrum equipped with the Zariski topology. Then for the standard open \(D(f),D(g)\) it holds

\begin{align*} D(f) \cap D(g) =& D(f \cdot g) \\ =& D(g \cdot f) \end{align*}

2. Proof

By definition,

\begin{align*} D(f) \cap D(g) = \{\mathfrak{p} \vert f, g \not\in \mathfrak{p}\} \end{align*}

2.1. \(D(f) \cap D(g) \subseteq D(f \cdot g)\)

Let \(\mathfrak{p} \in D(f) \cap D(g)\), thus \(f,g \not\in \mathfrak{p}\), then by contradiction we conclude, that if \(f \cdot g \in \mathfrak{p}\), because \(\mathfrak{p}\) is prime, it follows, that w.l.o.g. \(f \in \mathfrak{p}\)

2.2. \(D(f \cdot g) \subseteq D(f) \cap D(g)\)

Suppose \(\mathfrak{p} \in D(f \cdot g)\), then \(f \cdot g \not\in \mathfrak{p}\), then by contraposition, if w.l.o.g. \(f \in \mathfrak{p}\), it follows by closedness under multiplication, that \(f \cdot g \in \mathfrak{p}\).

The second equality \(D(f \cdot g) = D(g \cdot f)\) follows from the commutativity of intersections>
