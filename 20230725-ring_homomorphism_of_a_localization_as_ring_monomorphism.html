ring homomorphism of a localization as ring monomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be commutative ring and \(S \subseteq R\) a multiplicatively closed set and \(S^{-1}R\) the localization. TFAE:

  1. \(S\) contains no zero divisors
  2. the canonical ring homomorphism \(\varphi: R \rightarrowtail S^{-1}R\) is a monomorphism

2. Proof

Beweis durch Kontraposition

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose \(\varphi\) is not a monomorphism, then there exists an Element \(r \in R\) such that

\begin{align*} \varphi(r) =& \frac{r}{1} \\ =& \frac{0}{1} \end{align*}

Therefore, there exists an \(s \in S\), such that

\begin{align*} s \cdot (r \cdot 1 - 0 \cdot 1) =& 0 \\ s \cdot r =& 0 \end{align*}

and \(s \in S\) is a zero divisor

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(s \in S\) is a zero divisor, then there exists an \(r \in R \setminus \{0\}\) such that

\begin{align*} r \cdot s = 0 \end{align*}

Therefore

\begin{align*} \varphi(r) = \frac{r}{1} \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} \frac{r}{1} = \frac{0}{s} \\ 1 \cdot (r \cdot s - 1 \cdot 0) =& 1 \cdot (0 - 0) \\ =& 1 \cdot 0 \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

therefore \(r \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\) and minimal kernel and monomorphism
