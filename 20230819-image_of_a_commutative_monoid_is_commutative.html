image of a commutative monoid is commutative

1. Proposition

Let \(M,N\) be monoids, such that \(M\) is commutative and \(\varphi: M \rightarrow N\) a monoid homomorphism. Then \(\varphi[M]\) is also a commutative monoid

2. Proof

Let \(n_1,n_2 \in \varphi[M]\), then there exist \(m_1,m_2 \in M\) such that \(\varphi(m_1) = n_1, \varphi(m_2) = n_2\). Therefore

\begin{align*} n_1 \cdot n_2 =& \varphi(m_1) \cdot \varphi(m_2) \\ =& \varphi(m_1 \cdot m_2) \\ =& \varphi(m_2 \cdot m_1) \\ =& \varphi(m_2) \cdot \varphi(m_1) \\ =& n_2 \cdot n_1 \end{align*}
