finitely generated ideal and maximal ideal of the subset of finitely generated Ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathcal{a}\) be an ideal TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{a}\) is finitely generated
  2. Let \(S \coloneqq \{(a_1,...,a_n) \vert a_1,...,a_n \in \mathfrak{a}\}\) be the set of finitely generated ideals. Then there exists a maximal element in \(S\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

By assumption \(\mathfrak{a}\) is finitely generated and thus in \(S\). By construction, it is also maximal.

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(\mathfrak{m}\) be the maximal element of \(S\). Let \(a \in \mathfrak{a}\), then \((\mathfrak{m},a)\) is also finitely generated with \((\mathfrak{m}, a) \supseteq \mathfrak{m}\). Because \(\mathfrak{m}\) is maximal, we conclude that \(a \in \mathfrak{m}\) or \(\mathfrak{a} = \mathfrak{m}\)
