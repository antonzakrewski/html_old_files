particular point topology

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(X\) be a set and \(x_0 \in X\). The particular point topology \({PPX}_{x_0}\) on \(X\) is defined by:

\begin{align*} {PPX}_{x_0} := \{O \subseteq X \vert x_0 \in O \lor O = \emptyset\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a) trivial sets in a topology

By construction \(\emptyset \in {PPX}_{x_0}\) and \(x_0 \in X\) and therefore \(X \in {PPX}_{x_0}\)

2.2. b) Openness of a finite intersection of open sets / Openness of a union of open sets

Let \(O_i\) be collection of open sets with \(O_i \neq X\), then \(\forall i \in I : x_0 \not\in O_i\). Therefore we can conclude:

\begin{align*} p \not\in& \bigcap_{i \in I} O_i \Rightarrow \bigcap_{i \in I} O_i \in {PPX}_{x_0}\\ p \not\in& \bigcup_{i \in I} O_i \Rightarrow \bigcup_{i \in I} O_i \in {PPX}_{x_0} \end{align*}
