smooth invariance of domains

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be a real differentiable manifold. Then the dimension is locally constant

2. Proof

Let \((\varphi_1, U_1)\) and \((\varphi_2,U_2)\) be charts with nonempty intersection. Then by assumption

\begin{align*} f_{1,2}: \varphi_2 \circ \varphi_{1}^{-1}: \varphi_1(U_1 \cap U_2) \rightarrow \varphi_2(U_1 \cap U_2) \end{align*}

and its inverse \(f_{2,1}\) are Diffeomorphisms

Hence

\begin{align*} f_{12} \circ f_{21} =& \mathrm{id}_{\varphi_{1}(U_1 \cap U_2)} \\ f_{21} \circ f_{12} =& \mathrm{id}_{\varphi_{2}(U_1 \cap U_2)} \end{align*}

with

\begin{align*} D_{\varphi_2(x)}f_{12} \circ D_{\varphi_{1}(x)}f_{21} =& \mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{R}^m} \\ D_{\varphi_1(x)}f_{21} \circ D_{\varphi_{2}(x)}f_{12} =& \mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{R}^n} \end{align*}

and by Rangsatz (?) we conclude that \(m = n\)
