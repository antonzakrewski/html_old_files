zariski topology on an affine space as initial topology

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space. Then \(\mathbb{A}_K^n\) has the initial topology for \(K\) with the cofinite topology or equivalently for \(K\) with only \(\{0\}\) as closed set

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathbb{A}_K^n \arrow[]{r}{p(x)} K \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

2.1. finer

Suppose \(f \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) is a polynomial and \(C = \{c_1,...,c_l\} \subseteq K\) closed. Then \(f - c_i\) is also a polynomial, hence \((f - c_i)^{-1}(c_i) = f^{-1}(c_i)\) is closed. Thus \(C\) is closed as finite union of closed sets. Hence \(f\) is continuous (cf. Urbilder abgeschlossene Mengen unter stetigen Abbildungen)

2.2. coarser

Suppose \(C \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n\) is closed in the usual topology. Then it is generated as zero set of finitely many polynomials
