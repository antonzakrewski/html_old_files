Definition: Eulersche Zahl über Grenzwert der Ableitung

1. Definition

\begin{align*} e =& \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\left(1+\epsilon\right)^{\frac{1}{\epsilon}}\right) \end{align*}

2. Beweis

Formal Richtig ?

\begin{align*} (e^{x})' =& e^x \\ \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{e^{x + \epsilon} - e^{x}}{\epsilon}\right) =& e^x \\ e^x \cdot \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{e^{\epsilon} - 1}{\epsilon}\right) =& e^x && \vert \cdot e^{-x}\\ \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\frac{e^{\epsilon} - 1}{\epsilon}\right) =& 1 && \vert \cdot \left(\lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\epsilon\right)\right) \\ \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(e^{\epsilon} - 1\right) =& \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\epsilon \right) && \vert + 1\\ \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(e^{\epsilon}\right) =& 1 + \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\epsilon \right) && \vert (...)^{\frac{1}{\epsilon}} \\ e =& \lim_{\epsilon \to 0}\left(\left(1 + \epsilon\right)^{\frac{1}{\epsilon}}\right) \end{align*}
