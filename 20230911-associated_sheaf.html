associated sheaf to a module

1. Definition

Let \(A\) be a ring and \(M\) an \(A\)-module. The sheaf associated to \(M\) is defined as \(\tilde{M}\) with \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) equipped with the Zariski topology as topological space and:

\begin{align*} \tilde{M}(U) = \{s: U \rightarrow \coprod_{p \in U} M_{\mathfrak{p}}\} \end{align*}

where \(M_{\mathfrak{p}}\) is the localization of a module at the prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p}\) such that for each \(\mathfrak{p} \in U\) there exists a neighbourhood \(V \subseteq U\) and \(m \in M, f \in A\) such that for each \(\mathfrak{q} \in V\) it follows, that

\begin{align*} f \not\in& \mathfrak{q} \\ s(\mathfrak{q}) =& m/f \in M_{\mathfrak{q}} \end{align*}
