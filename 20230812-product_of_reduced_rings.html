product of reduced rings

1. Proposition

Let \(R_i\) be rings and \(\prod R_i\) the direct product of rings, \(\coprod R_i\) the direct sum of rings. TFAE:

  1. \(R_i\) is for \(i \in I\) reduced
  2. \(\prod R_i\) is reduced
  3. \(\coprod R_i\) is reduced

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(r = (r_i) \in \prod R_i \setminus \{0\}\) and \(n \in \mathbb{N}\). Then \(r^n = (r_i^n)\) and by assumption there exists an \(r_i \neq 0 \neq r_i^n\), hence \((r_i^n) \neq 0\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 3)

corollary of:

  • subring of a reduced ring as reduced ring
  • direct sum of rings as subring of the direct product

2.3. 3) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(r_i \in R_i\), then \((0) \times (r_i) \in \coprod R_i\). Furthermore, for any \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), we conclude, that

\begin{align*} ((0) \times (r_i))^n = (0) \times (r_i^n) \neq 0 \end{align*}

and hence \(r_i^n \neq 0\)
