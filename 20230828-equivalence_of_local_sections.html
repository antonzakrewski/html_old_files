equivalence of local sections

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{V}\) a category and \(\mathcal{F}\) a V-valued presheaf. Then local sections \((O_1,s_1), (O_2,s_2)\) are said to be equivalent at \(x \in X\), if there exists an open set \(U \subseteq X\) such that \(U \subseteq O_1, U \subseteq O_2\) and \(x \in U\) with

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U}(s_1) = \mathrm{res}_{U}(s_2) \end{align*}

2. Proof (as equivalence class)

2.1. symmetric relation

follows from the symmetry of \(=\)

2.2. reflexive Relation

follows from \(U = O\) for \((O,s)\)

2.3. transitive Relation

Let \((O_1,s_1),(O_2,s_2),(O_3,s_3)\) and restrictions \(U_1,U_2\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U_1}(s_1) =& \mathrm{res}_{U_1}(s_2) \\ \mathrm{res}_{U_2}(s_2) =& \mathrm{res}_{U_2}(s_3) \end{align*}

Then \(U_1 \cap U_2\) is open (cf. Openness of a finite intersection of open sets) hence

\begin{align*} \mathrm{res}_{U_1 \cap U_2}(s_1) =& \mathrm{res}_{U_1 \cap U_2}(s_2) \\ =& \mathrm{res}_{U_1 \cap U_2}(s_3) \end{align*}
