prime ideal as radical ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{p}\) a prime ideal. Then \(\mathfrak{p}\) is a radical ideal

2. Proof

Suppose there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) and \(a \in R\) such that \(a^n \in \mathfrak{p}\). Then \(a^{n-1} \cdot a \in \mathfrak{p}\) and by assumption of \(\mathfrak{p}\) being prime, we conclude w.l.o.g. \(a^{n-1} \in \mathfrak{p}\) Hence, by induction \(a \in \mathfrak{p}\)
