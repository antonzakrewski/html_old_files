Universal property of an Abelianization

1. Proposition

Let \(G\) be a group, \(A\) an abelian group and \(G/[G,G]\) the Abelianization. Then for \(\varphi: G \rightarrow A\), there exists a unique group-homomorphism

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} G \arrow[]{rd}{\varphi} \arrow[]{r}{\pi}& G/[G,G] \arrow[dashed]{d}{\exists! \psi}\\ & A \\ \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

2. Proof

by

  • fundamental theorem on group homomorphism by universal property
\begin{align*} \varphi([g,h]) =& \varphi(g h g^{-1} h^{-1}) \\ =& \varphi(g) \cdot \varphi(h) \cdot \varphi(g^{-1}) \cdot \varphi(h^{-1}) \\ =& \varphi(g) \cdot \varphi(h) \cdot \varphi(g)^{-1} \cdot \varphi(h)^{-1} \\ =& \varphi(g) \cdot \varphi(g)^{-1} \varphi(h) \cdot \cdot \varphi(h)^{-1} \\ =& e \cdot e \\ =& e \end{align*}
