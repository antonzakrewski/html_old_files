topological monoid

1. Definition

Let \(M, \cdot\) be a monoid and \(\mathcal{T}\) a topology on the set \(M\). Then \(M, \cdot, \mathcal{T}\) is said to be topological monoid, if the binary operation

\begin{align*} \cdot: M \times M \rightarrow& M \end{align*}

is a continuous map with respect to the product topology
