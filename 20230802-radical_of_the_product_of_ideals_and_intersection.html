radical of the product of ideals and intersection

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{a}_1,\mathfrak{a}_2\) ideals. Then

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2} =& \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_1 \cap \mathfrak{a}_2} \\ =& \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_1} \cap \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_2} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. 1) $ \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}1 ⋅ \mathfrak{a}2} \subseteq \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}1 ∩ \mathfrak{a}2}$

2.2. 2) $ \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}1 ∩ \mathfrak{a}2} \subseteq \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}1} ∩ \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}2}$

Suppose there exists an \(x \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_1 \cap \mathfrak{a}_2}\), then \(x^n \in \mathfrak{a}_1 \cap \mathfrak{a}_2\). Thus \(x \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_1}, \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_2}\)

2.3. 3) $\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}1} ∩ \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}2} \subseteq \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}1 ⋅ \mathfrak{a}2} $

Suppose there exists an \(x \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_1} \cap \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_2}\), then \(x^n \in \mathfrak{a}_1, x^m \in \mathfrak{a}_2\) for \(n,m \in \mathbb{N}\). Therefore \(x^{n} \cdot x^m = x^{n + m} \in \mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2\) and hence \(x \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}_1 \cdot \mathfrak{a}_2}\)
