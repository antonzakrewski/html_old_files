uniqueness of a categorical product up to isomorphism

1. Proposition

The categorical product is unique up to isomorphism.

2. Proof (sketch)

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category with objects \(X_i \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) and categorical products \(P := \prod_{i \in I}^{} X_i, P' := \prod_{i \in I}^{} X_i'\) with projections \(\pi_i, \pi'_i\) Then there exists the unique morphism \(\mathrm{id}_{P} : P \rightarrow P\) and unique morphisms \(I: P \rightarrow P', I: P' \rightarrow P\) such that the induced diagram commutes.
