zeros and the induced ring homomorphism for the polynomial rings

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be rings, \(\psi: A \rightarrow B\) a ring-homomorphism, \(\psi^X: A[X] \rightarrow B[X]\) the induced ring homomorphism, \(f \in A[X]\) a polynomial and \(\alpha \in A\).

If it holds for the evaluation homomorphism

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\alpha}(f) = 0 \end{align*}

then it follows, that

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\psi(\alpha)}(\psi^X(f)) = 0 \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(f = \sum \alpha_i X^i\). Then by definition we conclude

\begin{align*} \psi^X(f) = \sum \psi(\alpha_i) X^i \end{align*}

and by assumption

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\alpha}(f) =& \sum \alpha_i \cdot \alpha^i \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

Furthermore, we get

\begin{align*} \varphi_{\psi(\alpha)}(\sum \psi(\alpha_i) X^i) =& \sum \psi(\alpha_i) \cdot \psi(\alpha)^i \\ =& \psi( \sum \alpha_i \cdot \alpha^i) \\ =& \psi(0) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}
