radical of an ideal as preimage of the nilradal for the projection to the quotient ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a}\) an ideal and \(\pi: A \rightarrow A/\mathfrak{a}\) the canonical projection (see: quotient ring) Then

\begin{align*} \sqrt{a} = \pi^{-1}[\mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{a}} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Suppose \(a + \mathfrak{a} \in \mathcal{N}_{A/\mathfrak{a}}\), then this is equivalent to \(\exists n \in \mathbb{N}: (a + \mathfrak{a})^{n} = a^n + \mathfrak{a} = 0 + \mathfrak{a}\). Hence an ∈ \(\mathfrak{a}\) and by assumption \(a \in \pi^{-1}[a + \mathfrak{a}]\) or \(a \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{a}}\)
