split mono as monomorphism

1. Proposition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(f\) a split mono. Then \(f\) is also a monomorphism

2. Proof

By assumption there exists a left-inverse morphism \(f^{-1}\) Suppose \(f \circ g = f \circ h\), then by assumption we get

\begin{align*} f^{-1} \circ (f \circ g) =& f^{-1} \circ (f \circ h) \\ \mathrm{id} \circ g =& \mathrm{id} \circ h \\ g =& h \end{align*}
