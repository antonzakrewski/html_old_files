subspace collapse

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(B \subseteq A \subseteq X\) subsets and \(A/B\), \(X/B\) be topological collapses. Then there exists a canonical continuous inclusion

\begin{align*} \iota: A/B \rightarrowtail& X/B \\ [a] \mapsto& [a] \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

follows from \(B = B\)

2.2. injective

follows from construction

2.3. continuous

Suppose \(U_x \subseteq X/B\) is open, then \(U_x\) (or \(U_x \cup B\), if \([b] \in U_x\)) is open in \(X\). Thus by the definition of a subspace topology, \(U_x \cap A\) (or \((U_x \cup B) \cap B\)) is open in \(A\). And since \(\pi^{-1}[\pi[U_x \cap A]] = U_x \cap A\), it follows that \(\pi[U_x \cap A]\) is open (or similarly for \((U_x \cup B) \cap A)\)
