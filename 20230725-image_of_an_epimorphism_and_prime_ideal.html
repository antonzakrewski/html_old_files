Image of an epimorphism and prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be commutative rings, \(f: A \twoheadrightarrow B\) an epimorphism and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) an ideal with \(\mathrm{ker}(f) \subseteq \mathfrak{a}\) TFAE:

  1. \(\mathfrak{a}\) is prime
  2. \(f[\mathfrak{a}]\) is prime

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose \(b_1,b_2 \in B\) such that \(b_1 \cdot b_2 \in f[\mathfrak{a}]\) Then there exist \(a_1,a_2 \in A\) such that \(f(a_1) = b_1, f(a_2) = b_2\). Therefore

\begin{align*} b_1 \cdot b_2 =& f(a_1) \cdot f(a_2) \\ f(a_1 \cdot a_2) \end{align*}

Thus \(a_1 \cdot a_2 \in \mathfrak{a}\) (see: preimage of an image and kernel) and since \(\mathfrak{a}\) is prime, w.l.o.g. \(a_1 \in \mathfrak{a}\) and hence \(b_1 \in f[\mathfrak{a}]\)

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

corollary of Preimage of a prime ideal as prime ideal.
