quotient group acts on the orbital collapse

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(G\) a topological group and \(\triangle: G \times X \rightarrow X\) a topological group action. Then for a normal subgroup \(H \trianglelefteq G\) there exists a canonical, continuous Group Action

\begin{align*} \triangle': G/H \times X/H \rightarrow& X/H (g + H),[x] \mapsto [g \cdot x] \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

Suppose there exist \(g,g' \in G\) and \(x,x' \in X\) such that

\begin{align*} gH =& g'H \\ [x] =& [x'] \end{align*}

Then there exist \(h_g,h_x\) such that

\begin{align*} g \cdot h_g =& g' \\ h_x \triangleleft x =& x' \end{align*}

Then we get

\begin{align*} g'H \triangle' [x'] =& g \cdot h_g H triangle [h_x \cdot x] \\ =& [g \cdot h_g \cdot h_x \cdot x] \end{align*}

Let \(h \coloneqq h_g \cdot h_x\). Since conjugation is a group automorphism and \(H\) is a normal subgroup, we conclude that there exists an \(h'\) such that

\begin{align*} g^{-1} \cdot h' \cdot g = h \end{align*}

thus

\begin{align*} [g \cdot h \cdot x] =& [g \cdot g^{-1} \cdot h' \cdot g \cdot x] \\ =& [h' \cdot g \cdot x] \end{align*}

and by construction,

\begin{align*} h' \cdot (g \cdot x) =& (h' \cdot g \cdot x) \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{align*} [h' \cdot g \cdot x] =& [g \cdot x] \end{align*}

Or equivalently

\begin{align*} [g'x] = [g \cdot x] \end{align*}

2.2. continuous

By welldefinedness, this diagram commutes

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} G \times X \arrow[]{d}{\pi_{H^2}} \arrow[]{r}{\triangle}& X \arrow[]{d}{\pi_{H}} \\ (G \times X)/H^2 \arrow[]{r}{\triangle'}& X/H \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

By construction of a quotient topology, \(\triangle'\) is continuous if and only if $\triangle ˆ πH{2} = πH ˆ \triangle $ is continuous, where \(\triangle \circ \pi_H\) is continuous as composition of continuous maps.

Furthermore, by universal property of a quotient topology \((G \times X)/H^2\), the (pseudo-)identity from \(G \times X\) to \((G \times X)/H^2\) is continuous

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} G \times X \arrow[bend left]{rr}{} & G/H \times X/H \arrow[]{r}{} & (G\times X)/H^2 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
