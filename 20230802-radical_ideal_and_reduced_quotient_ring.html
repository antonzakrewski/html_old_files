radical ideal and reduced quotient ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq R\) an ideal. TFAE:

  1. \(\mathfrak{a}\) is a radical ideal
  2. the quotient ring \(R/\mathfrak{a}\) is a reduced ring

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose there exists an \(a \in R\) and \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that

\begin{align*} (a + \mathfrak{a})^n =& a^n + \mathfrak{a} \\ =& 0 + \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

then \(a^n \in \mathfrak{a}\) and by assumption \(a \in \mathfrak{a}\). Hence

\begin{align*} a + \mathfrak{a} = 0 + \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose there exists an \(a \in R\) such that \(a^n \in \mathfrak{a}\) for some \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) Then

\begin{align*} (a + \mathfrak{a})^n =& a^n + \mathfrak{a} \\ =& 0 + \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

and by assumption of \(R/\mathfrak{a}\) being a reduced ring, we conclude that \(a + \mathfrak{a} = 0\) or equivalently \(a \in \mathfrak{a}\)
