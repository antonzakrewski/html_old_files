preimage of a radical ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A,B\) be commutative rings, \(\varphi: A \rightarrow B\) an homomorphism and \(\mathfrak{b} \subseteq B\) an ideal. Then

\begin{align*} f^{-1}[\sqrt{\mathfrak{b}}] = \sqrt{f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]} \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(x \in \sqrt{f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]}\), then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(x^n \in f^{-1}[\mathfrak{b}]\) and this is equivalent to \(f(x^n) = f(x)^n \in \mathfrak{b}\) or \(f(x) \in \sqrt{\mathfrak{b}}\)
