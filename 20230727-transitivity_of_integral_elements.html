transitivity of integral elements

1. Proposition

Let \(A \subseteq B \subseteq C\) be rings, and \(\alpha \in C\), such that \(\alpha\) is an integral element over \(B\), i.e. there exist \(\beta_0,...,\beta_{n-1} \in B\) with

\begin{align*} 0 =& \alpha^n + \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \beta_i \alpha^i \end{align*}

where \(\beta_0,...,\beta_{n-1}\) are integral over \(A\).

Then \(\alpha\) is integral over \(A\)

2. Proof

By assumption, \(B' \coloneqq A[\beta_0,...,\beta_{n-1}]\) is a finitely generated module over \(A\). Therefore, \(B'[\alpha]\) is also finitely generated over \(A\) with \(\alpha \in B'[\alpha]\) (see: integral element and finitely generated module) Thus \(\alpha\) is integral over \(A\)
