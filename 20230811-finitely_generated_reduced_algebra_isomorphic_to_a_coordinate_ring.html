finitely generated reduced algebra isomorphic to a coordinate ring

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field, \(A\) a reduced, finitely generated K-Algebra. Then there exists an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\) and an affine subvariety \(V \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n\) such that

\begin{align*} A \simeq \mathcal{O}(V) \end{align*}

for the coordinate ring \(\mathcal{O}(V)\)

2. Proof

Let \(A \simeq K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathfrak{a}\) for an \(n \in \mathbb{N}\). Since \(A\) is reduced, \(\mathfrak{a}\) is a radical ideal (cf. radical ideal and reduced quotient ring) Let \(V(\mathfrak{a}) \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n\) be the affine subvariety, then we get

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}(V(\mathfrak{a})) = K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathcal{I}(V(\mathfrak{a})) \end{align*}

and since \(\mathfrak{a}\) is radical, applying radical ideal and affine subvariety for an algebraically closed field we get

\begin{align*} \mathcal{O}(V(\mathfrak{a})) =& K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathcal{I}(V(\mathfrak{a})) \\ =& K[X_1,...,X_n]/\mathfrak{a} \end{align*}
