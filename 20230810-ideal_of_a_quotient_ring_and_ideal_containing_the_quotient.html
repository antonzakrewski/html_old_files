ideal of a quotient ring and ideal containing the quotient

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq A\) an ideal and \(A/\mathfrak{a}\) the quotient ring. Then the map

\begin{align*} \{\overline{\mathfrak{b}} \subseteq A \vert \overline{\mathfrak{b}} \text{ ideal such that } \mathfrak{a} \subseteq \overline{\mathfrak{b}}\} \underrightarrow{\pi__{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}} \{\mathfrak{b} \subseteq A/\mathfrak{a} \vert \mathfrak{b} \text{ ideal}\} \end{align*}

is an isomorphism between posets (see: Category Pos)
