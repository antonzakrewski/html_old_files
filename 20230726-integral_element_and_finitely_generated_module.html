integral element and finitely generated module

1. Proposition

Let \(A \subseteq B\) be commutative rings and \(\alpha \in B\) TFAE:

  1. \(\alpha\) is integral element over a subring
  2. \(A[\alpha]\) is a finitely generated module over \(A\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

by assumption, there exists a polynomial \(f: \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X^i\) such that \(\varphi_{\alpha}(f) = f(\alpha) = 0\) (see: evaluation homomorphism) Therefore,

\begin{align*} \langle 1, \alpha,...,\alpha^n\rangle \end{align*}

Suppose \(\alpha^i \in A[\alpha]\) for \(i \leq m\), then

\begin{align*} \alpha^m =& \sum_{i=0}^{m-1} \lambda_i \alpha^i \end{align*}

Therefore

\begin{align*} \alpha^{m+1} =& \alpha \cdot \alpha^m \\ =& \alpha \sum_{i=0}^{m-1} \lambda_i \alpha^i \\ =& \sum_{i=0}^{m-1} \lambda_i \alpha^{i+1} \in \langle 1,...,\alpha^m\rangle \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)
