Product of coprime Ideals equals Intersection

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring and \(I,I'\) coprime ideals. Then for the Product of \(I,I'\) follows

\begin{align*} I \cdot I' = I \cap I' \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a)

follows from Komplexprodukt von Idealen als Teilmenge des Schnitts

2.2. b)

By assumption we know, that \(a \in I\) and \(b \in I'\) exist such that:

\begin{align*} a + b = 1 \end{align*}

Suppose \(c \in I \cap I'\), then it follows

\begin{align*} ca + cb = c \end{align*}

We conclude that \(c \cdot a \in I' \cdot I\) and \(c \cdot b \in I' \cdot I\)
