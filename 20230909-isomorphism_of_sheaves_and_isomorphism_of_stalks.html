isomorphism of presheaves induces isomorphism of stalks

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{G}\) V-valued presheafs for a category \(\mathcal{V}\) with stalks \(\mathcal{F}_p,\mathcal{G}_p\) at a point \(p \in X\). Suppose \(f: \mathcal{F} \rightarrow \mathcal{G}\) is a isomorphism of presheaves, then \(f\) induces an isomorphism \(f^*: \mathcal{F}_p \rightarrow \mathcal{G}_p\)

2. Proof

Follows from morphism of presheaves induces morphism on the stalks: by Assumption, \(f\) and \(f^{-1}\) induce unique $f*, f-1^, such that the induced diagrams commute. Hence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} \mathcal{F}(U) \arrow[bend right]{rd}{} \arrow[bend right]{rdd}{} \arrow[bend right]{rddd}{} \arrow[]{rr}{\mathrm{res}} && \mathcal{F}(V) \arrow[bend left]{ld}{} \arrow[bend left]{ldd}{} \arrow[bend left]{lddd}{}\\ & \mathcal{F}_p \arrow[bend right, swap]{dd}{\mathrm{id}} \arrow[]{d}{f^*} & \\ & \mathcal{G}_p \arrow[]{d}{f^{-1}^*} & \\ & \mathcal{F}_p & \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}
