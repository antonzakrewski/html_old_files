bilinearization of a map between modules

1. Construction

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(M_1,M_2,N\) \(R\)-modules and \(\iota: M_1 \oplus M_2 \rightarrow N\) a set theoretic map. Let

\begin{align*} S =& \{\iota(m_1,m_2 + m_2') - \iota(m_1,m_2) - \iota(m_1,m_2') \vert m_1 \in M_1, m_2,m_2' \in M_2 \} \\ \cup &\{\iota(m_1 + m_1',m_2) - \iota(m_1,m_2) - \iota(m_1',m_2) \vert m_1,m_1' \in M_1, m_2 \in M_2 \} \\ \cup &\{\iota(m_1,r \cdot m_2) - r\iota(m_1,m_2) \vert r \in R, m_1 \in M_1, m_2 \in M_2 \} \\ \cup &\{\iota(r \cdot m_1,m_2) - r\iota(m_1,m_2) \vert r \in R, m_1 \in M_1, m_2 \in M_2 \} \end{align*}

and \(V \coloneqq \langle S\rangle \subseteq N\) the generated submodule. Then we define

\begin{align*} \varphi: M_1 \oplus M_2 \rightarrow& N/N \\ (m_1,m_2) \mapsto \end{align*}

as bilinear map

2. Proof

Let \((m_1 + m_1',m_2+m_2') \in M_1 \oplus M_2\) and \(r_1,r_2 \in R\). Then

\begin{align*} \varphi(r_1 \cdot m_1 + m_1', r_2 \cdot m_2 + m_1') + V =& \varphi(r_1 \cdot m_1 + m_1', r_2 \cdot m_2 + m_1') - \varphi(r_1 \cdot m_1 + m_1', r_2 \cdot m_2 + m_1') + \varphi(r_1 \cdot m_1,r_2 \cdot m_2 + m_1') + \varphi(m_1',r_2 \cdot m_2 + m_1') + V \\ =& \varphi(r_1 \cdot m_1,r_2 \cdot m_2 + m_1') + \varphi(m_1',r_2 \cdot m_2 + m_2') + V \\ =& \varphi(r_1 \cdot m_1, r_2 \cdot m_2) + \varphi(r_1 \cdot m_1, m_2') + \varphi(m_1,r_2 \cdot m_2) + \varphi(m_1,m_2') + V \\ =& r_1 \cdot r_2 \cdot \varphi(m_1, m_2) + r_1 \cdot \varphi(m_1, m_2') + r_2 \cdot \varphi(m_1,m_2) + \varphi(m_1,m_2') + V \\ \end{align*}
