localization of a composition of module homomorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring, \(S \subseteq R\) multiplicatively closed and \(f: M \rightarrow N, g: N \rightarrow L\) module homomorphisms for $R$-modules \(M,N,L\). Then

\begin{align*} S^{-1}(g \circ f) = S^{-1}g \circ S^{-1}f \end{align*}

2. Proof

follows from the construction of the localization of a composition of module homomorphisms:

\begin{align*} S^{-1}(g \circ f)(m) =& \frac{(g \circ f)(m)}{1} \\ =& S^{-1}g \cdot \frac{f(m)}{1} \\ =& S^{-1}g \cdot S^{-1}f \end{align*}
