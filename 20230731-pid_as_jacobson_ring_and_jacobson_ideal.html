PID as jacobson ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a principal ideal domain. TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{J}_A = 0\) (see: Jacobson radical)
  2. \(R\) is a Jacobson ring

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

let \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq A\) be prime. If \(\mathfrak{p} \neq \{0\}\) is a maximal ideal prime ideal and maximal ideal in a PID. If \(\mathfrak{p} = \{0\}\), then it follows by definition

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

corollary of zero ideal as prime ideal and integral domain
