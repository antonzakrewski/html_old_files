topological manifold in general not differentiable

1. Proposition

Let \(M\) be a manifold with an Atlas \(\mathcal{A}\) Then \(M\) is in general not differentiable

2. Proof

Consider \(\mathbb{R}\) with the charts

\begin{align*} (\mathbb{R}, \mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{R}})_1 \\ (\mathbb{R}, \varphi)_2 \end{align*}

where

\begin{align*} \varphi: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ x \mapsto \begin{cases} x & \mbox{if } x \geq 0 \\ 2x & \mbox{else } \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}

by definition of a transition map of a chart we get

\begin{align*} \varphi_{1 \rightarrow 2} =& \mathrm{id}_{\mathbb{R}}^{-1} \circ \varphi \\ =& \varphi \end{align*}

which is not differentiable at \(x = 0\)
