integral elements closed under addition and multiplication

1. Proposition

Let \(R \subseteq S\) be a commutative ring and \(\alpha,\beta \in S\) be integral elements over \(R\). Then \(\alpha + \beta\) is also an integral element.

2. Proof

by assumption, \(R[\alpha]\) is a finitely generated module (cf. integral element and finitely generated module) Thus

\begin{align*} R[\alpha, \beta] = R[\alpha][\beta] \end{align*}

is also finetly generated, hence \(\alpha \cdot \beta, \alpha + \beta \in R[\alpha, \beta]\) are integral elements.
