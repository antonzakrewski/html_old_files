Monomorphismus von einem Ring auf das dazugehörige Polynom

1. Satz

Let \(R\) be a ring, then the map defined by Die Abbildung

\begin{align*} \phi: R \rightarrow& R[x] \\ \phi: a \mapsto& (a,0,0,...) \end{align*}

is a ring monomorphism

2. Beweis

2.1. injectiv

Suppose \(a,b \in R\) such that \(\phi(a) = \phi(b)\) Then follows

\begin{align*} (a,...) = (b,...) \end{align*}

and by definition we know, that \(a = b \in R\)

2.2. homomorphism

2.2.1. addition

Suppose \(a,b \in R\). Then follows:

\begin{align*} \phi(a + b) = (a + b,...) =& (a,...) + (b,...) \\ =& \phi(a) + \phi(b) \end{align*}

2.2.2. multiplication

Suppose \(a,b \in R\). Then we conclude:

\begin{align*} \phi(r \cdot a) =& (r \cdot a,0,...) \\ =& r \cdot (a,0,...) \end{align*}
