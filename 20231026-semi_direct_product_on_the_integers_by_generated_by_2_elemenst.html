semi direct product on the integers by generated by 2 Elements

1. Proposition

The semidirect product on the integers by sign \(\mathbb{Z} \rtimes \mathbb{Z}\) is generated by \((0,1)\) and \((1,0)\)

2. Proof

Proof by weak induction on \(\mathbb{Z} \times \mathbb{Z}\) Since \((0,0)\) is the neutral element (\((0,0) \cdot (a,b) = (0 + (-1)^{0} a, 0 + b) = (a, b)\)) and \((0,1) \cdot (0,-1) = (0 + (-1)^{0} \cdot 0, 1 - 1) = (0,0)\), we concldue, that \((0,-1) \in \langle (0,1), (1,0)\rangle\) as inverse element Analogously \((1,0) \cdot (-1,0) = (1 + (-1)^0 \cdot -1, 0 + 0) = (1 - 1,0) = (0,0)\), thus \((-1,0) \in \langle (0,1), (1,0)\rangle\)

2.1. a)

Let \((a,b) \in \mathbb{Z} \rtimes \mathbb{Z}\). Then

\begin{align*} (1,0) \cdot (a,b) =& ( 1 + (-1)^{0} \cdot a, 0 + b) \h =& (1 + a, b) \end{align*}

2.2. b)

\begin{align*} (a,b) \cdot (0,1) =& (a + (-1)^{b} \cdot 0, b + 1) \\ =& (a + 0, b + 1) \\ =& (a, b + 1) \end{align*}

2.3. c)

\begin{align*} (-1,0) \cdot (a,b) =& (-1 + (-1)^{0} \cdot a, 0 + b)\\ =& (-1 + a, b) \\ =& (a - 1, b) \end{align*}

2.4. d)

begin{align*} (0,-1) ⋅ (a,b) =& ( 0 ⋅ (-1)-1 a, -1 + b)
=& (-a, b -1) \end{align*} and applying \(2a\)-times procedure a) results in

\begin{align*} (a, b-1) \end{align*}
