prime ideal and maximal ideal in a PID

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a principal ideal domain and \(\mathcal{a} \neq 0\) an ideal. TFAE:

  1. \(\mathcal{a}\) is a maximal ideal
  2. \(\mathcal{a}\) is a prime ideal

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

special case of maximales Ideal als Primideal

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(\mathfrak{a} = (p)\), and \(\mathfrak{m} = (m)\) be a maximal ideal, such that \(\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\) (see: existence of a maximal ideal for a strict ideal). Then there exists an \(u \in (m)\) such that \(u \cdot m = p\). Let w.l.o.g. \(m \not\in (p)\), then by assumption we get \(u \in (p)\). Therefore there exists \(s \in R\) with \(p \cdot s = u\). Then

\begin{align*} p \cdot s \cdot m =& p \\ p(s \cdot m - 1) =& 0 \end{align*}

since a PID is by definition an integral domain, we conclude that \(s \cdot m = 1\) and therefore \(m \in R^{\times}\) or equivalently

\begin{align*} (m) = R \end{align*}

Contradiction, since then \((m)\) isn't strict.
