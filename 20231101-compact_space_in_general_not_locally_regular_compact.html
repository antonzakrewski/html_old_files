compact space in general not locally regular compact

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a compact topological space. Then \(X\) is in general not locally compact regular Space

2. Proof

Let \(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}\) be the Alexandroff-compactification of the rationals with respect to the standard topology. Then \(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}\) is compact, but for \(0\) and \((-1,1) \subsesteq \mathbb{Q}\), there does'nt exist a compact neighbourhood, as the rationals are not complete (cf. Kompaktheit bzw. Vollständigkeit und Totalbeschränktheit)
