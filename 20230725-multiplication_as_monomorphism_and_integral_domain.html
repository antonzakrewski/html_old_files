multiplication as monomorphism and integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a commutative ring. TFAE:

  1. for all \(r \in R \setminus \{0\}\) the homomorphism \(\varphi_{r}: s \mapsto r \cdot s\) is a monomorphism
  2. \(R\) is an Integral domain

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(a \in R \setminus \{0\}, b \in R\) and \(\varphi_{a}(b) = 0\). then by the characterization of a minimal kernel and monomorphism we conclude, that \(b = 0\).

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Let \(r \in R \setminus \{0\}\), then for \(\varphi_{r}(b) = r \cdot b = 0\) we conclude that \(b = 0\) Therefore again a consequence of a the characterization of a minimal kernel and monomorphism
