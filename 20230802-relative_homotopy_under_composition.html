relative homotopy under composition

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T}_X)\), \((Y, \mathcal{T}_Y)\) and \((Z, \mathcal{T}_Z)\) be topological spaces \(A \subseteq X\), \(B \subseteq Y\), \(f_0,f_1: X \rightarrow Y\) and \(g_0,g_1: Y \rightarrow Z\) continuous map such that

\begin{align*} f_0 \simeq& f_1 \mathrm{rel} A \\ g_0 \simeq& g_1 \mathrm{rel} B \end{align*}

and \(f_0[A] \subseteq B\) Then

\begin{align*} g_0 \circ f_0 \simeq g_1 \simeq f_1 \mathrm{rel} A \end{align*}

2. Proof

By assumption, there exist homotopies \(h: I \times X \rightarrow Y\) and \(h': I \times Y \rightarrow Z\) such that \(h(s,a) = f_0(a), h'(s,b) = g_0(b)\) for \(s \in [0,1]\) Then we define

\begin{align*} \overline{h}: I \times X \rightarrow& Z \\ s,x \mapsto& h'(s,h(s,x)) \end{align*}

the map is as a composition of continous maps itself continuous (see: Stetigkeit der Diagonalabbildung)

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} I \times X \arrow[]{r}{\Delta \times \mathrm{id}} I \times (I \times X) \arrow[]{r}{\mathrm{id} \times h} I \times Y \arrow[]{r}{h'} Z \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Furthermore

\begin{align*} \overline{h}(0,x) =& h'(0,h(0,x)) \\ =& h'(0,f_0(x)) \\ =& g_0(f_0(x)) \\ \overline{h}(1,x) =& h'(1,h(1,x)) \\ =& h'(1,f_1(x)) \\ =& g_1(f_1(x)) \end{align*}

Let \(a \in A\) and \(s \in [0,1]\) then since \(f(a) \in B\), we conclude

\begin{align*} \overline{h}(s,a) =& h'(s,h(s,a)) \\ =& h'(s,f_0(a)) \\ =& g_0(f_0(a)) \end{align*}
