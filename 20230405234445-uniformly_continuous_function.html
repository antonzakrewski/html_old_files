uniformly continuous function

1. Definition

Let \((X, \Phi), (Y,\Phi')\) be uniform spaces. A function \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) is uniformly continuous, if for every entourage \(V_y \in \Phi'\) there exists an entourage \(V_x \in \Phi\) such that for \((x,y) \in V_x\) it follows that:

\begin{align*} (f(x),f(y)) \in V_y \end{align*}
