vanishing ideal as radical ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(\mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) the affine space. Suppose \(T \subseteq \mathbb{A}_K^n\), then the vanishing ideal \(\mathcal{I}(T)\) is a radical ideal

2. Proof

Let \(f \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) such that \(f^n \in \mathcal{I}(T)\). If there exists an \(x \in T\) such that \(f(x) \neq 0\), then \(f(x) \cdot f(x) \cdot ... \cdot f(x) \neq 0\) or \(f^n \not\in \mathcal{I}(T)\), hence by contraposition we get

\begin{align*} \sqrt{\mathcal{I}(T)} = \mathcal{I}(T) \end{align*}
