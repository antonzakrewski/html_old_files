UFD and localization

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a unique factorization domain and \(S \subseteq R\) a multiplicatively closed subset with \(0 \not\in S\). Then \(S^{-1}R\) is also a unique factorization domain

2. Proof

Suppose \(\frac{r}{s} \in S^{-1}R\) with \(\frac{r}{s} \neq 0\).

2.1. Existence

Then by assumption, there exists a factorization \(r = \prod_{i = 1}^{n} r_i\). If there exists an \(s' \in S\) such that \(r_i \mid s\), then there also exists a \(c \in R\) s.t. \(\frac{r_i}{1} \cdot \frac{c}{s'} = \frac{s'}{s'} = 0\) Thus \(r_i \in S^{-1}R^{\times}\). Otherwise, if \(\frac{r_i}{1} = \frac{\alpha}{s_1} \cdot \frac{\beta}{s_2}\), then we conclude

\begin{align*} r_i \cdot s_1 \cdot s_2 =& \alpha \cdot \beta \end{align*}

By unique factorization in \(R\), it follows that w.l.o.g. \(r_i \mid \alpha\), because \(r_i \in R\) is irreducible.
