cofiltered category

1. Definition

A category \(\mathcal{C}\) is cofiltered, if the opposite category is a filtered category

1.1. Explizit

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category. Then \(\mathcal{C}\) is said to be a cofiltered category, if for any two objects \(j_1,j_2\)

  1. and parallel morphisms \(f,g: j_1 \rightarrow j_2\) there exists a fork
  2. there exists an object \(k\) and morphisms \(k \rightarrow j_1, k \rightarrow j_2\)
  3. \(\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) is nonempty
