kernel as subset and image of an intersection

1. Proposition

Let \(G,H\) be groups, \(\varphi: G \rightarrow H\) a homomorphism and \(G_i \subseteq G\) subgroups for \(i \in I\) such that \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \subseteq G_i\). Then

\begin{align*} \varphi[\bigcap_{i \in I} G_i] = \bigcap_{i \in I} \varphi[G_i] \end{align*}

Let \(g_i \in G_i, g_j \in G_j\) such that \(\varphi(g_i) = \varphi(g_j)\) Then \(\phi(g_i \cdot g_j^{-1}) = \varphi(g_i) \cdot \varphi(g_j)^{-1} = e\) and therefore \(g_i \cdot g_j^{-1} \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\). Hence since \(G_j\) is closed under multiplication, we get

\begin{align*} g_i \cdot g_j^{-1} \cdot g_j = g_i \in G_j \end{align*}
