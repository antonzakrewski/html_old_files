quotient ring of a jacobson ring as jacobson ring

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be a Jacobson ring and \(\mathfrak{a} \subseteq R\) an ideal. Then \(R/\mathfrak{a}\) is a Jacobson ring.

2. Proof

Let \(\mathfrak{p} \in R/\mathfrak{a}\) prime, then \(\mathfrak{q} \coloneqq \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}[\mathfrak{p}]\) is also prime (see: Preimage of a prime ideal as prime ideal). By assumption, we get

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{q} =& \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}} \mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

and hence

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} =& \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}[\pi_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}[\mathfrak{p}]] \\ =& \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}[\bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}} \mathfrak{m}] \\ \end{align*}

Furthermore, we get

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{a} =& \mathrm{ker}(\pi_{\mathfrak{a}}) \\ =& \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}[ \{0 + \mathfrak{a}\}] \\ \subseteq& \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}[\mathfrak{p}] \\ \subseteq& \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}] \end{align*}

and thus by kernel as subset and image of an intersection also

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{p} =& \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}[\bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}} \mathfrak{m}] \\ &= \bigcap_{\mathfrak{p} \subseteq \mathfrak{m}} \pi_{\mathfrak{a}}[\mathfrak{m}] \\ \end{align*}

Furthermore, \(\pi_{\mathfrak{a}}[\mathfrak{m}]\) are maximal ideals (see: Image of an epimorphism and maximal ideal) and therefore \(R/\mathfrak{a}\) is a Jacobson ring
