open set as irreducible sets in an irreducible space

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be an irreducible topological space and \(O \subseteq X\) nonempty and open. Then \(O\) is also irreducible.

2. Proof

Proof by contraposition: Suppose there exists closed sets \(A_1,A_2 \neq X\) such that \(O \subseteq A_1 \cup A_2\). Then \(A_1 \cup A_2\) is closed (cf. Abgeschlossenheit endlicher Vereinigung von abgeschlossenen Mengen). Thus we get either \(A_1 \cup A_2 = X\), or \(A_1 \cup A_2 \neq X\) and \(X = (X \setminus O) \cup (A_1 \cup A_2)\).
