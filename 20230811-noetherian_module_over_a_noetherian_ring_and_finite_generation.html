noetherian module over a noetherian ring and finite generation

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a noetherian ring, \(M\) an \(A\)-module. TFAE:

  1. \(M\) is a noetherian module
  2. \(M\) is a finitely generated module

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

corollary of:

  • noetherian module as finitely generated module

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

By assumption, there exists an epimorphism \(\varphi: R^n \rightarrow M\). Suppose there exists an ascending chain of submodules $M1 \subseteq M2 \subseteq …$ of \(M\). Then we get an ascending chain $ϕ-1[M1] \subseteq ϕ-1[M2] \subseteq …$ (see: preimage of a submodule as submodule). Furthermore, since \(R^n\) is noetherian (cf. finite products of noetherian modules are noetherian module) it stabilizes and hence \(M_i = \varphi[\varphi^{-1}[M_i]]\) also stabilizes. Thus \(M\) is noetherian
