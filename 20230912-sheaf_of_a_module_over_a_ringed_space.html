sheaf of a module over a ringed space

1. Definition

Let \((X,\mathcal{O}_X)\) be a ringed spaced. A sheaf of a module over \(\mathcal{O}_X\) is a sheaf \(\mathcal{F}\), where \(\mathcal{F}(U)\) is a module over the ring \(\mathcal{O}_X(U)\) such that the modulestructure is compatible with \(\mathcal{O}_X\), i.e.: For the restriction \(\rho_{U,O}: \mathcal{F}(U) \rightarrow \mathcal{F}(O)\) for \(O \subseteq U \subseteq X\) open and \(r \in \mathcal{O}_X(U), m \in \mathcal{F}(U)\) it holds:

\begin{align*} \rho_{U,O}(r \cdot m) =& \rho_{\mathcal{O}}(r) \cdot \rho_{U,O}(m) \end{align*}

where \(\rho_{\mathcal{O}}: \mathcal{O}_X(U) \rightarrow \mathcal{O}_X(O)\) is the restriction of the ringed space \((X, \mathcal{O}_X)\)
