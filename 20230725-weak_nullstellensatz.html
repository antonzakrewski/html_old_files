1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be an algebraically closed field and \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq K[X_1,...,X_n]\) a maximal ideal in the polynomial ring Then there exists \(a = (a_1,...,a_n) \in \mathbb{A}_{K}^n\) such that

\begin{align*} \mathfrak{m} = (X_1 - a_1,...,X_n - a_n) \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. a)

2.1.1. \(n=1\)

Let \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq K[X]\) be a maximal ideal. Then, since \(K[X]\) is a principal ideal domain, there exists an \(f \in K[X]\) such that \(\mathfrak{m} = (f)\). Since \(K\) is algebraically closed, it splits, hence \(f = \prod_{i=1}^n (x - \alpha_i)\). By assumption of maximality, we can assume, that

2.1.2. general

Let

\begin{align*} \pi_i: K[X_i] \rightarrowtail& K[X_1,...,X_n] \\ \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X_i^n \mapsto& \sum_{i=0}^{n} \alpha_i X_i^n \end{align*}

then we conclude, that

\begin{align*} \pi_i^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}] = (x - \alpha_i) \end{align*}
