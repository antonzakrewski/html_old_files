noetherian ring and stabilizing of an ascending chain

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring TFAE:

  1. \(A\) is noetherian, as in each ideal is finitely generated
  2. every chain of ideals \(\mathcal{a}_1 \subseteq \mathcal{a}_2 ...\) stabilizes

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Suppose $\mathfrak{a}1 \subseteq \mathfrak{a}2 …$ is a chain. Then the union of a chain of ideals is a finitely generated ideal. Thus taking the maximum \(\mathfrak{a}_i\) of the finitely many generators \(b_1,...,b_n\) is an upper bond of \(\bigcup \mathfrak{a}_i\) and hence \(\mathfrak{a}_i\) stabilizes after \(\mathfrak{a}_{\mathrm{max}}\).

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Suppose \(\mathfrak{a}\) is an ideal and not finitely generated.

2.2.1. IA

Let \(0 = \langle 0\rangle\) be the zero ideal.

2.2.2. IS

Let \(\mathfrak{a}_i = \langle b_1,...,b_i\rangle \subseteq \mathfrak{a}\) be finitely generated, then either \(\mathfrak{a}_i = \mathfrak{a}\) or \(\mathfrak{a}_i \subsetneq \mathfrak{a}\) and thus there exists an element \(b_{i+1} \in \mathfrak{a} \setminus \mathfrak{a}_i\). Therefore, \(\langle b_1,...,b_i,b_{i+1}\rangle \subseteq \mathfrak{a}\)

Thus we can construct a chain of ideals, which by assumption, stabilizes. Hence \(\mathfrak{a}_{\mathrm{stab}} = \mathfrak{a}\) where \(\mathfrak{a}_{\mathrm{stab}}\) is finitely generated
