valuation ring

1. Definition / Proposition

Let \(F\) be a field, \(G\) a totally ordered group and \(\nu: F \rightarrow G \sqcup \{\infty\}\) a valuation. Then the valuation ring \(R_{\nu}\) is defined as subring

\begin{align*} R_{\nu} \coloneqq \{\alpha \in F \vert \nu(\alpha) \geq 0\} \end{align*}

2. Proof

2.1. multiplicative neutral element

Let \(b \in F\), then

\begin{align*} \nu(b) =& \nu(1 \cdot b) \\ =& \nu(1) + \nu(b) \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} \nu(1) = 0 \end{align*}

2.2. additive neutral element

follows from

\begin{align*} \nu(0) = \infty \end{align*}

2.3. additive inverse element

By assumption

\begin{align*} \nu(1) =& \nu(-1 \cdot -1) \\ \nu(-1) + \nu(-1) \\ =& 0 \end{align*}

Hence \(\nu(-1) = 0\).

Thus \(\alpha \in R_{\nu}\), then

\begin{align*} \nu(- \alpha) =& \nu(-1 \cdot \alpha) \\ =& \nu(-1) + \nu(\alpha) \\ =& 0 + \nu(\alpha) \\ \geq& \nu(\alpha) \end{align*}

2.4. multiplicative closed

Let \(\alpha, \beta \in R_{\nu}\), then by assumption

\begin{align*} \nu(\alpha \cdot \beta) = \nu(\alpha) + \nu(\beta) \geq 0 \end{align*}

2.5. additive closed

Let \(\alpha, \beta \in R_{\nu}\), then by assumption

\begin{align*} \nu(\alpha + \beta) \geq \mathrm{min}(\nu(\alpha), \nu(\beta)) \geq 0 \end{align*}
