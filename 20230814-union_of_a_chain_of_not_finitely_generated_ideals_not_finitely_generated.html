union of a chain of not finitely generated ideals not finitely generated

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and $\mathfrak{a}1 \subseteq \mathfrak{a}2 …$ a chain of ideals, such that $\mathfrak{a}1,….$ are not finitely generated ideals. Then \(\mathfrak{m} \coloneqq \bigcup_{i \in \mathbb{N}} \mathfrak{a}_i\) is also not finitely generated

2. Proof

\(\mathfrak{m}\) is a welldefined ideal, since Vereinigung von einer Kette von Idealen als Ideal.

Suppose \(\mathfrak{m}\) is finitely generated, then there exist \(a_1,...,a_n \in \mathfrak{m}\) such that \((a_1,...,a_n) = \mathfrak{m}\). Furthermore, there exists an \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(a_1,...,a_n \in \mathfrak{a}_m\). Hence \(\mathfrak{m} = (a_1,...,a_n) \subseteq \mathfrak{a}_m \subseteq \mathfrak{m}\), contradiction to

Therefore \(\mathfrak{m}\) is not finitely generated.
