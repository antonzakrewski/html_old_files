linearization of a map between groups

1. Proposition

Let \(G,H\) be groups and \(\phi: G \rightarrow H\) a map. Suppose \(S = \{ \left(\varphi(h)^{-1} \cdot \varphi(g)^{-1} \right) \cdot \varphi(gh)\}\) and \(\mathrm{ncl}(S)\) the normal closure. Then there exist a homomorphism \(\varphi: G \rightarrow G/\mathrm{ncl}(S)\)

2. Proof

2.1. welldefined

because of the normal closure

2.2. linear

Let \(g,h \in G\), then

\begin{align*} \varphi(gh) \cdot \left(\varphi(h)^{-1} \cdot \varphi(g)^{-1} \varphi(gh)\right)^{-1} =& \varphi(gh) \cdot \left(\varphi(gh)^{-1} \right)^{-1} \cdot \left(\varphi(g)^{-1} \right)^{-1} \left(\varphi(h)^{-1}\right)^{-1} \\ =& \varphi(g) \varphi(h) \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{align*} \varphi(gh) \mathrm{ncl}(S) =& \varphi(g)\varphi(h) \mathrm{ncl}(S) \end{align*}
