closed, nonempty irreducible set is generated by a prime ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring and \(\mathrm{Spec}(A)\) the Zariski spectrum equipped with the Zariski topology. Suppose \(B \subseteq \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) is irreducible, closed and nonempty, then there exists an prime ideal \(\mathfrak{p} \in \mathrm{Spec}(A)\) such that

\begin{align*} V(\mathfrak{p}) = B \end{align*}

2. Proof

Since \(B\) is closed, it is by definition generated by an ideal \(\mathfrak{a}\) Because \(V(\mathfrak{a}) = V(\sqrt{\mathfrak{a}})\) (see: vanishing set and radical), we may assume w.l.o.g. that \(\mathfrak{a}\) is a radical ideal.

Proof by (two) contradictions

Let \(\mathfrak{a}\) not be a prime ideal, then there exist \(a_1,a_2 \in A \setminus \mathfrak{a}\) such that \(a_1 \cdot a_2 \in \mathfrak{a}\). Then we get \(\mathfrak{a} = (\mathfrak{a}, a_1) \cdot (\mathfrak{a}, a_2)\) with \((\mathfrak{a},a_1), (\mathfrak{a}, a_2) \supsetneq \mathfrak{a}\) Furthermore

\begin{align*} V(\mathfrak{a}) = V(\mathfrak{a}, a_1) \cup V(\mathfrak{a}, a_2) \end{align*}

(see: vanishing set of a product of ideals) Suppose \(a_1 \in (\mathfrak{a}, a_2)\), then there exists an \(u \in R\) such that \(u \cdot a_2 + \mathfrak{a} = a_1 + \mathfrak{a}\). Thus we get

\begin{align*} (u \cdot a_2 + \mathfrak{a}) \cdot (a_1 + \mathfrak{a}) =& u a_2 a_1 + \mathfrak{a} \\ =& \mathfrak{a} \end{align*}

contradiction to radical ideal and reduced quotient ring

Since \(a_2 \not\in (\mathfrak{a},a_1)\), we conclude that \((\mathfrak{a}, a_1)\) is a strict ideal. Hence \(V((\mathfrak{a}, a_1)) \neq \emptyset\), and analoge (see: maximal ideal as prime ideal).

At last we get

\begin{align*} (\mathfrak{a}, a_1) \neq& \mathfrak{a} \\ V((\mathfrak{a}, a_1)) \neq& V(\mathfrak{a}) \end{align*}

as a corollary of a Vanishing set as Injection

Hence \(V(\mathfrak{a})\) is reducible with

\begin{align*} V(\mathfrak{a}) = V((\mathfrak{a}, a_1)) \cup V((\mathfrak{a}, a_2)) \end{align*}
