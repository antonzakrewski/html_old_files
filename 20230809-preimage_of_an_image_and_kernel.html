preimage of an image and kernel

1. Proposition

Let \(G,G'\) be groups, \(\varphi: G \rightarrow G'\) a homomorphism and \(S \subseteq G\) a subgroup TFAE:

  1. \(\mathrm{ker}(\varphi) \subseteq S\)
  2. \(S = \varphi^{-1}[\varphi[S]]\)

2. Proof

2.1. 1) \(\implies\) 2)

Let \(s \in S, a \in G\) such that \(\varphi(s) = \varphi(a)\). Then

\begin{align*} \varphi(a \cdot s^{-1}) = \varphi(a) \cdot \varphi(s)^{-1} \\ =& \varphi(s) \cdot \varphi(s)^{-1} \\ =& e \end{align*}

or \(a\cdot s^{-1} \in \mathrm{ker}(\varphi)\). Since \(S\) is closed under multiplication, we get

\begin{align*} a \cdot s^{-1} \cdot s = a \in S \end{align*}

Thus

\begin{align*} \varphi^{-1}[\varphi[S]] = S \end{align*}

2.2. 2) \(\implies\) 1)

Since \(S\) is a group, we conclude that \(e \in S\) and thus \(\varphi[e] = e\) or

\begin{align*} \mathrm{ker}(\varphi) =& \varphi^{-1}[\varphi[e]] \\ \subseteq& \varphi^{-1}[\varphi[S]] \\ =& S \end{align*}
