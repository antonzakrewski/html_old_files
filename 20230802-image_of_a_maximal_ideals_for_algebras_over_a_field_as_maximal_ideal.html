preimage of a maximal ideals for algebras over a field as maximal ideal

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field, \(f: A \rightarrow B\) a map of K-Algebras \(A,B\), where \(B\) is a finitely generated K-Algebra, and \(\mathfrak{m} \subseteq B\) a maximal ideal. Then

\begin{align*} f^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}] \end{align*}

is also a maximal ideal

2. Proof

Let

\begin{align*} \pi: A/f^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}] \rightarrow B/\mathfrak{m} \end{align*}

the canonical map. By Zariski's Lemma, \(B/\mathfrak{m}\) is a finite dimensional vector space, <A> It is an ringmonomorphism into the field \(B/\mathfrak{m}\) Hence \(A/f^{-1}[\mathfrak{m}]\) is an Integral domain (see: monomorphism and integral domain). Thus the rest is a corollary of finite dimensional integral domain as field
