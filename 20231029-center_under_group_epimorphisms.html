center under group epimorphisms

1. Proposition

Let \(G,H\) be groups, \(\varphi: G \twoheadrightarrow H\) a algebraic group-epimorphism. Then for the center \(Z(G) \subseteq G\), we conclude that

\begin{align*} \varphi[Z(G)] \subseteq Z(H) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Let \(h \in H\) and \(z \in Z(G)\) Then by assumption, there exists an \(g \in G\) such that \(\varphi(g) = h\). Therefore

\begin{align*} \varphi(z) \cdot h =& \varphi(z) \cdot \varphi(g) \\ =& \varphi(z \cdot g) \\ =& \varphi(g \cdot z) \\ =& \varphi(g) \cdot \varphi(z) \\ =& h \cdot \varphi(z) \end{align*}
