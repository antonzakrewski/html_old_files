localization along an element isomorphic to a quotient of the polynomial ring

1. Proposition

Let \(A\) be a commutative ring, \(\alpha \in A\) an element and \(A_{\alpha}\) the localization along \(\alpha\). Then

\begin{align*} A_{\alpha} \simeq A[y]/(1 - \alpha \cdot y) \end{align*}

2. Proof

Consider the canonical inclusion and canonical projection

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} A \arrow[]{r}{\iota} & A[y] \arrow[]{r}{\pi}& A[y]/(1 - \alpha \cdot y) \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

Then \((\pi \circ \iota)(\alpha) = \alpha + (1 - \alpha \cdot y)\) is a unit in \(A[y]/(1 - \alpha \cdot y)\)
