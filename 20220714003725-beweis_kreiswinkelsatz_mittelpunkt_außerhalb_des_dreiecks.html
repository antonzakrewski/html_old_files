1. Beweis

o.B.d.A. für \(\angle ACB\) bzw. \(\angle CBA\)

\begin{tikzpicture} \draw[] (0,0) circle (1cm); \coordinate (a) at (10:1cm); \coordinate (b) at (70:1cm); \coordinate (c) at (280:1cm); \coordinate (m) at (0,0); \coordinate (d) at (100:1cm); \node[right] (A) at (a) {A}; \node[above] (B) at (b) {B}; \node[left] (C) at (c) {C}; \node[above] (D) at (d) {D}; \draw (a) -- (b) -- (c) -- cycle; \node[below] (M) at (m) {M}; \foreach \n in {a,b,c,i,d} \node at (\n)[circle, fill, inner sep=1pt]{}; \draw (a) -- (m); \draw (b) -- (m); \draw (c) -- (m); \draw[dashed] (c) -- (d); \end{tikzpicture}

aufgrund des ersten Falles gilt:

\begin{align*} m\angle BMD =& 2\cdot m\angle BCD \\ m\angle AMD =& 2 \cdot m\angle ACD \\ m\angle AMB + m\angle BMD =& 2 \cdot (m\angle ACB + m\angle BCD) \\ \Rightarrow m\angle AMB =& 2 \cdot m\angle ACB \end{align*}
