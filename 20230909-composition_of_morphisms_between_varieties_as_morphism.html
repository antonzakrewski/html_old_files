morphism of varieties as morphism

1. Proposition

A morphism of varieties is a welldefined morphism

2. Proof

2.1. Associativity

  • associativity of function composition

2.2. composition

Let \(X,Y,Z\) be varieties over a field \(K\) and \(\varphi: X \rightarrow Y\) and \(\psi: Y \rightarrow Z\) be morphism of varieties. Then for a regular function \(f: O \rightarrow K\) for \(O \subseteq Y\) open, we conclude, that \(f \circ \psi: \psi^{-1}[O] \rightarrow K\) is regular and furthermore

\begin{align*} f \circ \psi \circ \varphi: \varphi^{-1}[\psi^{-1}[O]] \rightarrow K \end{align*}

2.3. identity

Let \(f\) be a regular function, then \(f = f \circ \mathrm{id}_{X}\) is also regular
