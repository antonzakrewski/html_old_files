finite categorical product

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be a category and \(A,B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) objects. A product of \(A\) and \(B\) is a triple:

\begin{align*} (A \times B, \pi_1: A \times B \rightarrow A, \pi_2: A \times B \rightarrow B) \end{align*}

where \(A \times B \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) and \(\pi_1, \pi_2 \in \mathrm{Mor}(\mathcal{C})\) such that for any object \(X \in \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) and morphisms \(f: X \rightarrow A\) and \(g: X \rightarrow B\), there exists a unique maq

\begin{align*} \theta: X \rightarrow A \times B \end{align*}

such that the diagram commutes <diagram>
