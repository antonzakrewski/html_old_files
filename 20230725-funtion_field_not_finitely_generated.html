function field not finitely generated as Algebra

1. Proposition

Let \(K\) be a field and \(L \coloneqq K(X_1,...,X_n)\) be the function field. Then \(L\) is not finitely generated as a K-Algebra over \(K\)

2. Proof

Let \(\{y_1,...,y_m \} \subseteq L\) be a finite subset with \(y_i = f_i/g_i\) for \(f_i,g_i \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) Let \(p \in K[X_1,...,X_n]\) an irreducible element, coprime to \(\prod_{i=1}^m g_i\) (see: unendlich viele normierte, irreduzible Polynome über einem Körper) (existence of coprime ?) Then \(1/p \in L\) but \(\frac{1}{p} \not\in K[y_1,...,y_m]\): Suppose the opposite, then

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{p} \sum_{=}^{} \alpha_I (y_1 \cdot ... \cdot y_m)^I \\ =& f/g \in L = K[X_1,...,X_n] \end{align*}

Hence \(K[y_1,...,y_m] \subsetneq L\) is a strict inclusion for any finite subset \(\{y_1,...,y_m\}\)
