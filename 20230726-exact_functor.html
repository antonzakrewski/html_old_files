covariant exact functor

1. Definition

Let \(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{B}\) be abelian categories and \(F: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}\) a covariant additive functor. Then \(F\) is said to be a covariant left exact functor, if for any short exact sequence

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{r}{g} & C \arrow[]{r}{} & 0 \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

it also follows, that

\begin{displaymath} \begin{tikzcd} 0 \arrow[]{r}{} & A \arrow[]{r}{f} & B \arrow[]{r}{g} & C \end{tikzcd} \end{displaymath}

is exact

2. Alternative

a covariant exact functor is a covariant additive functor between abelian category, that is both left exact functor and right exact functor
