closed sets in a simple final topology

1. Proposition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space, \(Y\) a set and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a set-theoretic map. Then a subset \(A \subseteq Y\) is closed if and only if \(f^{-1}[A]\) is closed

2. Proof

Let \(f^{-1}[A]\) be closed. Then \(X \setminus f^{-1}[A]\) is open and by definition of a final topology, \(f[X \setminus f^{-1}[A]\) is open. By image of a preimage and complement, we conclude that

\begin{align*} f[X] \setminus f(f^{-1}[A]) =& f[X] \setminus A \end{align*}

is open. Furthermore, \(f^{-1}[Y \setminus f[X]] = \emptyset\), hence \(Y \setminus f[X]\) is open. Therefore,

\begin{align*} (Y \setminus f[X]) \cup (f[X] \setminus A) = Y \setminus A \end{align*}

is
