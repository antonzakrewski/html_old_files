generic point under continuous maps

1. Proposition

Let \((X,\mathcal{T})\) and \((Y,\mathcal{T}_Y)\) be topological spaces, \(x \in X\) a generic point and \(f: X \rightarrow Y\) a continuous map. Then \(f(x) \in Y\) is a generic point of the image \(f[X]\)

2. Proof

corollary of Bild des Abschlusses unter stetigen Abbildungen, hence

\begin{align*} f[X] =& f[\overline{\{x\}}] \\ \subseteq \overline{f[\{y\}]} \\ \end{align*}

and inheritance of a generic point
