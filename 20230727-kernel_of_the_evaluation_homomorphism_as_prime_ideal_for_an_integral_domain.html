kernel of the evaluation homomorphism as prime ideal for an integral domain

1. Proposition

Let \(R\) be an Integral domain, \(R[X_i \vert i \in I]\) the polynomial ring and \(\alpha_i \in R\) for \(i \in I\). Then the kernel \(\mathrm{ker}(f_\alpha)\) of the evaluation homomorphism

\begin{align*} f_{\alpha}: R[X_i] \rightarrow& R \\ f \mapsto& f((\alpha_i)) \end{align*}

is a prime ideal

2. Proof

\(f_{\alpha}\) is surjective. Applying the Homomorphiesatz für Ringe results in

\begin{align*} K[X_i] / \mathrm{ker}(f_\alpha) \simeq K \end{align*}

since \(K\) is an Integral domain, we conclude, that \(\mathrm{ker}(f_\alpha)\) is prime (see: Primideal in kommutativen Ringen und Faktorring als Integritätsbereich)
