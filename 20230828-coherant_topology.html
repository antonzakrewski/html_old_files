coherent topology

1. Definition

Let \((X, \mathcal{T})\) be a topological space and \(\mathcal{C} = (C_i)\) a family of subsets equipped with the subspace topology Then \(X\) is said to be coherent with \(\mathcal{C}\), if \((X, \mathcal{T})\) is the final topology for the inclusion map \(\iota_i: C_i \rightarrowtail X\)
